## 1. Brand Foundation

### Mission

Make creator analytics practical for everyday use so that any TikTok creator -- from 1,000 followers to 1 million -- can estimate likely outcomes in minutes instead of guessing in the dark.

### Value Propositions

1. **You will stop pricing brand deals by gut feeling.** TT Calculator gives you follower-tier benchmarks, engagement context, and niche multipliers so you walk into negotiations with a defensible number.
2. **You will understand where your money actually comes from.** The tools model every TikTok income stream side-by-side (Creator Rewards, brand deals, LIVE gifts, Shop, ads) so you can see which lever to pull next.
3. **You will trust the estimates because the assumptions are visible.** Every calculator exposes its inputs, formula logic, and known limitations. Nothing is a black box.
4. **You will make faster decisions without a data team.** One page, one question, one result, one next step. No dashboard onboarding, no login wall, no paywall.
5. **You will know when a number is too uncertain to act on.** TT Calculator widens ranges and flags volatility instead of overstating precision. That saves you from bad bets.

## 2. Brand Personality

### Archetype: The Sage with Caregiver traits

The Sage because TT Calculator's core identity is built on clarity, expertise, and transparency -- the brand exists to make complex creator economics understandable. The Caregiver secondary because the brand genuinely protects users from bad decisions: it flags uncertainty, widens ranges, and refuses to overstate precision. It does not try to impress; it tries to be useful.

### 3-Word Identity: Clear, Honest, Practical

- **Clear** -- Every page follows inputs-result-meaning-next-step. No jargon without explanation. No ambiguity in units.
- **Honest** -- Estimates are labeled as estimates. Assumptions are visible. Limitations are documented. The brand would rather lose a click than mislead.
- **Practical** -- Content is designed to be used before a negotiation, during a planning session, or while reviewing monthly performance. Not theory; applied.

### Anti-Identity: We are never hype-driven, condescending, or vague

- **Never hype-driven.** We do not use phrases like "get rich on TikTok," "secret formula," "guaranteed income," or "this one trick." We present ranges and context.
- **Never condescending.** We explain simply but we do not talk down. A new creator reading about RPM for the first time should feel informed, not patronized.
- **Never vague.** We do not publish pages that circle a topic without landing on a specific, useful conclusion. Every page answers one question with visible reasoning.

### Personality in Practice

| Situation | How We Respond |
|-----------|---------------|
| Creator confused about RPM | We define the term in one sentence, show how to calculate it, explain what range is normal for their niche, and link to the RPM calculator. No filler. |
| Competitor calculator mentioned | We do not name competitors. We differentiate on methodology transparency and depth. If asked, we explain what makes our approach different without attacking others. |
| Complex topic (e.g., tax implications) | We break it into numbered steps, use concrete dollar examples, flag where professional advice is needed, and add a disclaimer. We simplify without dumbing down. |
| Delivering bad news (e.g., Creator Fund pays very little) | We state the reality directly with data: "Most creators earn $0.02-$0.04 per 1,000 views from the Creator Fund." We then point to higher-value alternatives rather than dwelling on the negative. |
| Celebrating a win (e.g., new calculator launched) | Understated. "We added a new TikTok Shop commission calculator. Here's what it does." No superlatives, no exclamation marks in headlines. The tool speaks for itself. |

## 3. Voice & Tone Guide

### Reading Level: Grade 8-10

Accessible to an 18-year-old creator with no analytics background. Technical terms (RPM, CPM, engagement rate, CTR) are used freely because the audience learns them quickly, but each term is defined on first use within a page. Sentence structure stays simple even when the subject is complex.

### Formality: 5/9

Conversational expert. Professional enough for a brand manager to share with a CMO, casual enough for a 20-year-old creator to read on their phone. The brand avoids both academic stiffness and social-media slang.

### Perspective: Second person ("you") dominant

- **Calculator pages:** "Enter your average views" / "Your estimated earnings"
- **Guides:** "You can improve your engagement rate by..."
- **Trust pages (methodology, editorial policy):** "We" + "our" for the brand, "you" for the reader
- **Third person is avoided** except in data/reference pages where neutrality is needed

### Power Words (use frequently)

estimate, benchmark, transparent, practical, directional, assumptions, range, niche-specific, compare, understand, realistic, methodology, next step, context, decision

### Forbidden Words (never use)

guaranteed, hack, guru, secret, easy money, get rich, viral trick, massive, insane, ultimate (in headlines), crushing it, game-changer, synergy, utilize, utilize, skyrocket, explode (used figuratively), unlock (used as marketing verb), exclusive (unless genuinely exclusive)

### Branded Terms

| Term | Definition | Usage Context |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| TT Calculator | The brand name. Always "TT Calculator" on first reference per page, "TT" acceptable in UI/shorthand after that. Never "TikTok Calculator" as the brand name (that is an SEO keyword, not the brand). | Everywhere |
| Directional estimate | A result that indicates likely range and direction, not exact payout. | Calculator output explanations, methodology pages |
| Assumption band | The range of inputs (e.g., RPM $0.20-$0.80) used to bracket a calculator's output. | Methodology blocks, "How we estimate" sections |
| Inputs-result-meaning-next step | The four-part page structure used across all calculator pages. | Internal briefs, editorial policy |
| Desk (Editorial Desk, Research Desk, etc.) | The internal team unit that owns a content category. | Author bylines, about page |

### Tone by Content Type

| Content Type | Tone Shift | Example Sentence |
|-------------|-----------|-----------------|
| Calculator page (pillar) | Authoritative but approachable. Lead with the tool, follow with context. | "Enter your average video views and follower count. The calculator estimates your monthly earnings across Creator Rewards, brand deals, LIVE gifts, and Shop commissions." |
| How-to guide | Instructional, encouraging, step-by-step. Slightly warmer than calculator pages. | "Start by checking your engagement rate against the benchmarks below. If you are above 4%, you are in a strong position to pitch brand deals." |
| Benchmark / data page | Neutral, precise, table-heavy. Let the data lead. | "Average TikTok engagement rates by follower tier (2026 data): Nano (1K-5K): 8-15%. Micro (5K-50K): 5-8%." |
| News / update | Direct, timely, factual. No editorializing unless analysis is clearly labeled. | "TikTok expanded Shop to three additional European markets in Q1 2026. Here is what changes for sellers and affiliates." |
| About / trust page | Warm but measured. First-person plural. Emphasizes values and process. | "We treat creator analytics as an applied discipline. That means we care about model structure, input clarity, and reproducibility more than catchy claims." |

### Humor Policy

Minimal. Occasional dry wit is acceptable in guides ("The Creator Fund will not make you rich. It might buy you lunch.") but never in calculator outputs, methodology, trust pages, or data pages. No sarcasm. No memes in editorial content. The brand earns trust through reliability, not entertainment.

### Sentence Style Examples

**On-brand:** "Most creators with 50K followers earn between $1,500 and $4,000 per month when they combine brand deals, LIVE gifts, and Creator Rewards."
**Off-brand:** "If you have 50K followers, you could be making THOUSANDS every single month -- here's the secret most creators don't know!"

**On-brand:** "This calculator provides a directional estimate. Actual earnings depend on niche, region, engagement quality, and current platform rates."
**Off-brand:** "Disclaimer: results may vary. This is just for fun and entertainment purposes only. We are not responsible for anything."

**On-brand:** "Your engagement rate is the single most important metric brands evaluate before offering a deal."
**Off-brand:** "Engagement rate is literally EVERYTHING. If yours is low, you're basically invisible to brands."

**On-brand:** "We do not claim privileged access to internal TikTok systems. Our methodology uses public and community-visible signals."
**Off-brand:** "Our proprietary AI-powered algorithm uses advanced data science to predict your exact TikTok earnings."

## 4. CTA Strategy

### CTA Map by Funnel Stage

| Stage | Primary CTA | CTA Copy Template | Placement |
|-------|------------|-------------------|-----------|
| Awareness (just discovered TT Calculator via search) | Use a calculator | "Estimate your [metric] with the free [Calculator Name]" / "Start with a number" | End of intro section, header button, inline within first 300 words |
| Consideration (exploring multiple calculators/guides) | Explore related tools or guides | "Compare your results with the [Related Calculator]" / "Read the full [Guide Name]" | After calculator output, related tools section, guide cross-links |
| Decision (ready to act on insights) | Apply the insight | "Use this estimate to [specific action: negotiate your rate / set your price / plan your month]" | Below calculator result, within guide conclusion |
| Retention (returning user) | Suggest next calculator / request a tool | "Need a calculator we haven't built yet? Tell us what creators need help calculating next." | Footer CTA, contact page |

### CTA Voice: Confident and specific, never aggressive

CTAs describe the action and its outcome. They avoid urgency triggers, false scarcity, and exclamation marks. The default tone is an invitation, not a command.

- Good: "Estimate your TikTok earnings" / "Start with a number" / "Send a request"
- Bad: "Calculate NOW!" / "Don't miss out!" / "Get your FREE results before it's too late!"

### CTA Frequency Rule

- **Calculator pages:** One primary CTA in the header (persistent in nav). One contextual CTA after the calculator output. One cross-link CTA in the related tools section. No mid-content pop-ups.
- **Guides:** One inline calculator CTA within the first 300 words. One contextual CTA per major section where a tool directly applies. One summary CTA at the end.
- **Data/benchmark pages:** One cross-link to the relevant calculator. No aggressive CTAs.
- **Trust pages:** No sales CTAs. Link to related policies and contact only.

### Social Proof Template

TT Calculator uses quantitative social proof, not testimonials (the brand does not currently collect individual testimonials):
- "35+ free TikTok calculators" (tool count)
- "Trusted by 100k+ creators" (when/if traffic supports this claim -- remove until validated)
- "Updated weekly" (freshness signal)
- "No signup required" (friction-removal signal)

Do NOT fabricate social proof numbers. If a claim cannot be verified, do not make it.

### Urgency/Scarcity Policy: None

TT Calculator does not use urgency or scarcity triggers. The tools are free and always available. Artificial urgency ("Limited time!") is incompatible with the brand's honesty value. The only time-sensitive language used is factual: "2026 data" or "Updated March 2026" to signal freshness, not to create FOMO.

## 5. Content Formatting Rules

### Emoji: None in editorial content

No emojis in article body text, headings, meta descriptions, or alt text. Emojis may appear only in:
- Social media posts (if the brand publishes them)
- User-facing UI micro-copy where a single icon replaces a word (e.g., a sparkles icon next to the CTA button -- handled as a Lucide icon, not a Unicode emoji)

### Bold/Italic Usage

- **Bold** for key terms on first definition, for the lead sentence of FAQ answers, and for critical numbers/ranges within paragraphs.
- *Italic* sparingly, only for emphasis on a single word or for publication titles. Never italic for entire sentences.
- Do not bold entire paragraphs. Do not use bold as a substitute for headings.

### Callout Boxes

Used for:
- **Methodology notes** ("How we estimate this") -- present on every calculator page
- **Important caveats** ("This model does not account for X")
- **Quick examples** ("A creator with 100K followers and 5% engagement might charge $3,000-$5,000 per post")

Not used for: promotional CTAs, "pro tips," or decorative pull quotes.

### Image Style Direction

- **Primary visual language:** Data visualization, clean charts, input/output UI patterns. The product IS the visual.
- **Color palette:** Warm neutral background (#f6efe6), coral-red primary (#ff6b57), mint secondary (#6dd3b4), deep navy text (#172033). Warm, approachable, not corporate.
- **Typography:** Manrope (body), Fraunces (display headings), IBM Plex Mono (data/code). This combination reads as "modern editorial product," not "startup SaaS."
- **Photography:** Not used. The brand does not use stock photos of smiling influencers. If images are needed, use screenshots of calculator outputs, data tables, or custom illustrations.
- **Logo:** Coral gradient square with "TT" in white, followed by "TT Calculator" wordmark. Simple, recognizable, tool-oriented.

## Phase 3: Voice Calibration Test

The following paragraph describes what a TikTok engagement rate is and why it matters, written in three voices:

### 1. Generic SEO voice (content mill)

"TikTok engagement rate is a key metric that measures how much your audience interacts with your content. It is calculated by dividing total engagements (likes, comments, shares) by your follower count and multiplying by 100. A good engagement rate on TikTok is between 4% and 6%. If your engagement rate is high, brands will want to work with you. If it is low, you should try posting better content. Many creators wonder what a good engagement rate is, and this guide will help you understand everything you need to know about TikTok engagement rates in 2026."

### 2. Brand voice applied (TT Calculator)

"Your TikTok engagement rate measures how actively your audience responds to your posts relative to your follower count. The formula is straightforward: (likes + comments + shares) / followers x 100. In 2026, most creators with 10K-50K followers see rates between 5% and 8%. Rates above 6% are strong enough to support brand deal pricing at a premium. If your rate is below 3%, it is worth investigating whether posting time, hook quality, or content-audience fit is the issue before adjusting your strategy. Use our engagement rate calculator to benchmark your rate against your niche."

### 3. Over-applied brand voice (exaggerated boundaries)

"Let us be extremely precise about this. Your TikTok engagement rate is calculated as follows: total engagements (specifically: likes, comments, and shares -- not saves, not profile views, not follows) divided by total follower count, multiplied by 100. Our assumption band for 'good' is 4.0%-6.0% with a 95% confidence interval based on aggregated public signals. We must caveat that this range is directional, not deterministic, and subject to niche variance, regional algorithm weighting, and seasonal advertiser demand fluctuation. We recommend against any financial commitment based solely on this estimate. Please review our full methodology documentation before interpreting this result."

**Verdict:** Version 2 is the target. Version 3 is too hedged and academic -- the caveats are real but they belong in methodology blocks, not inline in every paragraph. The brand should be transparent about limitations without making every sentence feel like a legal disclaimer.

"Engagement rate is literally EVERYTHING. If yours is low, you're basically invisible to brands."

- **Reading level:** Grade 8-10
- **Perspective:** "You" for the reader, "We" for the brand
