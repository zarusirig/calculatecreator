### Findings: Current Vocabulary Usage

**Strengths (terms already used well):**
- The content consistently uses "Creator Fund," "brand deals," "engagement rate," and core monetization terms
- Follower tier terminology (nano, micro, mid-tier, macro, mega) is used consistently across pages
- The coins guide effectively differentiates coins/diamonds/gifts — a genuine vocabulary achievement
- Niche-specific terminology is well-deployed in the engagement rate article

**Weaknesses (vocabulary gaps identified):**

1. **Over-reliance on "engagement rate" without synonym rotation.** The engagement rate article uses "engagement rate" 30+ times but rarely uses "interaction rate," "audience response," "engagement level," or "engagement score." This signals narrow vocabulary to search engines.

2. **"Brand deal" dominates with minimal synonym variation.** The brand deal rate card uses "brand deal" extensively but underuses "paid partnership," "creator partnership," "sponsored collaboration," "brand collaboration." The term "endorsement" never appears. "Campaign" appears but only from the brand perspective.

3. **Missing troponym diversity in action verbs.** The content defaults to "earn," "make money," and "get" (brand deals). Missing verb variations: "generate revenue," "monetize," "capitalize on," "profit from," "bank," "net," "take home," "command" (rates), "secure" (partnerships), "land" (deals), "close" (negotiations).

4. **"Algorithm" vocabulary is narrow.** When discussing the algorithm, content uses "algorithm" and "FYP" but rarely "recommendation system," "content distribution system," "discovery engine," or "ranking signals." This limits the lexical field.

5. **Conditional synonym opportunities missed:**
   - "Earnings and Eligibility" (could connect monetization to requirements pages)
   - "Growth and Revenue" (could connect follower growth to earnings)
   - "Performance and Profit" (could connect analytics to monetization)
   - "Reach and Revenue" (could connect distribution to earnings)

6. **"Calculator" / "estimate" / "project" are underused as semantic signals.** As a calculator site, these tool-context words should appear more prominently to differentiate from general advice sites. Phrases like "use our calculator to estimate," "project your earnings," "model your revenue" should appear across pages.

7. **Missing "Tok" community vocabulary.** The content rarely uses TikTok community terminology like "FinTok," "BookTok," "FoodTok," "CleanTok," which are high-search-volume terms that signal cultural fluency.

8. **Hypernym layer is thin.** Content jumps from specific terms to very generic ones. Missing middle-layer hypernyms: "platform payout system" (for Creator Fund), "virtual economy" (for coins/diamonds), "social commerce" (for TikTok Shop), "content performance metrics" (for engagement/watch time).

9. **Antonym/contrast vocabulary missing.** No pages discuss: "why content flops," "demonetization risks," "audience decline," "engagement drop," or "revenue loss" — all of which would expand the semantic field through opposition and serve real searcher intent.

10. **Co-hyponym comparison vocabulary is limited.** Cross-platform comparisons exist as separate pages but the vocabulary rarely bleeds into main content. Terms like "YouTube RPM comparison," "Instagram engagement benchmark," "Twitch gifting equivalent" should appear as contextual references within core pages.
