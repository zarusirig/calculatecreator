## 1. Audience Segments

TT Calculator's audience differs from the default Semantic SEO split because the site serves a creator-economy niche where most visitors are practitioners (creators) at varying experience levels, plus a distinct minority of brand-side professionals. The adjusted segmentation:

### Segment A: Aspiring Creator (Novice) -- 25% of audience

**Who they are:** Pre-monetization TikTok users with 0-5K followers who have heard you can make money on TikTok and want to know if it is realistic for them.

**Defining characteristics:**
- Little to no understanding of monetization mechanics (Creator Fund, RPM, brand deals)
- Search using broad, curiosity-driven queries: "how much money can you make on TikTok," "TikTok pay per view," "do TikTokers get paid"
- Need foundational definitions, eligibility requirements, and realistic expectations
- High bounce risk if content assumes prior knowledge of terms like RPM, CPM, or engagement rate
- Most likely to arrive via high-volume informational queries from Google or TikTok itself
- Primary emotional state: curious but skeptical

**What they need from TT Calculator:**
- Plain-language explanations of every monetization stream
- Realistic earnings ranges so they can decide whether to invest effort
- Clear eligibility checklists (follower thresholds, region requirements)
- One-click access to the simplest calculators (Money Calculator, Engagement Rate)

### Segment B: Growing Creator (Intermediate) -- 40% of audience

**Who they are:** Active TikTok creators with 5K-100K followers who are monetizing (or about to monetize) and want to optimize. This is the largest and most commercially valuable segment.

**Defining characteristics:**
- Understand basic TikTok mechanics but need help with strategy, pricing, and analytics
- Search using specific, actionable queries: "how to price TikTok brand deals," "TikTok engagement rate by niche," "increase TikTok RPM," "best times to go live"
- Comfortable with terms like engagement rate, follower tier, and Creator Fund but need context for RPM, CPM, conversion rate, and watch time metrics
- Engage with multiple pages per session -- explorers who want to compare metrics
- Primary emotional state: motivated but uncertain about next steps

**What they need from TT Calculator:**
- Calculators that model their specific situation (niche, follower count, engagement)
- Benchmark data to compare themselves against peers
- Step-by-step guides for actionable decisions (pricing brand deals, optimizing LIVE earnings)
- Niche-specific guidance (beauty earnings vs. finance earnings vs. gaming)
- Content that bridges "I know what engagement rate is" to "I know how to use mine in a negotiation"

### Segment C: Established Creator (Advanced) -- 20% of audience

**Who they are:** Full-time or serious part-time creators with 100K+ followers who treat TikTok as a business. They already earn money and want to maximize and diversify.

**Defining characteristics:**
- Strong understanding of platform mechanics and monetization
- Search using precise, technical queries: "TikTok RPM by country," "creator fund vs creator rewards comparison," "TikTok Shop commission structure," "content repurposing ROI"
- Need advanced strategy, tax/legal guidance, multi-platform analysis, and business operations content
- Evaluate site authority based on data quality, methodology transparency, and depth
- Lower volume but higher engagement per visit and higher likelihood of bookmarking/returning
- Primary emotional state: strategic and efficiency-focused

**What they need from TT Calculator:**
- Advanced calculators (Campaign ROI, Content Value, Multi-Platform Earnings, Production Cost)
- Data pages with granular benchmarks (RPM by niche, brand deal rates by follower count)
- Business guides (LLC formation, taxes, contract negotiation, hiring a manager)
- Platform comparison data for multi-platform strategy decisions
- Methodology transparency to trust the estimates enough to use them in real business decisions

### Segment D: Brand/Agency Professional -- 15% of audience

**Who they are:** Marketing managers, talent managers, agency strategists, and brand partnership leads who evaluate TikTok creators for campaigns or manage creator talent.

**Defining characteristics:**
- Professional-level knowledge of influencer marketing metrics but may be less familiar with TikTok-specific mechanics (vs. Instagram or YouTube)
- Search using evaluation-oriented queries: "TikTok influencer pricing," "average TikTok engagement rate by follower count," "TikTok sponsorship ROI calculator," "TikTok CPM rates"
- Need benchmark data to evaluate creators, set campaign budgets, and justify spend to stakeholders
- Will immediately detect superficiality -- they compare against paid tools (HypeAuditor, CreatorIQ)
- Highest potential for linking to TT Calculator content in reports, pitch decks, and internal docs
- Primary emotional state: evaluative and ROI-focused

**What they need from TT Calculator:**
- Brand-side calculators (Campaign ROI, CPM/CPV, Ad Cost, Sponsorship ROI, Customer Acquisition Cost)
- Benchmark data tables they can reference in reports
- Comparison pages (TikTok vs Instagram rates, TikTok vs YouTube earnings)
- Industry data (creator economy size, advertising rate benchmarks, GMV growth)
- Content framed from the buyer/evaluator perspective, not just the creator perspective

## 2. Sophistication Levels

Each segment maps to a primary sophistication level, but visitors can operate at different levels depending on the sub-topic.

| Level | Vocabulary Comfort | Example Query | Content Type Expected | Segment Overlap |
|-------|-------------------|---------------|----------------------|-----------------|
| **Beginner** | Knows "followers" and "views" but not "RPM," "engagement rate formula," or "CPM" | "how much does TikTok pay" | Definitions, overviews, eligibility checklists, simple calculators | Segment A (primary), Segment D on TikTok-specific topics |
| **Intermediate** | Comfortable with engagement rate, brand deals, Creator Fund, follower tiers | "how to price TikTok sponsorships by engagement rate" | How-to guides, benchmark comparisons, niche-specific strategies, mid-tier calculators | Segment B (primary), Segment A when advancing |
| **Advanced** | Fluent in RPM, CPM, conversion funnels, multi-platform attribution, tax implications | "TikTok RPM by country 2026 data" | Data tables, advanced calculators, business strategy, platform comparisons | Segment C (primary), Segment D |
| **Expert** | Professional fluency in influencer marketing metrics, campaign measurement, ROI modeling | "TikTok campaign ROI calculator cost per acquisition" | ROI calculators, industry data, methodology pages, campaign planning tools | Segment D (primary), Segment C on business topics |

**Brand voice calibration by level:**
- **Beginner:** Warm, encouraging, jargon-free. Define every term on first use. "Here is what engagement rate means and why it matters for your earnings."
- **Intermediate:** Instructional, specific, data-backed. Terms used freely with context. "Your engagement rate of 6% puts you in the top quartile for beauty creators."
- **Advanced:** Precise, efficient, assumption-forward. No basic definitions. "RPM varies from $0.02 to $0.08 depending on niche, region, and content format."
- **Expert:** Neutral, data-dense, methodology-transparent. "Campaign ROI is modeled as (revenue attributed - total campaign cost) / total campaign cost, using last-touch attribution."

**Recommended rebalancing:** Produce more informational content targeting Segment A and B Problem Awareness queries. Every 3 new informational pages should map forward to an existing calculator or comparison page via internal linking.

## 7. Gap Analysis

### Gap 1: Segment A Problem Awareness Content is Thin (HIGH PRIORITY)

**The issue:** The site has extensive calculators and optimization guides but relatively few entry-level "what is" and "how does" pages that capture the highest-volume informational queries. Novice creators searching "how does TikTok pay" or "what is TikTok Creator Fund" need simple, definitional content before they are ready for a calculator.

**Current coverage:** A few guides cover this ("How to Make Money on TikTok," "TikTok Monetization Requirements") but the ratio is skewed toward intermediate/advanced content.

**Recommended additions:**
- "What Is TikTok Creator Rewards Program" (vs. the existing optimization-focused content)
- "How Does TikTok Pay Creators" (broad overview, not niche-specific)
- "TikTok Monetization for Beginners: Complete Guide"
- "Can You Really Make Money on TikTok? Realistic Expectations"
- Expanded glossary with individual term pages (RPM, CPM, engagement rate, watch time, etc.)

**Impact:** These pages capture high-volume queries, reduce bounce rate for novice visitors, and feed directly into calculator pages.

### Gap 2: Segment D (Brand/Agency) Content is Almost Entirely Creator-Framed (HIGH PRIORITY)

**The issue:** The site has strong brand-side calculators (Campaign ROI, CPM/CPV, Ad Cost, Sponsorship ROI, Customer Acquisition Cost) but nearly all editorial content is written from the creator's perspective. Brand professionals evaluating creators need content framed from the buyer's perspective.

**Current coverage:** Calculators exist but guides like "How to Evaluate TikTok Influencers" or "TikTok Influencer Marketing ROI Guide" do not.

**Recommended additions:**
- "How Brands Calculate TikTok Influencer ROI" (guide from the buyer's perspective)
- "TikTok Influencer Marketing Budget Guide 2026"
- "TikTok Creator Evaluation Checklist for Brands"
- "Average TikTok Campaign Performance Benchmarks" (data page)
- "TikTok vs Instagram for Influencer Marketing" (comparison from brand perspective)
- "How to Spot Fake TikTok Engagement" (trust/evaluation content)

**Impact:** This segment has the highest per-visit value, is most likely to generate backlinks, and is completely underserved by the current editorial layer.

### Gap 3: Solution Evaluation Cross-Linking is Weak (MEDIUM PRIORITY)

**The issue:** The comparison pages and data pages exist but are relatively isolated. They link to each other inconsistently and do not always link forward to the decision-stage content (calculators, action guides).

1. At least one relevant calculator

**The issue:** The news section exists but there is no email newsletter, no bookmark prompt, and no "what changed this month" summary page. Returning users have no clear reason to come back except habitual curiosity.

## 8. Conversion Path Planning

### Primary Conversion: Calculator Engagement (2+ minutes on a calculator page)

The site's primary conversion is not a purchase -- it is sustained engagement with a calculator tool. This drives ad revenue and establishes the site as a utility worth returning to.

**Optimal flow:**
1. Organic search lands on an informational page (guide, data page, or article)
2. Inline CTA within first 300 words directs to a relevant calculator
3. User engages with calculator (enters data, sees results)
4. Calculator result page recommends related calculators and guides
5. User explores 2-3 additional pages per session

**CTA strategy by page type:**
- **Guides:** "Estimate your [metric] with the free [Calculator Name]" -- inline after first major section
- **Data pages:** "Run your own numbers with our [Calculator Name]" -- after the first data table
- **Comparison pages:** "Model your own comparison with the [Calculator Name]" -- after the comparison table
- **Calculator pages:** "Compare your results with the [Related Calculator]" -- in the result output area
- **News pages:** "See how this affects your earnings -- re-run the [Calculator Name]" -- after the news analysis

### Secondary Conversion: Multi-Page Session (3+ pages per visit)

**Optimal flow:**
1. Any entry point links to at least 2 clear next steps
2. Calculator result pages always show "Related Calculators" and "Related Guides"
3. Data pages link to calculators and guides bidirectionally
4. No page is a dead end

### Future Conversion: Email Capture (not yet implemented)

When ready, the recommended placement for email capture:
- After calculator results: "Get monthly benchmark updates for your niche"
- On data pages: "This data is updated quarterly. Get notified."
- Not on trust pages, methodology, or the calculator input area

### Navigation Architecture Recommendations

The current header navigation (Tools, Benchmarks, Guides, News, About) serves segments B and C well but lacks direct paths for Segment A and Segment D.

**Recommended additions to site navigation:**

| Addition | Purpose | Serves Segment |
|----------|---------|---------------|
| "Get Started" or "New to TikTok?" link in header or homepage hero | Direct novice path | A |
| "For Brands" section or landing page | Direct professional path | D |
| "By Niche" filter or section in guides | Let intermediate users self-select | B |
| Sticky "Try a Calculator" banner on guide pages | Cross-funnel from informational to tool | A, B |

### Internal Linking Rules for Journey Path Support

1. **Every guide must link to at least one calculator** within the first 300 words
2. **Every calculator page must link to at least 2 related guides** in the RelatedGuides component
3. **Every data page must link to the calculator that uses that data** and the guide that explains it
4. **Every comparison page must link forward to a decision-stage action** (calculator, template, or action guide)
5. **News articles must link to the calculator or data page most affected** by the news
6. **Niche guides must cross-link to the niche profitability calculator and the relevant data pages**
7. **No page should have fewer than 3 internal links** in its body content (excluding nav and footer)
