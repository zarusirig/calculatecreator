## Calculator 1: Engagement Rate Calculator (/calculators/engagement-rate)
This page already has ~600 words. Enhance to 900+ words.

### Introduction (75 words)
Engagement rate is the most important metric for TikTok success. It measures how actively your audience interacts with your content through likes, comments, and shares. High engagement (5%+) signals to the algorithm that your content deserves wider distribution and makes you more valuable to brands.

### How We Calculate (175 words)
**Formula:** Engagement Rate = [(Likes + Comments + Shares) / Followers] × 100

**Alternative Formula (per video):** 
Engagement Rate = [(Likes + Comments + Shares) / Views] × 100

Explain when to use each formula:
- Follower-based: For brand deals and overall account health
- View-based: For analyzing individual video performance

**What We Include:**
- Likes: Primary engagement signal
- Comments: Indicates deeper audience investment
- Shares: Highest-value engagement (amplifies reach)

**What We Don't Include:**
- Views (used as denominator, not numerator)
- Profile visits (separate metric)
- Follows (separate conversion metric)

### Benchmarks (125 words)
| Rating | Rate | What It Means |
|--------|------|---------------|
| Excellent | 8%+ | Top 10% of creators, high brand value |
| Good | 5-8% | Above average, attractive for partnerships |
| Average | 3-5% | Typical for most creators |
| Below Average | 1-3% | Room for improvement |
| Concerning | <1% | May indicate bot followers or content mismatch |

**By Follower Count:**
- Under 10K: Expect 8-15% (smaller audiences engage more)
- 10K-100K: Expect 5-10%
- 100K-1M: Expect 3-7%
- 1M+: Expect 2-5%

### Why It Matters (150 words)
**🎯 Algorithm Performance**
TikTok's algorithm uses engagement as a primary signal for For You Page distribution. Videos with high engagement in the first 30-60 minutes get pushed to exponentially more viewers.

**💰 Brand Deal Value**
Brands prioritize engagement over follower count. A creator with 50K followers and 10% engagement is more valuable than one with 200K followers and 2% engagement. Use our [brand deal rate calculator](/calculators/brand-deal-rate) to see how engagement affects your rates.

**📈 Growth Indicator**
Consistent engagement indicates genuine audience connection. Viral spikes without engagement often indicate reach without resonance—those viewers won't return.

### How to Improve (200 words)
**8 Proven Strategies:**

1. **Hook in 3 seconds** - Your first frame determines if viewers engage or scroll
2. **Ask questions in captions** - Prompts comments and boosts engagement signals
3. **Respond to comments within 1 hour** - Early engagement triggers algorithm boost
4. **Use trending sounds strategically** - Familiarity increases engagement
5. **Create "save-worthy" content** - Educational content gets saved and reshared
6. **Post when audience is active** - Check analytics for your peak times
7. **Create duet/stitch-able content** - Invites participation and extends reach
8. **End with a CTA** - Tell viewers exactly what action to take

**What to Avoid:**
- Buying engagement (destroys authenticity signals)
- Posting inconsistently (algorithm penalizes dormancy)
- Ignoring comments (missed engagement opportunity)

Link to: [Complete Guide to Increasing Engagement](/guides/how-to-increase-engagement)

**Calculation:**
(2,800 + 95 + 45) / 45,000 × 100 = **6.5% engagement rate**

At 6.5%, this creator is above average—well-positioned for brand partnerships. According to our brand deal calculator, this engagement rate supports charging $800-$1,500 per sponsored post rather than the $400-$600 a lower-engagement creator at the same follower count might charge.

**Recommendation:** Focus on increasing shares (lowest metric) by creating more "send to a friend" worthy content.

### FAQ (5 questions, 125 words)
1. **What's a "good" engagement rate?** 5%+ is considered good for brand deals. 8%+ puts you in the top tier.

2. **Why is my engagement rate dropping as I grow?** Normal—larger audiences naturally engage at lower rates. Focus on absolute engagement growth.

3. **Should I use follower-based or view-based calculation?** Follower-based for brand pitches; view-based for individual video analysis.

4. **Do engagement pods help?** Short-term yes, long-term no. TikTok detects artificial patterns and may penalize your account.

5. **How often should I check my engagement rate?** Weekly for trends, monthly for strategic decisions.
