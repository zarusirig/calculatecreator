## Calculator 1: Brand Deal Rate Calculator (/calculators/brand-deal-rate)

### Introduction (75 words)
Determine how much to charge for sponsored TikTok content. Your rate depends on follower count, engagement rate, niche, and deliverables. Undercharging leaves money on the table; overcharging loses opportunities. This calculator uses industry data from thousands of brand deals to recommend competitive pricing that reflects your true value.

### How We Calculate (175 words)
**Base Rate Formula:**
Base Rate = (Followers / 1,000) × $10-$25 (varies by niche)

**Engagement Multiplier:**
- Below 3%: 0.7x base
- 3-5%: 1.0x base (standard)
- 5-8%: 1.3x base
- 8%+: 1.5-2.0x base

**Niche Multipliers:**
- Finance/Business: 1.5x
- Tech/Software: 1.4x
- Beauty/Fashion: 1.2x
- Fitness/Health: 1.1x
- Entertainment: 0.9x
- Gaming: 0.8x

**Deliverable Adjustments:**
- Single TikTok: Base rate
- TikTok + Instagram Story: +30%
- TikTok + Instagram Post: +50%
- Usage rights (30 days): +25%
- Usage rights (perpetual): +100-200%
- Exclusivity: +50-100%

**Data Sources:**
- Creator earnings surveys (500+ responses)
- Industry rate cards from talent agencies
- Platform-specific benchmarks

### Why It Matters (150 words)
**💰 Maximize Earnings**
Creators who know their worth earn 2-3x more than those who accept first offers. Brands expect negotiation—lowball offers test if you'll push back.

**🤝 Professional Positioning**
Presenting a data-backed rate establishes you as a professional. "My rate is $1,500 based on my 8% engagement and 75K followers" is more persuasive than "I was thinking maybe $500?"

**⚖️ Fair Exchange**
You're not just selling a post—you're selling access to your audience, your creative skills, and your credibility. Price accordingly.

See [How to Get Brand Deals](/guides/how-to-get-brand-deals) for outreach strategies.

### Negotiation Strategies (200 words)
**8 Tactics for Higher Rates:**

1. **Never accept first offer** - Brands typically offer 50-70% of budget initially
2. **Lead with value, not price** - Present engagement data and past results first
3. **Package multiple deliverables** - Bundles justify higher total prices
4. **Use calculator as evidence** - "Based on industry data, creators with my metrics charge..."
5. **Know your walk-away number** - Have a minimum you won't go below
6. **Ask about budget** - "What's the budget range for this campaign?"
7. **Negotiate deliverables down** - If price is fixed, reduce what you provide
8. **Build relationships** - Long-term partnerships often pay premium rates

**Red Flags to Avoid:**
- "Exposure" as payment (worthless for established creators)
- Unlimited usage rights without significant premium
- Exclusivity without matching compensation
- Payment contingent on "performance metrics"

### Real Example (125 words)
**Scenario:** Lifestyle creator evaluating a brand offer

**Creator Stats:**
- Followers: 85,000
- Engagement Rate: 6.5%
- Niche: Lifestyle/Home

**Brand Offer:** $1,000 for one TikTok + Instagram story + 90-day usage rights

**Calculator Recommendation:**
- Base Rate (85K followers): $850-$2,125
- Engagement Multiplier (6.5%): 1.3x = $1,105-$2,762
- Instagram Story: +30% = $1,436-$3,590
- Usage Rights (90 days): +35% = $1,939-$4,847

**Verdict:** Brand offer of $1,000 is **50-75% below market rate**. Counter with $3,000-$3,500.

### FAQ (5 questions, 125 words)
1. **Should I share my calculator rate with brands?** Share the range, not exact number. "Creators with my metrics typically charge $2,000-$4,000."

2. **What if a brand says my rate is too high?** Ask what their budget is. Consider reducing deliverables rather than rate.

3. **Do rates vary by brand size?** Yes. Fortune 500 companies have bigger budgets than startups.

4. **Should I charge less for brands I love?** Slightly, but never free. Your enthusiasm shows in content quality, which they benefit from.

5. **How do I handle "take it or leave it" offers?** If below your minimum, leave it. Accepting undervalued work sets bad precedent.
