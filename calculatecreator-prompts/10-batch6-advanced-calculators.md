## Calculator 1: Creator Tax Calculator (/calculators/creator-tax)

### Introduction (75 words)
Estimate your tax obligations as a TikTok creator. As a self-employed individual, you're responsible for self-employment tax (15.3%) plus income tax (10-37% depending on bracket). This calculator helps you set aside the right amount—because nothing kills a creator business faster than an unexpected tax bill.

**Disclaimer:** This is for estimation only. Consult a qualified tax professional for personalized advice.

### How We Calculate (175 words)
**Self-Employment Tax:**
- Social Security: 12.4% on income up to $168,600 (2024)
- Medicare: 2.9% on all income
- Additional Medicare: 0.9% on income over $200,000
- **Total SE Tax: 15.3%** on net self-employment income

**Income Tax (2024 brackets, Single):**
| Income | Rate |
|--------|------|
| $0-$11,600 | 10% |
| $11,601-$47,150 | 12% |
| $47,151-$100,525 | 22% |
| $100,526-$191,950 | 24% |
| $191,951-$243,725 | 32% |
| $243,726-$609,350 | 35% |
| Over $609,350 | 37% |

**Deduction:**
- Self-employment tax deduction: 50% of SE tax (deducted from income)
- Standard deduction: $14,600 (2024, single)

**Formula:**
Net Income = Gross Income - Business Expenses - Standard Deduction
SE Tax = Net Income × 15.3%
Income Tax = (Net Income - SE Tax Deduction) × Bracket Rate
Total Tax = SE Tax + Income Tax

|----------------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| $25,000 | ~20-22% | $415-$460 |

**Rule of Thumb:** Set aside 25-35% of all creator income for taxes.

### Why It Matters (150 words)
**📅 Quarterly Estimated Taxes**
If you expect to owe $1,000+ in taxes, IRS requires quarterly estimated payments (April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15). Miss these and face penalties.

**💸 Avoid Surprises**
Many new creators spend all earnings, then face a massive tax bill. Setting aside 30% from day one prevents financial crisis.

**📊 Business Planning**
Understanding true after-tax income helps make decisions about:
- Equipment purchases (tax deductible)
- Going full-time (need 30% more gross than desired take-home)
- Hiring help (creates business expenses)

See [Creator Tax Deductions Guide](/advanced/creator-tax-deductions-guide) for ways to reduce tax burden.

**Tax Calculation:**

### FAQ (5 questions, 125 words)
1. **Do I need to pay taxes on gifted products?** Usually yes, if over $600 from one brand. The FMV is taxable income.

2. **Can I deduct my entire phone bill?** Only the business-use percentage. If 60% business use, deduct 60%.

3. **Do I need an LLC?** Not required, but can provide liability protection and tax benefits at higher incomes.

4. **What if I don't hit $1,000 in taxes?** You can pay annually instead of quarterly.

5. **Should I hire an accountant?** Strongly recommended once earning $50,000+. Tax savings usually exceed their fees.
