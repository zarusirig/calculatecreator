# Source Context Definition

The Source Context is the single most important strategic decision in Koray Tugberk Gubur's Semantic SEO framework. It determines your Central Entity, Central Search Intent, and shapes every downstream decision — from which entity attributes matter to how your topical map is structured. Two websites about the same topic (e.g., "Germany") will produce completely different semantic content networks depending on their Source Context (a visa consultancy vs. a travel blog vs. a history site).

## Why Source Context Matters

The same entity produces radically different topical maps depending on Source Context:
- A **trekking consultancy** writing about Mount Fuji focuses on trails, gear, altitude, seasons
- A **shoe brand** writing about Mount Fuji focuses on terrain, grip, volcanic rock, endurance
- A **travel guide** writing about Mount Fuji focuses on transport, accommodation, views, culture

Source Context filters which attributes are relevant, which queries to target, and how deep to go on each topic.

## Step 1: Gather Brand Identity Inputs

Ask the user these questions (adapt to their context):

1. **What does your business/website do?** (The core offering — be specific)
2. **How do you make money?** (Monetization model: services, products, ads, affiliate, SaaS, consulting)
3. **What makes your perspective unique?** (Your expertise angle — what do you know that others don't?)
4. **Who is your primary audience?** (The humans you serve — not "everyone")
5. **What is the ONE thing your entire website revolves around?** (This becomes the Central Entity)

## Step 2: Define the Central Entity

The Central Entity is the overarching entity your entire website revolves around. It appears (explicitly or implicitly) in ALL articles site-wide.

Rules for identifying the Central Entity:
- It must be broad enough to encompass all your content
- It must be specific enough to differentiate you from competitors
- It should be a real-world entity or well-defined concept (not a keyword)
- Test: Can you trace a logical path from every planned article back to this entity?

**Example:** For a wellness diet program → Central Entity = "Wellness"
**Example:** For a German visa consultancy → Central Entity = "Germany" (filtered through immigration lens)
**Example:** For an AI writing tool → Central Entity = "Content Creation"

## Step 3: Define the Central Search Intent

The Central Search Intent is the PURPOSE that threads through every page. It is expressed as predicates (verbs/actions) that define what users want to DO.

Rules:
- It must reflect the user's goal, not just the topic
- It appears as predicates (action words) across all articles site-wide
- Combined with the Central Entity, it creates site-wide n-grams (phrases that repeat everywhere)

**Example:** Wellness site → "personal development, lifestyle improvement" → predicates: "improve," "develop," "optimize"
**Example:** Visa consultancy → "know before going, prepare for living" → predicates: "know," "go," "prepare," "live"
**Example:** AI writing tool → "create, optimize, publish" → predicates: "create," "optimize," "publish"

## Step 4: Generate Site-wide N-grams

Site-wide n-grams are phrases created by combining the Central Entity with the Central Search Intent. They appear across all articles.

Formula: [Central Entity terms] + [Central Search Intent predicates] = Site-wide N-grams

**Example for visa consultancy:**
- "Germany" + "know before going" = "What to Know Before Going to Germany"
- These n-gram components ("know," "Germany," "going," "before") repeat across ALL articles

## Step 5: Define Quality Nodes

Quality Nodes are high-quality articles placed closest to the homepage. They:
- Encourage deeper crawling by search engines
- Signal that the site is worth investing crawl budget in
- Must be the BEST content on the site
- Are linked directly from the homepage or 1 click away

## Advanced Source Context Principles

### Source Context and Site-wide N-grams
Source Context must be reflected on the website on every web page — in boilerplate, main content, and supplementary content. The site-wide N-grams created from Source Context + Central Entity must appear consistently. If your source context is "Real Estate Consultancy", the climate of a country is processed for property investment impact, NOT for best travel seasons.

### Central Entity Identity Management
The Central Entity is a living thing inside knowledge bases whose identity changes with different attribute-value associations. Your Source Context determines WHICH associations strengthen the desired entity identity. Document statistics (mentions, declarations from sources) shape how search engines resolve entity identity. Align your content's entity processing with the identity you want search engines to assign.

### Topical Authority Through Source Context
Topical Authority = Topical Coverage × Historical Data. Historical Data is NOT time — it is the quality of user engagement (mouse-over, impression, click patterns, session quality). Bad historical data from 6 months ago causes current ranking loss. Source Context determines which content generates POSITIVE historical data (content that satisfies your audience) vs NEGATIVE historical data (content that doesn't match user expectations set by your Source Context).

### Web Components and Search Activity Signals
Source Context must be reflected in web page layout and components. A credit calculation page needs a calculator. A visa consultancy needs application forms. Search engines match web components to possible search activities — if the real-world behavior can't be completed on your page, you can't rank for commercial queries regardless of content quality.

## Important Principles

- The Source Context is IMMUTABLE once set. Changing it means rebuilding the entire semantic content network.
- Every future skill in this framework takes Source Context as an input. Without it, nothing else works.
- Source Context determines which entity attributes pass the Relevance filter (Skill 2) and which queries matter (Skill 3).
- Two competing sites with different Source Contexts will naturally differentiate, even covering the same entities.
