A TikTok "shadowban" is the creator term for reduced For You feed eligibility: your videos stop being recommended on the For You page or surfaced in search, and views collapse without any notification. TikTok does not use the word shadowban — officially, content can become ineligible for recommendation under the platform's For You feed Eligibility Standards — and the fix is to remove the triggering content or behavior, post original guideline-safe videos, and rebuild positive signals over two to four weeks.

This guide explains what is actually happening when creators say "shadowban," how to confirm restricted reach in your analytics instead of guessing, what it costs you in real dollars, and the step-by-step recovery process.

## What a TikTok Shadowban Actually Is

The term "shadowban" originated on Reddit and Twitter to describe hiding a user's content without telling them. On TikTok, the reality is more specific: there is no single "shadowban" switch. TikTok's [For You feed Eligibility Standards](https://www.tiktok.com/community-guidelines/en/fyf-standards) define categories of content that will not be recommended, and its [official recommendation-system documentation](https://newsroom.tiktok.com/en-us/how-tiktok-recommends-videos-for-you) confirms that spam, duplicated content, and content under moderation review are ineligible for the For You feed. What creators experience as a shadowban is typically one of three distinct states.

**Content-level suppression** is the most common form. One or more videos trigger a policy review — automated or manual — and while the review is pending, or after a soft violation is detected, those specific videos receive near-zero distribution. The account otherwise functions normally, and new videos that avoid the same trigger can still perform.

**Account-level throttling** is more severe. The entire account is flagged and all new content receives dramatically reduced initial distribution. This usually follows repeated content violations, suspicious activity patterns (mass follow/unfollow, bot-like engagement), or unauthorized third-party automation tools. Creators in this state commonly report new videos stalling at a tiny fraction of their usual views regardless of quality.

**Search and hashtag de-indexing** means videos stop appearing in search results and hashtag feeds while retaining some For You distribution. Restricted or banned hashtags and gray-area content are frequent triggers.

Identifying which state you are in matters because each calls for a different recovery emphasis — generic "shadowban fixes" waste time when they target the wrong problem.

## Content TikTok Says Is Ineligible for the For You Feed

You do not have to guess what triggers suppression. TikTok publishes the categories of content that are ineligible for recommendation. The most relevant for creators:

1. **Unoriginal, duplicated, or low-quality content** — reposts, content without meaningful creative input, and near-identical uploads.
2. **Spam and deceptive behavior** — engagement bait, manipulative posting patterns, and inauthentic activity.
3. **Content under moderation review** — flagged videos sit out of recommendation until cleared.
4. **Sexually suggestive borderline content** — material that skirts, without breaking, the adult-content rules.
5. **Dangerous activities and challenges** shown without professional context.
6. **Unverified claims and misinformation-adjacent content**, especially on health, financial, and civic topics.

The full list lives in the official [For You feed Eligibility Standards](https://www.tiktok.com/community-guidelines/en/fyf-standards) — reading it once is worth more than any shadowban folklore. Content that outright violates the rules escalates beyond suppression into strikes; our guide to [TikTok community guidelines strikes](/learn/tiktok-community-guidelines-strikes/) covers that system.

## Common Causes of Restricted Reach

Beyond the official ineligibility categories, these are the triggers creators most frequently report.

### Community Guidelines Gray Areas

Even accidental brushes with policy can trigger review: revealing clothing or innuendo that trips the risk filter, injuries or medical procedures shown without context, health or financial claims the system cannot verify, copyrighted audio flagged by rights management, and spam-pattern behavior such as posting near-identical videos repeatedly. While a flagged video sits in review — a period creators commonly report lasting one to three days — it receives minimal distribution, and the lost early momentum often persists even after clearance.

### Suspicious Account Activity

Actions that mimic bot behavior frequently trigger account-level restrictions: following or unfollowing accounts in rapid bursts, mass-liking sprees, posting many videos in a single day, connecting unauthorized third-party apps, and rapid switching between devices or VPN locations.

### Technical and Metadata Issues

Less obvious triggers include [banned or restricted hashtags](/learn/tiktok-hashtag-strategy-2026/), unverifiable URLs in captions or bios, and upload metadata suggesting unauthorized tooling.

## How to Detect a Shadowban

Not every dip is a shadowban. [TikTok's algorithm naturally fluctuates](/learn/tiktok-algorithm-explained/), and one underperforming video means nothing. Run these four checks — our [TikTok analytics guide](/learn/tiktok-analytics-guide/) shows where each metric lives.

**Check traffic sources.** In analytics, open Content, select a recent video, and look at "Traffic source types." For You traffic at zero or near-zero across multiple videos — while profile and Following traffic stay normal — is the strongest indicator of suppression.

**Test search visibility.** Post a video containing a unique made-up word in the caption (like "testxyz789"), wait an hour, and search the term. If the video never appears, your content is likely de-indexed from search.

**Compare engagement rates.** A suppressed video typically shows collapsed views but roughly normal engagement rates among the few viewers who do see it. If views and engagement rate dropped together, the likelier culprit is content quality or audience fatigue — see [why engagement rates drop](/learn/engagement-rate-dropping/).

**Watch view velocity.** Normal distribution curves upward when early engagement is strong. Suppressed videos flatline within the first hour and never accelerate, regardless of how well that small audience responds.

| Indicator | Normal dip | Likely suppression |
|---|---|---|
| Views on new videos | Moderately below average | Collapsed to a small fraction of average |
| For You page traffic | Reduced but present | Zero or near-zero |
| Search visibility | Normal | Videos not appearing |
| Duration | 1-3 videos | 5+ consecutive videos |
| Engagement rate (of those who see it) | Lower than usual | Normal or only slightly lower |

These patterns reflect commonly reported creator experiences — TikTok publishes no official suppression signatures.

## What a Shadowban Costs You

For monetized creators, restricted reach is a direct pay cut, because the [Creator Rewards Program](https://support.tiktok.com/en/business-and-creator/creator-rewards-program/creator-rewards-program) pays only on **qualified views** — unique For You feed views watched more than 5 seconds — at an estimated $0.50-$2.00 per 1,000. A video that never enters For You recommendation generates zero qualified views and zero payout, and each video must clear 1,000 qualified For You feed views before earning anything.

Worked example: a creator averaging 2 million qualified views per month earns an estimated $1,000-$4,000 from Creator Rewards. If suppression cuts For You distribution to 5% of normal for a month, that is 100,000 qualified views — roughly $50-$200. The suppression costs an estimated $950-$3,800 in that month alone, before counting lost brand-deal leverage and follower growth.

There is a second-order risk: Creator Rewards eligibility requires 100,000 video views in the last 30 days. An extended suppression can drop an otherwise-qualified account below that threshold, pausing earnings even after distribution recovers. ## Step-by-Step Shadowban Recovery

If your checks confirm suppression, follow these steps in order. Creators commonly report recovery within 7-21 days for content-level issues and 14-30 days for account-level restrictions; TikTok publishes no official timelines.

**Step 1: Remove the trigger.** Review your last five to ten videos against the eligibility standards above. Delete videos with strikes or warnings and anything clearly borderline. If you used restricted hashtags, delete the affected videos entirely rather than editing captions — moderation classification attaches to the upload.

**Step 2: Stop all growth shortcuts.** End any follow/unfollow activity, bot-assisted engagement, unauthorized automation, or engagement pods immediately. These signals compound the restriction.

**Step 3: Reduce posting frequency temporarily.** Scale to one video per day for a week, then two to three per week the following week. Flooding the feed during suppression is a common mistake that can extend it.

**Step 4: Post safe, high-quality original content.** During recovery, stay unambiguously inside the guidelines and prioritize content built for strong [watch time and completion](/calculators/completion-rate/) — retention signals are what rebuild algorithmic standing fastest.

**Step 5: Engage authentically.** Spend 15-20 minutes daily genuinely engaging in your niche: thoughtful comments, real shares, replies on your own videos. This rebuilds normal-human account signals.

**Step 6: Appeal specific violations.** If a video received a violation notice, appeal it through notifications with a clear explanation. A successful appeal restores that video's distribution and can accelerate account-level recovery.

## Shadowban Recovery Timeline

The framework below reflects commonly reported creator experiences, not official TikTok data — treat it as a planning guide.

| Recovery phase | Timeline | What to expect | Key action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cleanup | Days 1-3 | Views still heavily suppressed | Remove triggers, stop shortcuts, reduce posting |
| Early recovery | Days 4-10 | First videos regain partial For You traffic | Post safe original content, engage authentically |
| Mid recovery | Days 11-18 | View velocity begins accelerating again | Gradually increase posting frequency |
| Full recovery | Days 19-30 | Distribution approaches pre-suppression norms | Resume normal strategy |

If distribution has not meaningfully improved after 30 days of clean behavior, the account may carry a longer-lived flag — at that point, sustained guideline-safe posting and appeals are the only levers, and starting fresh content series can help re-establish positive signals.

## How to Prevent Future Shadowbans

Prevention is far cheaper than recovery. Build these habits into your workflow.

**Re-read the eligibility standards quarterly.** Policies evolve — pay particular attention to AI-generated content, health claims, and financial advice.

**Run a pre-post checklist.** Copyrighted audio? Visuals that could read as graphic or suggestive? Hashtags verified as unrestricted? Claims that need substantiation?

**Monitor analytics weekly.** Falling For You traffic across multiple videos is the early warning. Track [engagement rate trends](/calculators/engagement-rate/) alongside traffic sources to spot anomalies before they compound.

**Never manipulate engagement.** Bought followers, pods, and automation are exactly the "spam and deceptive behavior" the eligibility standards name — the short-term bump is never worth a multi-week suppression.

**Diversify content signals.** Accounts that post one format with one audio style are fragile: a single policy change can affect the whole catalog. Mix formats, topics, and sounds.

## FAQ

### Are TikTok shadowbans real?

The experience is real; the official feature is not. TikTok has never confirmed "shadowbans," but it openly documents that certain content is ineligible for For You recommendation and that flagged content is suppressed during review. "Shadowban" is simply the creator shorthand for landing in those states.

### How long does a TikTok shadowban last?

Creators commonly report 7-21 days for content-level suppression and 14-30 days for account-level throttling when they follow a structured cleanup. TikTok publishes no official timelines, and continuing the triggering behavior extends the restriction.

### Does deleting videos fix a shadowban?

Deleting the specific videos that triggered flags — strikes, warnings, restricted hashtags — helps remove the cause. Mass-deleting your catalog does not, and re-uploading the same video is counterproductive: duplicated content is itself ineligible for recommendation.

### Can you reset the TikTok algorithm to escape a shadowban?

No. The feed-refresh option resets what you see as a viewer, not how your content is distributed. Starting a new account forfeits your followers and monetization eligibility and usually repeats the cycle if the triggering behavior continues.

### Does a shadowban affect Creator Rewards earnings?

Directly. Creator Rewards pays only on qualified For You feed views, so suppressed videos earn nothing, and an extended suppression can drop you below the program's 100,000-views-in-30-days eligibility requirement.

### Does a shadowban affect all your videos or just the ones that triggered it?

It depends on which state you are in. Content-level suppression hits only the specific videos that tripped a review, so new guideline-safe uploads can still reach the For You feed normally. Account-level throttling is broader — every new video launches with reduced initial distribution until the flag clears. Checking "Traffic source types" across several recent videos tells you which pattern you have, and therefore whether to delete a handful of triggering posts or clean up account-level signals like automation and follow/unfollow bursts.
