TikTok Shop commission rates split into two separate numbers: the **referral fee sellers pay TikTok** (roughly 2%–8% of the order, by product tier) and the **affiliate commission sellers pay creators** (2%–20%, around 5% site-standard, and higher for negotiated deals). Affiliates keep their commission gross — they do not pay the referral fee, payment processing, or shipping. Sellers pay all of it, which is why the seller and affiliate views of "commission" look so different.

This page breaks down both sides by category, stacks every fee on a single worked example, and shows what actually reaches a seller's bank account after a sale clears. ## Quick Answer

- **Seller referral fee:** roughly 2%–8% of the order value, by category tier (2% essentials, 5% standard, 8% luxury).
- **Site calculator assumption:** the profit calculator applies a **2.9% blended platform fee** as its default.
- **Affiliate commission:** 2%–20%, about 5% site-standard; Open Collaboration averages 10–15%, Targeted deals reach 18–50%.
- **Payment processing:** roughly 2% on top of the referral fee.
- **Who pays what:** sellers pay referral, processing, shipping, and affiliate commission; affiliates pay nothing and receive gross commission.

## TikTok Platform Commission

TikTok Shop charges **sellers** a referral fee on every transaction completed through the platform. This referral fee is TikTok's primary revenue mechanism for its marketplace, and it applies to all orders regardless of how the buyer found the product — organic content, live shopping, or paid promotion. Affiliates never pay this fee; it comes out of the seller's revenue before disbursement.

The referral fee runs on a category tier structure of roughly **2% to 8%**: about 2% for essentials, 5% for standard categories, and 8% for luxury and high-margin categories. The fee applies to the total order value, including the product price and any buyer-paid shipping, and TikTok deducts it before funds reach the seller.

The site's profit calculator uses a simplified **2.9% blended platform fee** as its default assumption, which sits inside that 2%–8% band. External 2026 reporting cites higher effective numbers: [Dashboardly](https://www.dashboardly.io/post/tiktok-shop-affiliate-commissions-2026-payouts-clawbacks-profit-math) describes a flat **~6% referral fee** for US sellers, with an optional ~6% shipping fee stacking on top for sellers who opt into TikTok Shop logistics. Ignore the stale 2%-only claim from TikTok Shop's 2023–2024 launch era — that was a promotional rate, not the current standard. When your margins are tight, model against the higher 6% figure to stay conservative.

## Seller Referral Fees by Category

The distinction between the tiers is driven by product margin. Higher-margin categories like beauty and fashion absorb the top-tier fee more easily than thin-margin categories like electronics.

| Category tier | Typical referral fee | Example categories |
|---|---|---|
| Essentials | ~2% | Everyday basics, low-margin staples |
| Standard | ~5% | Home & garden, food & beverage, most general merchandise |
| Luxury / high-margin | ~8% | Beauty, fashion, jewelry, wellness devices |

Beauty and personal care sit at the top tier because most beauty products carry 60–80% gross margins, making an 8% referral fee manageable. Electronics sit at the lower tier because tech margins are thin and a high fee would price the category out of the marketplace.

## Affiliate Commission Rates by Category

Affiliate commission is a **separate cost the seller chooses to pay creators** to promote a product — it is not a TikTok platform fee. The site-standard commission is around 5%, within a 2%–20% working range. Sellers set their own rate per product, so two brands in the same category often post very different commissions.

| Category | Typical affiliate commission | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Beauty & Skincare | 8% – 20% | Highest commissions; smaller brands bid up to win creators. |
| Health & Wellness | 10% – 18% | Repeat-purchase behavior supports higher rates. |
| Fashion & Jewelry | 10% – 15% | High conversion, higher return rates. |
| Pet Products | 8% – 15% | Shareable content, steady engagement. |
| Baby & Parenting | 8% – 14% | Trust-driven recommendation purchases. |
| Home Organization | 5% – 12% | Impulse category with strong demos. |
| Kitchen Gadgets | 5% – 12% | Visual payoff drives conversion. |
| Tech / Electronics | 2% – 8% | Low rate offset by higher order values. |

TikTok Shop structures affiliate deals two ways. **Open Collaboration** offers a fixed public rate — [ShortFormNation](https://www.shortformnation.com/blog/tiktok-shop-affiliate-marketing-the-complete-2026-guide) reports these commonly sit around 10–15%. **Targeted Collaboration** is a private negotiated deal, where rates reach 18–50% for creators with a proven sales record. [Hamster Garage](https://www.hamstergarage.com/article/tiktok-shop-affiliate-statistics-benchmarks-roi) puts the average effective US Shop commission near 13% once negotiated deals are blended in. For the creator's side of this math — commission per sale and monthly income — see [TikTok Shop affiliate earnings](/learn/tiktok-shop-affiliate-earnings/).

## Additional Fees

The referral fee is not the only cost of selling on TikTok Shop. Several fees reduce a seller's effective margin and must be built into profit calculations.

**Payment processing fee: approximately 2%.** TikTok charges a transaction fee to cover payment processing on top of the referral fee. Combined, a seller's baseline platform cost lands near 7%–10% before any other expense.

**Shipping and fulfillment.** Sellers cover shipping in most markets unless the buyer pays a separate shipping fee. Lightweight products ship cheaply through TikTok's partnered carriers; heavier items erode margin fast. Sellers who opt into TikTok Shop logistics or a shipping-fee program can see an additional ~6% stack on top of the referral fee, per [Dashboardly](https://www.dashboardly.io/post/tiktok-shop-affiliate-commissions-2026-payouts-clawbacks-profit-math).

**Affiliate commissions.** When a seller uses the affiliate network, the commission stacks on top of platform fees. A beauty seller offering a 15% affiliate commission pays roughly 15% + an 8% referral fee + ~2% processing = about **25%** in combined fees and commissions before product cost. This stacking is the single most important cost to plan for.

**Returns, refunds, and clawbacks.** TikTok Shop's return policy is buyer-friendly, and sellers bear return costs. Return rates run roughly 5%–15%, with fashion at the high end. Affiliate commissions on returned orders are **clawed back** during the settlement window, and rate changes carry a protection period (commonly a 30-day rate lock), so neither commission nor rate is locked in permanently.

## Worked Example: Full Fee Stack on a $45 Product

The table below stacks every cost on a single $45 beauty product sold through an affiliate at a 12% commission, from the seller's perspective. It shows exactly what is left for profit after TikTok's cut, the creator's cut, shipping, and product cost.

| Line item | Amount | % of sale |
|---|---|---|
| Sale price | $45.00 | 100% |
| Affiliate commission (12%) | −$5.40 | 12.0% |
| Referral fee (8% beauty tier) | −$3.60 | 8.0% |
| Payment processing (~2%) | −$0.90 | 2.0% |
| Shipping (seller-paid estimate) | −$4.00 | 8.9% |
| Product cost / COGS (~30%) | −$13.50 | 30.0% |
| **Estimated seller profit** | **$17.60** | **39.1%** |

On this order, the **affiliate receives $5.40 gross** — and pays none of the other fees. The **seller keeps about $17.60** after every cost. If the customer returns the item inside the settlement window, the affiliate's $5.40 is clawed back and the seller absorbs the shipping and handling both directions. ## How TikTok Shop Fees Compare

TikTok Shop's fee stack lands competitively against other marketplaces once you account for the full picture. Amazon charges referral fees of roughly 8%–15% by category, plus fulfillment and storage fees for FBA sellers. Shopify charges no marketplace referral fee but requires a monthly subscription plus payment processing on every order. TikTok Shop's roughly 2%–8% referral fee positions it at the lower end of marketplace costs — especially for sellers who drive sales through organic content instead of paid ads.

The catch is the affiliate layer. On Amazon or Shopify, creator commissions are optional and often run 3%–10%. On TikTok Shop, affiliate promotion is central to how products get discovered, so most sellers offer 10%–20% to stay competitive for creator attention. That single line item usually outweighs the referral fee, which is why the [worked fee-stack example](#worked-example-full-fee-stack-on-a-45-product) above shows commission as the largest platform-side cost on the order.

## How to Reduce Your Costs

Selling profitably on TikTok Shop means managing the cumulative impact of platform fees, shipping, and affiliate costs. These strategies improve effective margin.

**Bundle products to raise average order value.** A higher order value spreads fixed shipping across more revenue. Bundling a cleanser and moisturizer keeps the referral percentage the same while shrinking shipping as a share of revenue.

**Optimize packaging and shipping weight.** Lighter, more compact packaging cuts per-order shipping cost. Small reductions compound across thousands of orders.

**Tier your affiliate commissions.** Offer a 5–8% baseline and reserve higher rates for top performers who drive real volume through Targeted Collaboration. This keeps average affiliate cost down while still rewarding your best promoters.

**Use TikTok's promotional credits and subsidies.** TikTok periodically offers shipping discounts and seller subsidies. Free-shipping subsidies alone can add 3–5% back to margin on qualifying orders — monitor Seller Center and time launches around them.

**Drive organic traffic to cut acquisition cost.** Sellers who create their own content or build relationships with genuine creators generate sales without paying affiliate commission on every order. The long-term margin advantage of organic sales over affiliate-driven sales is substantial.

**Price with the full fee stack in mind.** Work backward from a target margin. If you need 40% gross after all costs, price the product to deliver that margin net of referral fee, processing, shipping, affiliate commission, and estimated returns — not off product cost alone.

## FAQ

### What is the TikTok Shop commission rate in 2026?

TikTok Shop's affiliate commission is about **5% site-standard**, within a 2%–20% range, and separately TikTok charges **sellers** a referral fee of roughly **2%–8%** by category. Open Collaboration commissions typically run 10–15%, and negotiated Targeted deals reach 18–50%.

### What fees does TikTok Shop charge sellers per sale?

TikTok Shop charges sellers a **referral fee of about 2%–8%** plus a **payment processing fee near 2%**, so baseline platform cost is roughly 7%–10%. Sellers also pay shipping and any affiliate commission they offer, which stacks on top.

### Do affiliates pay TikTok Shop fees?

No. Affiliates receive their commission **gross** and pay none of the referral fee, processing fee, or shipping. Those costs come out of the seller's revenue, which is why the seller's take-home is much lower than the sale price.

### Why does the calculator use 2.9% when web sources say 6%?

The site's profit calculator uses a **2.9% blended platform fee** as a simplified default that sits inside TikTok's 2%–8% tier structure. Third-party 2026 reporting cites a flat ~6% referral fee for US sellers, so model against 6% when you want a conservative estimate.

### Are TikTok Shop commission rates locked in?

No. Affiliate commissions are **clawed back on returns** within the settlement window, and sellers can change rates, subject to a protection period (commonly a 30-day rate lock). Neither the commission nor the rate is permanent.
