TikTok rewards the videos that hold attention, not the accounts with the most followers. That is good news if you learn to read your analytics: the same dashboard that shows your views also tells you exactly why the algorithm did or did not push a video. This guide covers the metrics that matter, the benchmarks to measure yourself against, and a simple weekly routine for turning data into growth.

_Last updated: July 2026 · Benchmarks reflect TT Calculator's Q1 2026 dataset._

## How TikTok's Algorithm Distributes Videos

TikTok tests almost every upload on a small audience first, then expands reach in waves based on how that test performs. Understanding the stages tells you what to optimize for.

1. **Initial test (roughly 200–500 viewers).** TikTok shows the video to a small sample and measures completion rate and early engagement.
2. **First push (about 1K–10K viewers).** If the test performs well — think 60%+ completion and 5%+ engagement — reach expands to a broader audience.
3. **Viral threshold (100K+ viewers).** If metrics hold (70%+ completion and rising shares), the video moves to wide For You feed distribution.
4. **Sustained distribution (1M+ viewers).** If the numbers stay strong, a video can keep circulating for days or weeks, with shares and saves extending its lifespan.

The takeaway: the first hour is critical. Those first few hundred viewers decide whether your video gets a bigger audience, so a strong hook and immediate engagement matter more than anything else.

## The Metrics That Matter Most

Not every number in your dashboard deserves equal attention. Prioritize the signals TikTok actually uses to rank content.

- **Completion rate** — the single strongest signal. It is the share of viewers who watch to the end, calculated as (average watch time ÷ video length) × 100. A 20-second video with 16 seconds of average watch time scores 80%.
- **Engagement rate** — (likes + comments + shares + saves) ÷ views × 100. The platform average is about **4.07%**. Under 2% is below average, 2–5% is average, 5–10% is good, and 10%+ is excellent.
- **Shares and saves** — weighted more heavily than likes because they signal real value. Saves in particular tell the algorithm a video is worth returning to.
- **Follow conversion** — profile visits that turn into follows, a measure of how compelling your overall presence is.

## Business Metrics for Creators and Brands

If you sell products, services, or run campaigns, layer profitability metrics on top of reach metrics.

- **Conversion rate** — the share of viewers who take a desired action (click, purchase, sign up). Around 1.5–3% is solid for e-commerce; 5%+ is excellent. Track it with UTM links via the [Conversion Rate Calculator](/calculators/conversion-rate/).
- **Customer acquisition cost (CAC)** — total spend ÷ customers acquired. Aim to keep CAC below about 30% of a customer's lifetime value.
- **Lifetime value (LTV)** — average purchase × purchase frequency × customer lifespan. A healthy business keeps LTV at roughly 3–5x CAC, so retention pays off.
- **Campaign ROI** — (revenue − cost) ÷ cost × 100. Track every cost (ads, creator fees, production) to keep the math honest.

## How to Analyze Your Performance

### Weekly review process

- **Monday:** Run the Engagement Rate Calculator on last week's videos and identify your top three performers.
- **Tuesday:** Analyze completion rates. Which hooks and formats held viewers longest?
- **Wednesday:** Check follower conversion. Is your profile-visit-to-follow ratio healthy?
- **Thursday:** Review share and save rates to see what drove reach.
- **Friday:** Plan next week from the data — more of what worked, plus one or two new formats to test.

### Monthly optimization

- Compare month-over-month growth metrics.
- Identify the content themes with the best performance.
- Audit low performers for patterns to avoid.
- Set next month's targets (engagement rate, follower goal).
- Test new angles based on what is trending in your niche.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the most important TikTok metric to track?

Completion rate is number one. It measures the percentage of viewers who watch your video to the end, and videos with 70%+ completion tend to get the widest For You feed distribution. It signals content quality to the algorithm more directly than likes or views.

### What is a good engagement rate on TikTok?

5–10% is good and 10%+ is excellent; most creators fall in the 2–5% range, against a platform average near 4.07%. Smaller accounts (under 10K) typically run higher, often 8–15%. Engagement rate matters more than follower count for brand deals — 50K followers at 8% engagement is more valuable than 200K at 2%.

### How do I access TikTok Analytics?

Switch to a Creator or Business account (Settings → Manage Account), then open Profile → Menu → Creator tools → Analytics. It requires at least 100 followers and shows the last 7, 28, or 60 days of views, followers, engagement, and audience demographics.

### What does completion rate mean on TikTok?

Completion rate is the percentage of viewers who watched your entire video, calculated as (average watch time ÷ video length) × 100. A 20-second video with 16 seconds of average watch time equals 80%. It is the strongest algorithmic signal, and 80%+ completion often triggers viral distribution.

### What metrics do brands look for in TikTok creators?

Brands prioritize engagement rate (they look for 4%+, and premium brands for 6%+), audience demographics that match their target market, a consistent niche, healthy follower-conversion rates, and steady average views. High engagement with a relevant audience beats a large follower count with low engagement.

### How can I improve my TikTok completion rate?

Hook viewers in the first three seconds, keep early videos short enough to finish, use pattern interrupts every five to seven seconds, cut dead air, and deliver on your hook's promise. Testing different openings on similar content is the fastest way to learn what holds attention.

### Should I focus on views or engagement rate?

Both, for different reasons. Views drive reach and Creator Rewards earnings; engagement rate drives brand-deal value and long-term growth. Optimizing for high engagement tends to lift views too, because the algorithm rewards quality signals with more distribution.
