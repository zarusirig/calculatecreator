To qualify for TikTok's current view-based monetization in 2026 you need at least **10,000 followers, 100,000 video views in the last 30 days, an age of 18 or older (19 in South Korea), a Personal account in good standing, and residence in an eligible country**. Those thresholds now gate the **Creator Rewards Program** — the performance-based program that replaced the original Creator Fund, which TikTok has discontinued — and Creator Rewards adds one rule the Fund never had: only original public videos at least one minute long can earn.

Last updated: July 2026 · Written by the TT Calculator Team. This guide breaks down every requirement, what counts and what does not, and how close your account is, based on [TikTok's official Creator Rewards eligibility criteria](https://support.tiktok.com/en/business-and-creator/creator-rewards-program/creator-rewards-program). One clarification up front, because it trips up almost everyone: the "1,000 views" number you may have seen is *not* an eligibility bar — it is the per-video threshold a video must clear before it starts earning. Account eligibility still requires 100,000 views in 30 days.

## Current TikTok Creator Fund and Rewards Eligibility Criteria Explained

The short answer: if you are 18 or older (19 in South Korea), have at least 10,000 followers, have accumulated 100,000 views in the last 30 days, run a Personal account in an eligible region, and have no active Community Guidelines violations, you meet the bar. That bar belongs to the Creator Rewards Program, which TikTok rolled out as the successor to the original Creator Fund across 2024 and 2025. The legacy Creator Fund is now discontinued per [TikTok's Creator Fund support page](https://support.tiktok.com/en/business-and-creator/creator-fund-us/creator-fund), so applying to it is no longer an option — Creator Rewards is the path for new applicants.

Here is a side-by-side comparison of the eligibility requirements for the legacy Fund and the current program:

| Requirement | Creator Fund (legacy, discontinued) | Creator Rewards Program (current) |
|---|---|---|
| Status in 2026 | Discontinued | Active |
| Minimum age | 18 years old | 18 years old (19 in South Korea) |
| Follower count | 10,000+ | 10,000+ |
| Video views (last 30 days) | 100,000+ | 100,000+ |
| Minimum video length | No minimum | Over 1 minute (60 seconds) |
| Content originality | Standard (no reposts) | Original public content only |
| Account standing | Good standing, no active strikes | Good standing, no active strikes |

| Application method | In-app Creator Tools | TikTok Studio → Creator Rewards Program |
| Reply time | Historic | About 3 days |

The two programs share the same entry bar — 10,000 followers and 100,000 views in 30 days. The real difference is at the content level: Creator Rewards only pays on original videos over one minute, and it counts qualified views rather than raw views. That content requirement, not a lower follower or view threshold, is what defines who actually earns under the current program.

## How Current TikTok Creator Fund and Rewards Eligibility Criteria Works

TikTok evaluates eligibility through an automated system that checks your account metrics when you submit your application through TikTok Studio. The platform pulls your current follower count, calculates your rolling 30-day view total, verifies your age from your registration data, confirms your account type and region, and cross-references your account against any active Community Guidelines violations. TikTok says it typically replies within about three days.

One critical detail many creators miss: the 30-day view count is a rolling window, not a calendar month. TikTok recalculates it daily. If you hit 100,000 views on January 15 but your oldest qualifying views start dropping off by February 14, your total could dip below the threshold before your application processes. The safest approach is to apply when your 30-day view count sits at least 20-30% above the minimum.

Your account standing is judged against TikTok's Community Guidelines and Terms of Service. A single minor violation that has been resolved usually will not disqualify you, but an active strike at the time of application triggers a rejection. If your application is rejected, you can appeal within 30 days, or submit a fresh application 30 days after the appeal window closes.

There is one more mechanic worth understanding before you apply, because it is the source of the "1,000 views" confusion. Under Creator Rewards, being eligible does not mean every video earns. Each individual video must reach **1,000 qualified For You feed views** — unique views watched for more than 5 seconds — before it starts generating rewards. That is a per-video earning gate that sits *inside* the program, separate from the 100,000-view account eligibility requirement.

### Age

You must be at least 18 years old to have joined the Creator Fund or to join the Creator Rewards Program (19 in South Korea, per TikTok's official criteria). TikTok verifies age through the birthdate you provided during account registration. There is no way to change that date after account creation without contacting TikTok support and providing government-issued identification.

Creators between 13 and 17 can build their audience and content library, but they cannot monetize through the program until they turn 18. Once you reach the age threshold, your existing follower count and view history count toward eligibility immediately — you do not need to accumulate followers or views only after your birthday.

If you entered an incorrect birthdate when creating your account, TikTok's age-correction process usually takes one to two weeks and requires a clear photo of a valid ID. Plan ahead if your 18th birthday is approaching and your account birthdate does not match your actual date of birth.

### Followers

The program requires a minimum of 10,000 followers. This is your total follower count at the time of application, not your net gain over any specific period. Purchased or bot followers may pad the raw number, but TikTok periodically purges inauthentic accounts. If a purge drops you below 10,000 after approval, your monetization access can be paused until you recover the threshold.

Most creators reach 10,000 followers over several months of consistent posting, and accounts in high-engagement niches like entertainment, comedy, and lifestyle often get there faster — but there is no guaranteed timeline, and growth speed depends heavily on niche, posting frequency, and how early the algorithm finds your audience.

Building a genuine follower base matters beyond the raw count. Accounts with strong follower-to-engagement ratios tend to earn higher [per-view payouts](/learn/creator-fund-pay-per-view/), because TikTok distributes their content to more engaged audiences, which supports the watch time and qualified views that drive RPM.

### Views

Both the legacy Fund and Creator Rewards require 100,000 views in the trailing 30-day window — there is no lower view bar for Creator Rewards, despite a widespread myth to the contrary. 100,000 views in 30 days works out to roughly 3,333 views per day on average. A creator posting once daily needs each video to average at least 3,333 views; a creator posting three times daily needs only about 1,111 views per post. Volume and consistency both feed directly into clearing this threshold.

Where the "1,000 views" figure comes from is the per-video earning gate described above: under Creator Rewards, an individual video must reach 1,000 qualified For You feed views before it earns anything. That is not an eligibility requirement — it is a floor each monetized video crosses on its way to earning. The account-level gate remains 100,000 views in 30 days.

The real content gate for Creator Rewards is format. Videos must be longer than one minute and genuinely original. Compilations, re-uploads, Duets, Stitches, watermarked content, photo slideshows, and videos that lean on unoriginal audio or footage will not earn monetized views even when the creator's account is enrolled. ## Creator Fund Eligibility Requirements 2026 Data and Numbers

The figures below reflect TikTok's published guidelines and canonical site estimates as of July 2026. Every dollar figure is a planning estimate, not a guaranteed rate — TikTok publishes no official rate card:

- **Creator Fund RPM (historical):** $0.02-$0.05 per 1,000 views, averaging about $0.03 — roughly $20-$50 per million views.
- **Creator Rewards RPM (estimate):** $0.50-$2.00 per 1,000 qualified views; high-CPM niches like finance run higher (up to about $3.00), while comedy sits lower (about $0.30-$0.80).
- **Regional variation:** The US is the 1.0x baseline. The UK runs about 0.9x, the EU about 0.75x, and lower-CPM regions such as Africa about 0.3x of US rates on the same view count.
- **Follower bar in context:** 10,000 followers is the entry point of the "micro" creator tier (10,000-50,000). View-based earnings scale up through the mid (50,000-200,000) and macro (200,000-1,000,000) tiers.
- **Most common rejection reason:** a 30-day view count that slips below 100,000 while the application is processing.
- **Second most common rejection reason:** an active Community Guidelines strike at the time of application.

Country availability shapes earning potential as much as eligibility does. US-based creators consistently see the highest RPMs, followed by the UK and Western Europe; lower-CPM regions earn a fraction of the US rate on the same view count. The gap between the old Fund and Creator Rewards is substantial: Creator Rewards pays an estimated 10-40x more per view for eligible content. That is what makes the shift to longer-form original video financially worthwhile for creators who previously relied on the Fund.

## How to Apply for the Creator Rewards Program

Once you clear the thresholds above, the application itself is short. TikTok processes it automatically and usually replies within about three days.

1. **Confirm your numbers first.** Check that you have 10,000+ followers, 100,000+ views in the last 30 days, a Personal account in good standing, and are 18 or older (19 in South Korea). Apply when your 30-day views sit comfortably above 100,000, because the window recalculates daily and a dip during processing is the most common rejection reason.
2. **Open TikTok Studio.** In the app, go to your Profile, tap the Menu button, and open TikTok Studio.
3. **Select Creator Rewards Program and apply.** Follow the prompts and complete any identity or tax verification TikTok requests, then submit and wait for the automated review — typically about three days.
4. **If you are rejected, appeal or reapply.** You can appeal within 30 days of a rejection, or submit a fresh application 30 days after the appeal window closes. Most rejections trace back to a metric slipping below threshold during processing or an unresolved account-standing issue.
5. **Post new content — the program is not retroactive.** Only videos uploaded after you join can collect rewards, so time your application before a content push, not after one. Your back catalogue will not start earning.

TikTok's [Creator Academy](https://www.tiktok.com/creator-academy/en/articles/monetize/creator-rewards) covers the same steps in its official walkthrough, and our [guide to joining the Creator Fund's successor program](/learn/how-to-join-creator-fund/) adds screenshots and troubleshooting.

## How to Improve Your Results

If you do not yet meet the criteria, here is a targeted roadmap.

**To reach 10,000 followers faster:**
- Post 4-7 times per week consistently. TikTok's algorithm favors accounts that demonstrate regular activity.
- Focus on one niche for your first 30-60 days. The algorithm categorizes your account from early content signals, and niche consistency helps it find your audience faster.
- Engage with trending sounds and formats within your niche, but always add original commentary or perspective.
- Respond to comments within the first hour of posting. Early engagement signals boost distribution.

**To hit the 100,000-view threshold:**
- Increase posting frequency temporarily. Three posts a day for two weeks can accumulate views quickly even if individual videos perform modestly.
- Use Duets and Stitches to tap into high-view conversations for reach — but note these formats build audience only; they do not themselves earn under Creator Rewards.
- Post during peak hours for your target audience. For US audiences, 7-9 AM and 7-11 PM EST consistently show the strongest initial engagement.

**To qualify to earn under Creator Rewards:**
- Produce content over one minute. This is the single most important shift for accessing the higher-paying program.
- Keep every monetized video original. Remove re-used footage, and use original audio or properly licensed sounds.
- Favor educational, tutorial, or story-driven formats that naturally support longer watch times and higher qualified-view rates.

## FAQ

### Is the TikTok Creator Fund still available in 2026?

No. TikTok has discontinued the Creator Fund, and its [official support page](https://support.tiktok.com/en/business-and-creator/creator-fund-us/creator-fund) confirms it is no longer available. The Creator Rewards Program is the current view-based monetization program; all Fund-era rates ($0.02-$0.05 per 1,000 views) are historical reference points only.

### How many followers and views do you need to join the Creator Rewards Program?

At least 10,000 followers and 100,000 video views in the last 30 days, plus an age of 18 or older (19 in South Korea), a Personal account in good standing, and residence in an eligible country. You also need to post original public videos over one minute long to actually earn.

### Is the Creator Rewards view requirement really only 1,000 views?

No — that is a common mix-up. Account eligibility requires 100,000 views in 30 days. The 1,000 figure is a *per-video* earning gate: each individual video must reach 1,000 qualified For You feed views before it starts generating rewards. It is separate from the account eligibility bar.

### Do you have to be 18 to qualify?

Yes. You must be at least 18 (19 in South Korea) to earn through either program, verified against the birthdate on your account. Creators aged 13-17 can build their audience but cannot monetize until they turn 18.

### Can Business or faceless accounts qualify for Creator Rewards?

Faceless accounts can qualify — the program judges content, not whether you appear on camera, so long as videos are original, public, and over one minute. Business accounts cannot: TikTok requires a Personal account, and Business, political, and government accounts are not eligible. Switch to a Personal account before applying if needed.

### Which countries have the Creator Rewards Program?

Creator Rewards is available in select markets, including the US, UK, and major EU countries. The legacy Creator Fund historically ran in the US, UK, Germany, France, Italy, and Spain, and those markets carry over — but availability changes, so check the in-app eligibility list in TikTok Studio for your region. Rates also vary by country; our [Creator Fund rates by country breakdown](/learn/creator-fund-rates-by-country/) maps the regional differences.
