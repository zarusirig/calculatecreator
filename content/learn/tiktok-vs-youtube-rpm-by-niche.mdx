TikTok vs YouTube RPM depends entirely on which YouTube format you compare against: YouTube long-form AdSense pays roughly **$2.00 to $4.00 per 1,000 views**, while TikTok's **Creator Rewards Program pays about $0.50 to $2.00 per 1,000 qualified views** for original videos over 60 seconds. For long-form uploads, YouTube wins on ad revenue per view — but TikTok's Creator Rewards actually out-pays **YouTube Shorts ($0.04 to $0.10)** and **Instagram Reels ($0.01 to $0.03)**, and once brand deals, TikTok Shop commissions, and LIVE gifts are layered in, TikTok's effective RPM closes most of the gap in commerce-friendly niches. This platform RPM comparison breaks the numbers down across eight content niches using TikTok's current Creator Rewards rates — not the near-defunct legacy Creator Fund, which paid just $0.02 to $0.05 per 1,000 views — so the figures reflect what creators actually earn in 2026.

Last updated: July 2026 · Figures are estimates based on published RPM ranges and TikTok's official Creator Rewards documentation.

## Quick Comparison

Before going niche by niche, it helps to see where each program sits. The table below compares per-view pay across the major short-form and long-form monetization programs. TikTok publishes no official RPM figures, so its rates are estimates drawn from creator-reported data; YouTube AdSense figures reflect long-form ad revenue only.

| Platform / Program | Format | RPM per 1,000 views | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| YouTube AdSense (long-form) | Videos 60s+, monetized | $2.00 – $4.00 | Highest per-view ad pay; peaks in finance and tech |
| Twitch | Live streams + ads | $2.00 – $5.00 | Includes subscriptions and bits |
| TikTok Creator Rewards | Original videos 60s+ | $0.50 – $2.00 (qualified views) | Current program; 10–40x the legacy Fund |
| YouTube Shorts | Short-form vertical | $0.04 – $0.10 | Shared Shorts ad-revenue pool |
| TikTok Creator Fund (legacy) | Any length | $0.02 – $0.05 | Superseded in most markets |
| Instagram Reels | Short-form vertical | $0.01 – $0.03 | Bonus programs paused in many regions |

The single most important takeaway: **when you compare like-for-like short-form to short-form, TikTok pays more than YouTube.** TikTok Creator Rewards ($0.50–$2.00) beats YouTube Shorts ($0.04–$0.10) by roughly 10x to 50x per qualified view. The "YouTube pays more" narrative is true only for long-form uploads, where AdSense's $2.00–$4.00 RPM outruns everything else on this list. If you are choosing between posting a vertical clip to TikTok or to YouTube Shorts, TikTok is the better-paying home for it.

## Per-Niche RPM Deep Dive

RPM is set by advertiser demand, and advertiser demand varies enormously by audience. A finance viewer is worth far more to advertisers than a dance viewer, and that difference shows up in both platforms' rates. The table below pairs TikTok's Creator Rewards RPM (by niche, for qualified US views) against YouTube's AdSense band for the same content category.

| Niche | TikTok Creator Rewards RPM | YouTube AdSense RPM | Per-view winner |
|---|---|---|---|
| Finance / Investing | $1.50 – $3.00 | $3.50 – $4.00 | YouTube (~1.3–2.7x) |
| Technology | $1.00 – $2.50 | $3.00 – $4.00 | YouTube (~1.5–3x) |
| Education / How-to | $0.80 – $2.00 | $3.00 – $4.00 | YouTube (~2–4x) |
| Beauty / Fashion | $0.60 – $1.50 | $2.50 – $3.50 | YouTube on ads; TikTok Shop can flip it |
| Fitness / Health | $0.60 – $1.50 | $2.50 – $3.00 | YouTube (~2–4x) |
| Food / Lifestyle | $0.50 – $1.20 | $2.00 – $3.00 | YouTube (~2–4x) |
| Entertainment / Gaming | $0.40 – $1.00 | $2.00 – $3.00 | YouTube (~2.5–5x) |
| Comedy | $0.30 – $0.80 | $2.00 – $2.50 | YouTube (~3–6x) |

TikTok's Creator Rewards RPMs above are estimates from creator-reported data — TikTok's [official Creator Rewards documentation](https://support.tiktok.com/en/business-and-creator/creator-rewards-program) confirms the program pays on qualified views but publishes no dollar rates. Independent 2026 analyses corroborate the range: [Miraflow's monetization report](https://miraflow.ai/blog/tiktok-rpm-monetization-2026-how-much-tiktok-pays) puts US Creator Rewards RPM at roughly $0.40–$1.50 and names audience geography as the single biggest RPM factor. YouTube AdSense averages $2.00–$4.00 platform-wide, landing at the top of that band in high-CPM niches like finance and tech and near the floor in comedy and entertainment.

**Finance and investing** is the highest-paying niche on both platforms. YouTube finance creators sit at the very top of the AdSense band (near $4.00 per 1,000 views) because banks, brokerages, and trading apps bid aggressively for that audience. TikTok finance creators earn the highest Creator Rewards RPM on the platform — $1.50 to $3.00 per 1,000 qualified views — and command premium brand-deal rates on top. A mid-tier finance creator (50K–200K followers) can charge $1,000 to $5,000 per sponsored video, which routinely dwarfs their platform RPM income.

**Beauty and skincare** is where TikTok can pull ahead despite a lower ad RPM. YouTube's AdSense sits around $2.50–$3.50, higher than TikTok's $0.60–$1.50 Creator Rewards rate. But product-linked TikTok videos drive direct sales through [TikTok Shop](/learn/tiktok-advertising-rates-benchmarks/), where creators earn a 5% standard affiliate commission (ranging from 2% up to 20% by category). For a beauty creator who converts, Shop commission plus brand deals can push effective earnings per view above YouTube's ad-only figure — one of the few niches where TikTok's total monetization beats YouTube's.

**Gaming** sits in the middle. YouTube gaming AdSense ($2.00–$3.00) is modest by YouTube standards but still comfortably above TikTok's $0.40–$1.00 Creator Rewards rate. What TikTok offers gaming creators is reach: clips frequently go viral and funnel viewers toward a YouTube channel, Twitch stream, or merch store. Many gaming creators treat TikTok as a discovery engine and YouTube or Twitch as the revenue engine.

**Comedy and entertainment** has the lowest RPMs on both platforms because broad entertainment audiences are cheaper for advertisers to reach than niche professional ones. YouTube comedy AdSense ($2.00–$2.50) is low for YouTube but still several times TikTok's $0.30–$0.80. This is the hardest category to monetize through ads alone on either platform, which is why comedy creators lean heavily on brand deals, merch, and live gifting.

**Education and how-to content** earns premium RPMs on YouTube ($3.00–$4.00) because it aligns with high-value advertisers in software, online courses, and professional services. TikTok educational creators earn a solid $0.80–$2.00 Creator Rewards RPM — second only to finance and tech — and their action-oriented audiences make them attractive for brand partnerships in the $1,000–$5,000 range per video for mid-tier accounts.

## The Legacy Creator Fund: Why Old Comparisons Showed a 50–100x Gap

If you have read older "TikTok vs YouTube pay" articles claiming YouTube pays 50 to 100 times more per view, they were comparing YouTube AdSense against TikTok's legacy Creator Fund, not the current program. TikTok's [rewards-calculation model](https://www.tiktok.com/support/faq_detail?id=7543897461245450808) now pays a Standard Reward of qualified views multiplied by an RPM, plus an additional content-quality reward — a very different system from the old fixed-pool Fund.

| Program | RPM per 1,000 views | Vs YouTube AdSense | Requirement |
|---|---|---|---|
| Creator Fund (legacy) | $0.02 – $0.05 | ~50–100x less | 10K followers, any video length |
| Creator Rewards (current) | $0.50 – $2.00 (qualified) | ~2–4x less | 10K followers, 60s+ original video |

The switch to Creator Rewards raised per-view pay by roughly **10 to 40 times** over the legacy Fund. That single change is why the honest 2026 comparison is "YouTube pays about 2 to 4 times more per view for long-form," not the "50 to 100 times more" figure still circulating online. Always confirm which TikTok program a comparison is using before trusting its ratio.

## Worked Examples: 1 Million Views on Each Platform

Every payout on either platform follows the same formula: **views ÷ 1,000 × RPM = earnings.** Here is how 1 million views translates on each program, using blended mid-range RPMs.

- **TikTok Creator Rewards:** 1,000,000 qualified views × $1.00 ≈ **$1,000** (typical range $500–$2,000). A finance creator at $1.50–$3.00 earns **$1,500–$3,000** for the same million.
- **YouTube AdSense (long-form):** 1,000,000 views × $3.00 ≈ **$3,000** (range $2,000–$4,000).
- **YouTube Shorts:** 1,000,000 views × $0.07 ≈ **$70** (range $40–$100).
- **Legacy Creator Fund:** 1,000,000 views × $0.03 ≈ **$30** (range $20–$50).

Line those up and the real story is clear. One million long-form YouTube views ($2,000–$4,000) beats one million TikTok Creator Rewards views ($500–$2,000) by roughly two to four times. But one million TikTok views beats one million **YouTube Shorts** views ($40–$100) by 10 to 50 times. And both current programs bury the legacy Fund's $20–$50. You can model your own niche and view counts in the [RPM calculator](/calculators/rpm/) or check the full [RPM rates by niche](/learn/rpm-rates-by-niche/) breakdown.

Remember that only **qualified views** count toward Creator Rewards: unique For You feed views watched more than five seconds, on original videos of at least 60 seconds. Paid, promoted, repeat, and artificial views are excluded, so your paid RPM is calculated against a smaller number than your public view count.

## Long-Term Income Deep Dive

RPM is a snapshot. Long-term income depends on how that rate compounds with content volume, catalogue value, and audience growth over months and years — and here the two platforms behave very differently.

**YouTube's evergreen advantage.** YouTube videos keep earning for months or years after upload. A well-optimized YouTube video commonly earns three to ten times its first-month revenue across its lifetime through search and suggested traffic. TikTok videos have a much shorter shelf life — most views arrive within the first 48 to 72 hours, with little long-tail traffic afterward.

**TikTok's volume advantage.** Because TikTok videos take far less time to produce, a creator can post daily and accumulate views that a weekly YouTube uploader cannot match. Higher volume at a lower RPM can rival — sometimes beat — lower volume at a higher RPM in any given month.

The illustrative model below assumes a daily TikTok poster (60s+ originals, so roughly half of raw views qualify) and a weekly YouTube uploader, both in a mixed mid-CPM niche. Treat every figure as an estimate.

| Metric | TikTok Creator (daily posting) | YouTube Creator (weekly posting) |
|---|---|---|
| Monthly total views | 5,000,000 | 800,000 |
| Monetized / qualified views | ~2,500,000 | 800,000 |
| Program RPM | $1.00 (Creator Rewards) | $3.00 (AdSense) |
| Monthly platform revenue | ~$2,500 | ~$2,400 |
| Monthly brand-deal revenue | ~$3,000 | ~$2,000 |
| Monthly total | ~$5,500 | ~$4,400 |
| Annual total | ~$66,000 | ~$52,800 |
| Cumulative (3 years, incl. back catalogue) | ~$198,000 | ~$215,000+ |

Notice the crossover. In a single year, the high-volume TikTok creator can out-earn the YouTube creator on current cash flow. Over three years, YouTube's back catalogue keeps generating passive ad revenue while the TikTok catalogue earns almost nothing after its first week, so YouTube's cumulative total pulls ahead. The optimal answer for most creators is not "either/or" — it is TikTok for discovery and fast cash flow, YouTube for compounding long-term revenue, a pattern we detail in our [multi-platform strategy guide](/learn/multi-platform-strategy/).

## What Actually Moves Your RPM

Two creators in the same niche can see very different RPMs. These are the factors that actually explain the difference, in rough order of impact.

- **Audience geography (the biggest lever).** US audiences pay the most. Using TikTok's regional pattern, RPM scales down from a US baseline of 1.0 to roughly 0.9 in the UK, 0.85 in Canada, 0.8 in Australia and New Zealand, 0.75 in the EU, 0.6 across Asia-Pacific, 0.55 in MENA, 0.5 in Latin America, and 0.3 in Sub-Saharan Africa. A creator with a US audience can earn twice what an identical creator with a South American audience earns.
- **Niche and advertiser demand.** As the tables above show, finance and tech RPMs are three to five times entertainment RPMs on both platforms.
- **Watch time and retention.** Qualified views require more than five seconds of watch time, and the Additional Reward rewards completion. Higher retention directly raises paid RPM.
- **Video length.** Creator Rewards only pays on original videos of 60 seconds or longer. Sub-minute clips earn nothing from the program (though they can still fuel reach and brand-deal value).
- **Seasonality.** Advertiser budgets swing 40% to 80% between January (lowest) and Q4 (highest), and brand-deal rates climb 50% to 100% from October through December. See our [seasonal earnings trends](/learn/seasonal-tiktok-earnings-trends/) for the month-by-month pattern.

## Common Mistakes When Comparing TikTok and YouTube RPM

1. **Comparing the legacy Fund to YouTube AdSense.** The $0.02–$0.05 Fund is not current pay. Use Creator Rewards ($0.50–$2.00 qualified) for any 2026 comparison.
2. **Comparing short-form output to long-form YouTube.** If you post vertical clips, the fair comparison is YouTube Shorts ($0.04–$0.10) — which TikTok beats — not long-form AdSense.
3. **Ignoring the qualified-views rules.** Only unique For You feed views over five seconds on 60s+ originals earn. Your paid view count is smaller than your public one.
4. **Treating RPM as total income.** For most mid-tier creators, brand deals and Shop commissions earn more than platform RPM combined. RPM is the floor, not the ceiling.
5. **Applying US RPMs to a non-US audience.** Multiply by the regional factor above before you project earnings.
6. **Forgetting production cost.** YouTube's higher RPM often comes with higher camera, editing, and time costs that shrink the real margin per dollar earned.

## Which Platform Pays Better

The honest answer depends on your niche, format, and time horizon.

**Choose YouTube if** you make long-form or mid-form content in a high-CPM niche (finance, tech, education, business), you value passive back-catalogue income, and you can commit to a consistent schedule for 12 or more months.

**Choose TikTok if** you make short-form content with viral potential, you are in a commerce-friendly niche where Shop commissions supplement RPM (beauty, fashion, home), and you want fast audience growth and quicker first income. TikTok's 10,000-follower, 100,000-view eligibility bar is also easier to clear than YouTube's 1,000 subscribers plus 4,000 watch hours.

**Choose both if** you want maximum total income. Cross-platform creators consistently out-earn single-platform creators at the same audience size. The efficient workflow is to shoot longer content for YouTube and repurpose the strongest moments as TikTok clips — capturing YouTube's high long-form RPM and TikTok's high reach and Creator Rewards pay from the same production effort. ## FAQ

### Is TikTok or YouTube better for earnings per 1,000 views?

For long-form content, YouTube AdSense ($2.00–$4.00 per 1,000 views) pays about two to four times more per view than TikTok Creator Rewards ($0.50–$2.00 per 1,000 qualified views). For short-form content, TikTok wins: Creator Rewards pays 10 to 50 times more than YouTube Shorts ($0.04–$0.10). Once brand deals and TikTok Shop are added, TikTok's total earnings per view can match or beat YouTube in commerce-friendly niches.

### How much does TikTok pay per 1,000 views in 2026?

TikTok's current Creator Rewards Program pays an estimated $0.50 to $2.00 per 1,000 qualified views on original videos of at least 60 seconds, with high-CPM niches like finance reaching $1.50 to $3.00. The legacy Creator Fund paid only $0.02 to $0.05 and has been superseded in most markets. These are creator-reported estimates; TikTok publishes no official RPM.

### Does TikTok pay more than YouTube Shorts?

Yes. TikTok Creator Rewards ($0.50–$2.00 per 1,000 qualified views) pays roughly 10 to 50 times more per view than YouTube Shorts ($0.04–$0.10). If you are deciding where to post a vertical clip, TikTok is the higher-paying option. YouTube only pulls ahead on long-form uploads monetized through AdSense.

### Which niche has the highest RPM on TikTok and YouTube?

Finance and investing pays the most on both platforms. On YouTube, finance AdSense RPM sits near the top of the $2.00–$4.00 band; on TikTok, finance Creator Rewards RPM leads at $1.50–$3.00 per 1,000 qualified views. Technology and education follow closely. Comedy, dance, and general entertainment sit at the bottom on both platforms.

### Do TikTok videos under one minute earn money?

Not through Creator Rewards. The program only pays on original videos that are 60 seconds or longer, so a sub-minute clip earns nothing from the RPM program. Short clips can still build reach, followers, and brand-deal value, but to earn per-view payouts your videos must clear the one-minute threshold and gather qualified For You feed views.

### How much is 1 million views worth on TikTok vs YouTube?

Roughly $500 to $2,000 on TikTok Creator Rewards, versus $2,000 to $4,000 on long-form YouTube AdSense and only $40 to $100 on YouTube Shorts. Finance content pushes the TikTok figure to $1,500–$3,000. Brand deals and Shop commissions are layered on top of all these numbers and often exceed the platform payout itself.
