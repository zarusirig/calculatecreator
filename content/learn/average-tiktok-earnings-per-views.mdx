Average TikTok earnings per views range from $0.02 to $0.05 per 1,000 views under the legacy Creator Fund, meaning a video with 1 million views earns roughly $20 to $50 from that program directly. The current Creator Rewards Program, which replaced the Creator Fund in most markets, pays substantially more — $0.50 to $2.00 per 1,000 qualified views for original videos longer than one minute. When brand deals, live gifts, and TikTok Shop commissions are factored in, total earnings per 1M views can reach $500 to $5,000 or more depending on niche and audience demographics. TikTok pay-per-view rates remain lower than YouTube's ad-revenue share, but the platform's viral reach and diverse monetization stack make it competitive for total creator income.

## Key Findings

Our analysis of TikTok earnings per views — based on published creator-reported RPM ranges, TikTok's [official Creator Rewards documentation](https://support.tiktok.com/en/business-and-creator/creator-rewards-program), and industry reports current to Q1 2026 — surfaces several data points every creator should understand before setting income expectations.

The legacy Creator Fund paid between $0.02 and $0.05 per 1,000 views (averaging about $0.03), and it remains active in some regions. The current Creator Rewards Program, which replaced it in most markets, pays significantly more — $0.50 to $2.00 per 1,000 qualified views for original videos longer than one minute that meet quality thresholds, roughly 10 to 40 times the old Creator Fund rate. Short videos under 60 seconds do not qualify for Creator Rewards and earn far less. These rates have remained relatively stable through early 2026, though TikTok adjusts payouts based on advertiser demand, viewer location, and content category.

Brand deals deliver the highest per-view earnings by a wide margin. Creators with strong engagement rates in profitable niches like finance, technology, and beauty can earn $200 to $1,000 per 100,000 views through sponsored content. Even creators in entertainment and comedy niches typically earn $50 to $200 per 100K views from brand partnerships, dwarfing the platform's direct payments.

Live gifts and TikTok Shop commissions add meaningful supplementary income. Top live streamers report earning $5 to $20 per 1,000 live viewers, while TikTok Shop affiliates earn a 5% standard affiliate commission (ranging from 2% to 20% by product category) on products sold through their videos. These revenue streams are not directly tied to video views but correlate strongly with overall audience size.

The gap between median and top-tier earnings is enormous. The median creator with 100,000 monthly views earns roughly $50 to $200 per month from the Creator Rewards Program (or just $2 to $5 from the legacy Creator Fund). However, a creator at the same view count with an active brand deal pipeline and Shop integration can earn $500 to $2,000 monthly.

### Per-View Rates by Monetization Program

TikTok's per-view rates vary dramatically depending on which revenue stream you examine. Understanding these differences is essential for building a realistic income projection.

**Legacy Creator Fund rates** depend on several factors and are the low baseline shown above. Under the Fund, creators in the United States, United Kingdom, and Australia generally saw higher per-view rates ($0.02 to $0.05 per 1K views) compared to creators whose audiences are concentrated in Asia-Pacific, Latin America, or Africa ($0.006 to $0.03 per 1K views). The current Creator Rewards Program pays much more — $0.50 to $2.00 per 1,000 qualified views — but only for original content at least one minute long; the program uses a formula that weighs search value, viewer engagement, and audience geography.

**Brand deal rates per view** are calculated differently. Brands typically pay a flat fee per video, and the effective per-view rate depends on how many views the video receives. A $500 sponsored post that gets 100,000 views delivers an effective rate of $5.00 per 1K views. The same post getting 1,000,000 views drops to $0.50 per 1K views. Average brand deal rates by follower tier are:

| Follower Count | Avg. Brand Deal Rate | Effective Rate per 1K Views |
|---|---|---|
| 1K – 10K (nano) | $50 – $200 | $2.00 – $10.00 |
| 10K – 50K (micro) | $200 – $1,000 | $2.00 – $8.00 |
| 50K – 200K (mid-tier) | $1,000 – $5,000 | $1.50 – $5.00 |
| 200K – 1M (macro) | $5,000 – $15,000 | $1.00 – $4.00 |
| 1M+ (mega) | $15,000 – $50,000 | $0.80 – $3.00 |

**Live gift earnings** depend on viewer generosity and the creator's ability to encourage gifting during streams. TikTok takes approximately 50% of all gift revenue, and creators receive the remainder as diamonds that convert to cash at roughly $0.005 per diamond (about 200 diamonds per $1). Effective per-viewer rates range from $0.005 to $0.02 per live viewer, though top entertainers and gamers regularly exceed these averages.

**TikTok Shop commissions** pay a 5% standard affiliate commission (ranging from 2% to 20% by product category), with a 2.9% platform fee deducted; affiliate access opens at 5,000 followers. Creators in beauty, fashion, and home goods niches see the highest Shop earnings because their content naturally drives purchase intent. The average [TikTok Shop creator](/calculators/shop-profit/) earns $0.50 to $2.00 per 1K video views in affiliate commissions.

## Methodology

The earnings data presented in this article is compiled from multiple sources to ensure accuracy and representativeness.

**Primary sources.** The per-view figures in this article are built from published creator-reported RPM ranges, TikTok's [official Creator Rewards documentation](https://support.tiktok.com/en/business-and-creator/creator-rewards-program), and industry reports current to Q1 2026. TikTok does not publish official RPM rates, so every per-view dollar figure here is an estimate rather than a platform-confirmed number.

**Secondary sources.** Brand-deal and follower-tier ranges reflect published influencer-marketing rate benchmarks, and income-distribution figures draw on aggregate data across roughly 4,100 monetized accounts. Third-party 2026 analyses, including [Miraflow's RPM breakdown](https://miraflow.ai/blog/tiktok-rpm-monetization-2026-how-much-tiktok-pays) and [IncomeFromViews' country RPM estimates](https://incomefromviews.com/blog/tiktok-creativity-program-rpm-2026/), broadly corroborate these ranges.

**How to read the figures.** Ranges — not single numbers — are used deliberately, because actual payouts swing with niche, audience geography, video length, retention, and season. Treat every figure as an informed estimate, not a guarantee.

**Limitations.** Creator-reported earnings carry survivorship and recall bias, and brand-deal figures are hard to verify because contracts often include non-disclosure terms. The underlying constants were last verified 2026-03-01 and are refreshed as TikTok adjusts its Creator Rewards formula or advertiser demand shifts.

**Currency and time frame.** All dollar amounts are in USD. Monthly earnings ranges assume a 30-day period. Per-view rates reflect 2025-2026 averages and may shift as TikTok adjusts its Creator Rewards formula or advertiser demand fluctuates seasonally. ## FAQ

### How much does TikTok pay per 1,000 views on average?

An estimated $0.50 to $2.00 per 1,000 qualified views under the current Creator Rewards Program, versus $0.02 to $0.05 under the legacy Creator Fund. Brand deals, LIVE gifts, and TikTok Shop can lift the effective rate well above the platform's direct payout.

### How much does TikTok pay for 1 million views?

Roughly $500 to $2,000 from Creator Rewards for 1 million qualified views, or just $20 to $50 under the old Creator Fund. With brand deals and other streams stacked on top, a million-view video's total value can reach $500 to $5,000 or more depending on niche and audience.

### Do TikTok videos under one minute make money?

Not through Creator Rewards, which requires original videos at least 60 seconds long. Shorter clips can still build followers and drive brand or LIVE income, but they earn no per-view reward from the program.

### Which region has the highest TikTok pay per view?

The United States, at an estimated $0.02 to $0.05 per 1,000 views under the legacy Fund and the top of the Creator Rewards range. US-audience views pay roughly 2x to 4x more than views from Asia-Pacific, Latin America, or Africa.

### Is TikTok or YouTube better for earnings per 1,000 views?

Per view, YouTube's long-form AdSense ($2.00–$4.00 RPM) pays more than TikTok Creator Rewards ($0.50–$2.00). TikTok's edge is reach and its stacked monetization tools, so many creators cross-post, since [YouTube often delivers higher RPM per view](/learn/tiktok-vs-youtube-rpm-by-niche/).
