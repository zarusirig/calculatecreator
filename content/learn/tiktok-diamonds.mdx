TikTok diamonds are the creator-side currency you earn when viewers send you gifts during a LIVE stream. Viewers buy coins with real money, spend those coins on gifts, and TikTok converts part of each gift's value into diamonds in your creator balance. Once that balance clears the minimum, you withdraw the diamonds as cash.

This guide covers what a diamond is worth, how to cash out, the minimum thresholds, the payout methods available, the fees that quietly reduce your take-home pay, and the tax rules that apply once the money reaches you.

Last updated: July 2026 · Figures reflect commonly observed conversion rates, not official TikTok-published numbers.

## What a TikTok diamond is worth

Each diamond is commonly valued at about **$0.005**, which is the same as **200 diamonds = $1**. TikTok does not publish an official rate — its [LIVE Gifts documentation](https://support.tiktok.com/en/live-gifts-wallet/tiktok-live/live-gifts-on-tiktok) states only that Diamonds are "awarded based on popularity" — so treat $0.005 per diamond as a widely reported working estimate rather than a guaranteed figure.

| Diamonds | Approximate cash value |
| --- | --- |
| 200 | $1.00 |
| 2,000 | $10.00 |
| 10,000 | $50.00 |
| 25,000 | $125.00 |

Because a viewer's coins cost about $0.0106 each and each resulting diamond is worth roughly half that, creators net close to **50% of what a viewer spends** on a gift — the other half is TikTok's cut. ## Identity verification for your first withdrawal

Your first withdrawal may require identity verification. You will need to submit a government-issued ID (driver's license or passport) and possibly a selfie. This typically takes 1-3 business days, and subsequent withdrawals are faster once your identity is on file.

## Minimum cash-out thresholds

Minimum withdrawal thresholds vary by account tier and region. Most creators start with a $1 minimum, which can rise to $10-$50 as you earn more. Daily and weekly limits help prevent fraud and keep the payout system secure.

| Tier | Minimum diamonds | USD equivalent | Typical user |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| New creators | 200 | $1.00 | Just getting started |
| Regular streamers | 2,000 | $10.00 | Streaming consistently |
| Some regions | 4,000 | $20.00 | Region-dependent floor |
| Higher-floor accounts | 10,000 | $50.00 | Certain markets |

Diamonds below your threshold carry forward indefinitely — they do not expire. Keep earning until you reach your account's minimum. Typical account limits look like this:

- **Daily limit:** around $1,000 per 24-hour period
- **Weekly limit:** up to about $7,000 on some accounts
- **Monthly limit:** varies by account standing

## Payout methods

TikTok offers several payout methods with different processing times and fees. Choose based on availability in your region, processing speed, and fee tolerance. All withdrawals are governed by [TikTok's Virtual Items Policy](https://www.tiktok.com/legal/virtual-items).

| Method | Processing time | Fees | Availability |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| PayPal | 24-48 hours | 0-2% | Global |
| Bank transfer | 2-5 business days | $0-$5 | Most countries |
| Payoneer | 2-5 business days | 1-2% | Select regions |

- **PayPal** is the most popular option — fast, reliable, and available in most countries.
- **Bank transfer / direct deposit** suits larger withdrawals, where a flat fee beats a percentage cut.
- **Payoneer** can be cheaper than PayPal for some international creators.
- **Digital wallets** such as GCash and OVO are available in specific countries.

Set up your payout method before you need it so your first cash-out is not delayed, and double-check the details to avoid rejected transactions. TikTok's overall processing window commonly runs **5 to 15 business days** (occasionally up to 21), separate from the payment provider's own timing.

## Fees and deductions

Understanding the full fee stack helps you calculate real take-home pay. TikTok's roughly 50% cut is only the first layer.

- **TikTok platform cut:** approximately 50% of a gift's coin value before conversion to diamonds
- **Payment processor fees:** 2-3% (PayPal, bank transfer, or other method)
- **Currency conversion fees:** 1-3% for withdrawals in non-USD currencies

### Worked example: Universe gift

- A viewer sends a Universe gift: **34,999 coins**
- Coin cost to the viewer: about **$370.99**
- Creator value after TikTok's ~50% cut: about **$185.50**
- PayPal fee at 2%: about **-$3.71**
- Creator nets: about **$181.79**

That is roughly **49%** of what the viewer originally paid ($181.79 ÷ $370.99). Once you fold in payment-processor and currency fees, creators typically keep about **47 to 49 cents** for every dollar a viewer spends on gift coins. Knowing this helps you set realistic income expectations and price any exclusive perks accordingly.

## Tax implications

Diamond income is taxable as self-employment income in most countries. In the US, TikTok's payout partner issues a 1099-NEC form if your annual earnings cross the reporting threshold, and you are responsible for income tax plus self-employment tax.

**US obligations:**

- Income tax based on your bracket (10-37%)
- Self-employment tax of 15.3% (Social Security + Medicare)
- State and local tax where applicable
- Quarterly estimated payments if you expect to owe $1,000 or more

**Commonly deductible expenses:**

- Equipment (camera, lighting, microphone)
- Internet and phone service
- Home-office space
- Software and apps
- Professional services (accountant, lawyer)

Keep records of every withdrawal (TikTok statements, PayPal receipts), track monthly earnings even below the reporting threshold, and retain records for at least three years. A practical habit is to set aside 25-30% of diamond earnings for taxes in a separate account. This is general information, not tax advice — confirm the rules for your country with a qualified professional, and see the [creator tax strategy guide](/learn/creator-tax-strategy/) for a deeper walkthrough.

## Troubleshooting cash-out issues

If a withdrawal fails or your balance looks wrong, contact support through the app:

1. Open TikTok, then go to Profile and the menu
2. Open Settings and Privacy, then Report a Problem
3. Choose Payment Issues
4. Describe the issue in as much detail as possible
5. Attach screenshots of any error message or your balance screen
6. Allow 1-3 business days for a support response

A negative diamond balance usually means a viewer's coin purchase was charged back after you already received the gift. You will need to earn enough new diamonds to offset the negative amount before you can withdraw again. Contact TikTok support if you believe the deduction is an error.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much is 1 TikTok diamond worth?

One TikTok diamond is commonly valued at about $0.005, or 200 diamonds per dollar. TikTok does not publish an official conversion rate, but $0.005 per diamond is the figure creators and calculators consistently rely on, and it does not change with gift type or creator size.

### How do I cash out TikTok diamonds?

Open TikTok, go to Profile, then TikTok Studio, Balance, and Withdraw. Choose an amount that meets your minimum threshold, select a payment method (PayPal, bank transfer, and so on), confirm, and wait for processing — typically 5 to 15 business days (occasionally up to 21). Your first withdrawal may require identity verification.

### What is the minimum to cash out TikTok diamonds?

The minimum varies by account tier and region: as low as $1 (200 diamonds) for new creators, $10 (2,000 diamonds) for regular streamers, and $20-$50 in some regions or for higher-floor accounts. Check your specific minimum in TikTok Studio under Balance. Diamonds below the minimum carry forward indefinitely.

### How long does a TikTok diamond withdrawal take?

TikTok's processing window commonly runs 5 to 15 business days, and can stretch to about 21. PayPal payouts tend to arrive fastest (24-48 hours after processing), while bank transfers take 2-5 business days. First-time withdrawals may take longer because of identity verification (1-3 additional days).

### Does TikTok charge withdrawal fees?

TikTok itself does not charge a withdrawal fee. Payment processors do: PayPal charges 0-2%, bank transfers $0-$5, and Payoneer 1-2%. Currency conversion adds another 1-3% for non-USD withdrawals, so total fees usually land around 2-3% of the amount you withdraw.

### What percentage does TikTok take from diamonds?

TikTok keeps approximately 50% of a gift's coin value when it converts to diamonds. When a viewer sends a gift, TikTok retains about half as its platform cut, and the remaining ~50% becomes diamonds for the creator. This split applies broadly regardless of creator size.

### Why is my diamond balance negative?

A negative balance usually happens when a viewer's coin purchase is charged back after you already received diamonds from their gift — often when the viewer disputes the charge with their bank or card issuer. You will need to earn additional diamonds to offset the negative amount before withdrawing again. Contact TikTok support if you believe it is an error.

### Do I have to pay taxes on diamond earnings?

Yes. TikTok diamond earnings are taxable as self-employment income. In the US, TikTok issues a 1099-NEC if you earn $600 or more per year, and you owe income tax based on your bracket plus 15.3% self-employment tax. Set aside 25-30% of earnings for taxes and make quarterly estimated payments if you expect to owe $1,000 or more.

### Can I convert diamonds back to coins?

No. Diamonds are a one-way currency that can only be converted to real money through the cash-out process. You cannot convert diamonds back to coins, send them to other users, or use them to buy gifts — they exist solely as a record of your withdrawal-eligible earnings.

### What is the daily withdrawal limit for TikTok diamonds?

The daily limit is about $1,000 (200,000 diamonds) per 24-hour period. Some accounts also have a weekly limit near $7,000 and monthly limits that vary by account standing. If you hit the daily limit, wait 24 hours before trying again; high-earning creators can request higher limits through TikTok support.
