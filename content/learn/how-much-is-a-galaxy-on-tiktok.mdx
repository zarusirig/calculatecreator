**A Galaxy on TikTok costs 1,000 coins — about $10.60 for the viewer to send, and roughly $5.30 in earnings for the creator.** The gap between those two numbers is TikTok's platform cut: viewers buy coins at full price, but creators receive about half of that value after coins are converted to diamonds and cashed out. That ~$5.30 payout is the same whether the gift comes from an iPhone or an Android phone — what actually shifts the price is *where* the viewer buys their coins, since coins bought on TikTok's website skip the app-store fee and cost slightly less than coins bought inside the app.

## What Is a Galaxy on TikTok?

A Galaxy is one of TikTok's mid-to-high tier LIVE gifts — an animated purple gift a viewer sends during a live stream to support a creator. It sits well above everyday gifts like the Rose (1 coin) or Hand Hearts (100 coins), but far below the top-tier spectacle gifts such as the Lion or the Universe. At 1,000 coins, the Galaxy is a common "big thank-you" gift: expensive enough to stand out in the stream, but affordable enough that regular viewers actually send it.

Only creators who can go LIVE receive Galaxies in the first place. Starting a live stream requires **1,000 followers**, streamers must be at least 16 years old, and only creators aged 18 or older can receive gifts — thresholds laid out in Darkroom's [2026 TikTok creator policy guide](https://www.darkroomagency.com/observatory/what-brands-need-to-know-about-tiktok-new-rules-2026).

Like every TikTok gift, the Galaxy is priced in **coins** — the virtual currency viewers buy with real money. When a viewer taps to send a Galaxy, 1,000 coins leave their balance and the creator is credited on the payout side in **diamonds**. Understanding that two-currency system is the key to reading its real value — the [TikTok gifts guide](/learn/tiktok-gifts/) walks through the full coins-to-diamonds-to-cash pipeline that every gift follows.

## How the Galaxy's Value Is Calculated

The Galaxy's value depends on which side of the transaction you are on, because a gift passes through three currencies before it becomes cash.

### Coins to diamonds to dollars

- **Coins** are what the viewer buys. TikTok sells coins in bundles at roughly **$0.0106 per coin**, so 1,000 coins cost the viewer approximately **$10.60**.
- **Diamonds** are what the creator earns. TikTok credits creators in diamonds worth about **half** the coins spent, keeping the rest as its platform cut.
- **Dollars** are what the creator withdraws. Once a diamond balance clears TikTok's withdrawal minimum, diamonds convert to cash through PayPal or a linked payout method at roughly **$0.0053 per coin** gifted — about **$5.30** for a single Galaxy.

## Viewer Cost vs Creator Payout

The single most misunderstood fact about the Galaxy is that its cost and its payout are not the same number.

| Perspective | Amount (≈USD) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Viewer pays (in-app coins) | ~$10.60 | 1,000 coins at ~$0.0106 each |
| Viewer pays (web coins) | ~$10.50 | 1,000 coins at ~$0.0105 — no app-store fee |
| Creator earns | ~$5.30 | ~50% of coin value, any device |

So when a viewer sends you a Galaxy, they spent about $10.60, but you take home about $5.30. TikTok retains the difference. This is why headline gift prices always overstate creator income — the platform cut is baked into the diamond conversion before you ever see the money. The Galaxy is a clean anchor to memorize: **1,000 coins ≈ $5.30 in creator earnings**.

## In-App vs Web Coins: Where the Price Really Changes

The device your viewer streams from does *not* change what you earn. A 1,000-coin Galaxy converts to the same diamond balance and the same **~$5.30 payout** whether it came from an iPhone or an Android phone. That is a common misconception worth clearing up: Apple's App Store fee and Google Play's fee raise the price a viewer pays for coins bought inside the app, but they do not shrink the diamonds a creator receives.

What actually moves the number is *where* the viewer buys coins. Coins purchased inside the TikTok app cost about **$0.0106 each**, while coins bought through TikTok's website run closer to **$0.0105 each** because they skip the app-store fee. The gap is small — a few cents on a 1,000-coin Galaxy — but it means viewers who top up on desktop stretch their money slightly further. Either way, the creator still nets roughly $5.30, so build your LIVE revenue targets around that ~50% payout rather than the sticker price your viewers see.
