TikTok monetization by country varies dramatically: audience in the United States earns creators the most through the Creator Rewards Program, at roughly **$1.00-$2.00 per 1,000 qualified views**, while views from India or the Philippines pay as little as **$0.02 per 1,000**. That is a gap of up to 50x for the exact same content. The single most important rule to understand is that **Creator Rewards RPM is set by where your viewers are located, not where you live** — a creator in Manila whose audience is mostly American is paid at US rates, and a creator in New York with a mostly Indonesian audience is paid at Indonesian rates.

Understanding where TikTok pays best, and which monetization programs are available in your market, is essential for setting realistic income expectations. This guide breaks down Creator Rewards RPM by audience country, the legacy Creator Fund, TikTok Shop availability, and the strategies creators in lower-paying markets use to close the gap.

Last updated: July 2026 · Program named "Creator Rewards Program" (formerly the Creativity Program Beta, which replaced the original Creator Fund). Figures are estimates based on this site's canonical RPM data and TikTok's official documentation.

## Key Findings

- **The United States pays the most**, at $1.00-$2.00 per 1,000 qualified views, followed by the UK ($0.80-$1.50), Canada ($0.70-$1.30), Australia ($0.70-$1.20), and Germany ($0.60-$1.10).
- **Audience location, not creator location, sets the rate.** RPM tracks where viewers are, so building an audience in high-CPM markets matters more than moving countries.
- **Tier 1 audiences pay 10-50x more per view** than emerging markets. A million qualified views from a US audience is worth $1,000-$2,000; the same million from India is worth $20-$100.
- **The Creator Rewards Program has replaced the legacy Creator Fund** in most markets. As of early 2026 it is available in roughly 30 countries, up from the original handful of Creator Fund markets. TikTok does not publish a definitive country list, so treat availability as approximate.
- **TikTok Shop reshapes the math** where it is live. In Southeast Asia, where Creator Rewards RPMs are among the lowest, Shop commissions frequently become a creator's primary income.
- **Currency and purchasing power matter.** A $200/month payout that barely dents rent in New York is a meaningful income supplement in the Philippines or Nigeria.

## TikTok RPM by Country — Primary Data

RPM (revenue per mille) is the amount a creator earns per 1,000 *qualified* views through the Creator Rewards Program. Qualified views are unique For You feed views and exclude views under 5 seconds, repeat views, and paid or artificial views — so your qualified-view count is always lower than your raw view count. The figures below reflect this site's canonical RPM ranges by audience country.

### Tier 1 Countries: Highest-Paying Markets

Tier 1 countries share high GDP per capita, mature digital-advertising markets, and strong advertiser competition on TikTok. Creators serving these audiences benefit from the premium CPMs advertisers pay to reach them.

| Audience Country | Creator Rewards RPM (per 1K qualified views) | TikTok Shop | Micro-tier brand deal (10K-50K) |
|---|---|---|---|
| United States | $1.00 - $2.00 | Yes | $200 - $1,000/post |
| United Kingdom | $0.80 - $1.50 | Yes | $200 - $800/post |
| Canada | $0.70 - $1.30 | Yes | $180 - $700/post |
| Australia | $0.70 - $1.20 | Yes | $180 - $700/post |
| Germany | $0.60 - $1.10 | Limited | $150 - $600/post |

US audiences rank first on RPM because American advertisers spend more per impression than advertisers in any other market. The UK, Canada, Australia, and Germany follow. Brand-deal figures use the canonical micro-tier range ($200-$1,000 per in-feed post), scaled down modestly for markets with lower advertiser CPMs.

### Tier 2 and Tier 3 Countries: Emerging Markets

Emerging markets have growing digital-advertising ecosystems but far lower CPMs. Creators here often compensate with TikTok Shop, cross-border brand deals, or [diversified income streams](/learn/diversify-tiktok-income-streams/).

The takeaway is stark: an Indonesian or Filipino audience pays 20-50x less per view than a US audience through Creator Rewards alone. That is precisely why creators in these markets lean so heavily on TikTok Shop, where commissions are tied to sales value rather than ad CPMs.

## The Legacy Creator Fund vs. Creator Rewards

Before the Creator Rewards Program, TikTok paid creators through the **Creator Fund**, which paid only **$0.02-$0.05 per 1,000 views** (an average of about $0.03, or $20-$50 per million views). The Creator Rewards Program pays 10-40x more — $0.50-$2.00 per 1,000 qualified views — which is why TikTok replaced the Fund in most markets.

The Creator Fund is **legacy, not fully shut down**: it launched in the US, UK, Germany, France, Spain, and Italy, and still operates in some regions where Creator Rewards has not rolled out. It carries a $10 minimum payout, paid monthly via PayPal or bank transfer. If you are choosing between them, Creator Rewards wins on rate wherever it is available. For the historical detail, see the [Creator Fund rates by country data](/learn/creator-fund-rates-by-country/) and TikTok's own [Creator Fund documentation](https://support.tiktok.com/en/business-and-creator/tiktok-creator-fund-us).

## Why Country Differences Exist

TikTok's per-view payouts are fundamentally tied to advertising revenue. The platform earns from advertisers and passes a share to creators, so RPM tracks what advertisers are willing to spend to reach a given audience. Where consumers have more purchasing power, advertisers bid higher CPMs, and creators receive higher RPMs.

That advertising hierarchy roughly follows GDP per capita, adjusted for market maturity and advertiser competition — US and Western European CPMs run several times higher than those in Southeast Asia. Because Creator Rewards pays on *audience* location, the country gap is really a reflection of what advertisers pay per impression in each market, not where the creator happens to sit.

## Strategies for Creators in Lower-Paying Countries

Creators in emerging markets can narrow the earnings gap several ways:

**Build an audience in high-CPM markets.** Since RPM follows viewers, English-language content that attracts US and UK audiences earns those higher rates regardless of where you film. The [TikTok algorithm guide](/learn/tiktok-algorithm-explained/) explains how the recommendation system distributes content across borders.

**Prioritize brand deals over platform payouts.** Creator Rewards RPM is fixed by audience geography, but brand-deal rates are negotiable. A Brazilian creator whose audience overlaps a US brand's target demographic can command rates closer to US benchmarks.

**Lean on TikTok Shop where available.** In Southeast Asia, TikTok Shop is heavily promoted, and commissions are tied to sales value, not ad CPMs. The standard affiliate commission is 5% (ranging 2-20% by category), with a 2.9% platform fee and biweekly payments; Shop affiliate access requires 5,000 followers. For many Indonesian and Filipino creators, Shop earnings exceed their Creator Rewards income several times over.

**Sell digital products in USD.** A course or template priced in dollars and sold through an international processor gives creators in any country access to global purchasing power. A creator in Nigeria selling a $47 product to a US buyer earns the same $47 as a US-based creator. See the [digital product selling guide](/learn/selling-digital-products-tiktok/) for setup.

## Getting Paid Across Borders

Two creators earning the identical gross RPM can take home very different net amounts once cross-border payments are settled. TikTok pays out in a limited set of currencies, and creators outside those regions absorb currency-conversion spreads, intermediary bank fees, and — for many US-audience payouts — withholding tax if the correct tax forms are not on file. A creator in a lower-cost market who earns US-audience RPMs can keep a large share of that income in local purchasing-power terms, but only after accounting for these deductions.

Before you estimate take-home pay, confirm which currency TikTok deposits in for your region, whether your payment provider (PayPal, bank transfer, or a local partner) adds conversion fees, and whether tax treaties reduce withholding on income from foreign platforms. ## Common Mistakes When Reading Country Rates

- **Assuming your passport sets your rate.** It does not — your audience does. Track your viewer geography in TikTok Analytics before you estimate RPM.
- **Confusing Creator Fund and Creator Rewards numbers.** The Fund's $0.02-$0.05 rates and the Rewards Program's $0.50-$2.00 rates are two different programs at very different scales. Always check which one a figure refers to.
- **Using raw views instead of qualified views.** Sub-5-second, repeat, and non-For-You views do not earn. Your qualified-view count — and therefore your payout — is lower than your total views.
- **Ignoring TikTok Shop in emerging markets.** In Southeast Asia, judging earning potential by Creator Rewards RPM alone badly understates the real opportunity.

## FAQ

### Which country pays TikTok creators the most?

The United States, at roughly $1.00-$2.00 per 1,000 qualified views through the Creator Rewards Program. The UK ($0.80-$1.50), Canada ($0.70-$1.30), Australia ($0.70-$1.20), and Germany ($0.60-$1.10) round out the top tier. Because RPM follows audience location, the "best country" is really the best *audience* to have.

### Does TikTok pay based on where I live or where my viewers are?

Where your viewers are. Creator Rewards RPM is set by audience location, so a creator anywhere in the world earns US-level rates on views from a US audience, and low emerging-market rates on views from those regions.

### How much money do you get for 1 million views on TikTok?

Through the Creator Rewards Program it depends on audience country: about $1,000-$2,000 for a US audience, $150-$400 for a Brazilian audience, and $20-$100 for an Indian audience — all on qualified views, which are lower than raw views.

### Which countries can join the TikTok Creator Rewards Program?

As of early 2026 the program is available in roughly 30 countries, including the US, UK, Germany, France, Canada, Australia, Brazil, Japan, South Korea, Italy, and Spain. TikTok does not publish a definitive list, so confirm current availability on the official Creator Rewards page.

### What is the difference between the Creator Fund and the Creator Rewards Program?

The legacy Creator Fund paid $0.02-$0.05 per 1,000 views (about $20-$50 per million). The current Creator Rewards Program pays $0.50-$2.00 per 1,000 qualified views — 10-40x more — and has replaced the Fund in most markets, though the Fund still operates in some regions.
