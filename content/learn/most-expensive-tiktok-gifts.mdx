The most expensive TikTok gift is the **TikTok Universe** at 34,999 coins, which costs the sender **$370.99** at in-app prices. The second most expensive is the **Lion** at 29,999 coins (**$317.99**). Creators do not keep the full sticker price — at the benchmark most calculators use, a creator receives roughly **50% of a gift's coin value**, so a Universe is worth about **$185.50** to the recipient and a Lion about **$159.00**. This page ranks TikTok's premium gifts by real dollar value, shows what the sender pays versus what the creator actually earns, and explains the math behind the split.

Last verified: July 2026 · USD figures use TikTok's in-app coin rate of about $0.0106 per coin. TikTok publishes no official coin- or diamond-to-dollar rate, so treat all payout figures as commonly-reported estimates.

## Key Findings

- The **TikTok Universe** (34,999 coins) is the single most expensive gift — **$370.99** at in-app prices, with the creator receiving about **$185.50**.
- The **Lion** (29,999 coins, **$317.99**) is the second most expensive and the most-sent "mega gift" during competitive LIVEs; the creator keeps about **$159.00**.
- Creators receive roughly **50% of a gift's value** at the benchmark used here. TikTok itself only states that Diamonds are ["awarded based on popularity"](https://support.tiktok.com/en/live-gifts-wallet/tiktok-live/live-gifts-on-tiktok) and publishes no fixed rate.
- Coins cost about **$0.0106 each** at in-app prices (65 coins for $0.69); the largest single package is **6,607 coins for $69.99**.
- Buying coins on **tiktok.com (web/desktop)** is the cheaper channel because it avoids the ~30% App Store / Google Play fee — about **$0.0105 per coin** versus $0.0106 in-app.
- You must be **18+ (19 in South Korea)** to send or receive gifts, and government or politician accounts cannot collect Diamonds.

## How a Gift's Coin Cost Becomes Creator Cash

Understanding what a gift is "really worth" means following the full value chain from the viewer's wallet to the creator's payout.

| Step | What Happens | Value Retained |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Viewer buys coins | Viewer pays real money for TikTok coins (~$0.0106 each in-app) | 100% |
| 2. Viewer sends gift | Coins are spent and convert into a gift animation | 100% (coins spent) |
| 3. Creator earns Diamonds | The gift converts to [Diamonds](https://support.tiktok.com/en/business-and-creator/video-gifts-on-tiktok/diamonds), the creator-side reward unit | ~50% |
| 4. Creator withdraws cash | Diamonds are cashed out to PayPal or a bank | ~50% of original |

At the benchmark used across this site, the creator ends up with roughly **half of the coin value** in cash. So a gift that costs a viewer $10.60 (a Gold Mine) is worth about $5.30 to the creator. The exact rate is not something TikTok publishes — the official LIVE Gifts page only says Diamonds are "awarded based on popularity" — so this 50% figure is a widely-reported working estimate, not a guaranteed rate. Note also that coins and gifts are generally **non-refundable** once purchased, under TikTok's [Virtual Items Policy](https://www.tiktok.com/legal/virtual-items).

## Web vs In-App: The Cheaper Way to Send Gifts

Where you buy coins changes what an expensive gift costs the sender. Buying on **tiktok.com** from a desktop or mobile browser is the cheaper channel because it avoids the roughly 30% App Store and Google Play fee baked into in-app purchases. In the site's reference data, web coins run about **$0.0105 per coin** versus **$0.0106 in-app** — a modest per-coin difference, but on a 34,999-coin Universe it still trims the sender's cost from about $370.99 to roughly **$367**. ## Common Mistakes About TikTok Gift Values

- **Assuming the creator pockets the sticker price.** A Universe costs $370.99 but pays the creator about $185.50 — plan around what is kept, not what is spent.
- **Confusing coins with Diamonds.** Coins are what viewers buy and spend; Diamonds are what creators earn and withdraw. They are not the same unit.
- **Treating any conversion rate as "official."** TikTok publishes no coin- or diamond-to-dollar rate. Every figure here is a commonly-reported estimate.
- **Quoting a 16,500-coin maximum.** That is a stale figure. The largest single coin package is **6,607 coins for $69.99**; sending a Universe simply requires buying enough packages.
- **Expecting a refund.** Coins and gifts are generally non-refundable under TikTok's Virtual Items Policy.
- **Thinking minors can gift.** You must be **18+ (19 in South Korea)** to send or receive gifts.

### How much is the TikTok Universe gift worth to the creator?
