TikTok does not publish a single global coin price — what you pay depends on your country's currency, local taxes, and whether you buy inside the app or on the web. As a reliable anchor, coins cost about **$0.0106 each at in-app US prices** (65 coins for $0.69, up to a maximum single package of 6,607 coins for $69.99), and buying on **tiktok.com** is consistently the cheapest channel because it avoids the roughly 30% App Store and Google Play fee. This guide explains exactly why coin prices differ between countries, how they compare, and how to find your own exact local price.

## Key Findings

- TikTok publishes **no official global price list** — coin prices are set per region and change with currency and app-store policies.
- The **US in-app anchor** is about **$0.0106 per coin**: 65 coins for $0.69, scaling up to 6,607 coins for $69.99.
- **Buying on the web (tiktok.com) is the cheapest reliable method** in any country because it sidesteps the ~30% Apple/Google in-app fee — roughly **$0.0105 per coin** versus $0.0106 in-app.
- Country-to-country differences come from **four drivers**: currency conversion, VAT/sales tax treatment, app-store fees, and TikTok's regional (purchasing-power) pricing.
- Emerging markets with weaker currencies **tend to** show lower USD-equivalent prices, but you generally cannot buy from another region without that region's payment method and app-store account.
- The only figure you can fully trust is the one **displayed in your own app or web checkout** — everything else is an estimate.

## Why TikTok Coin Prices Differ Between Countries

Four factors explain almost every difference you will see between regions:

1. **Currency conversion.** TikTok prices coin packages in local currency. As exchange rates move, the USD-equivalent of a €1.09 or ₹89 package drifts up or down even when TikTok changes nothing.
2. **VAT and sales tax treatment.** In the EU, UK and Australia, tax is typically built into the displayed price. In the US and Canada, tax is usually added at checkout, so the sticker price looks lower than what you actually pay.
3. **App-store fees.** Apple and Google take a cut (commonly around 30%) of in-app purchases, and how that flows into the price varies by store and region. This is the single biggest reason in-app prices run higher than web prices.
4. **Regional (purchasing-power) pricing.** TikTok, like most app publishers, sets lower nominal prices in markets with lower average incomes, so the same package can cost less in USD terms in some emerging markets.

Because all four move independently — and because TikTok also A/B-tests price points — there is no stable, verifiable "price per country" table that stays accurate for long. That is why the comparison below is framed as tendencies rather than fixed prices.

## The Cheapest Way to Buy Coins in Any Country: Web vs In-App

Regardless of where you live, buying coins on **tiktok.com** from a desktop or mobile browser is the cheapest reliable method, because it avoids the app-store fee that inflates in-app prices. In the site's reference data, web coins run about **$0.0105 per coin** versus **$0.0106 in-app**. The per-coin gap looks small, but on larger purchases it adds up, and the web channel avoids the surcharge that Apple and Google build into in-app pricing. TikTok's own [LIVE, Gifts and wallet support hub](https://support.tiktok.com/en/live-gifts-wallet) documents the coin-purchase flow, and our [how to buy TikTok coins](/learn/how-to-buy-tiktok-coins/) guide walks through the web checkout step by step.

Practical rules that hold in every country:

- **Buy on the web when you can.** It is the cheapest channel everywhere and the pricing is not affected by which phone you use.
- **Only buy what you will spend.** Coins are virtual items and are generally non-refundable under TikTok's [Virtual Items Policy](https://www.tiktok.com/legal/virtual-items).
- **Ignore "buy from another country" hacks.** Region-spoofing risks your account and rarely beats simply buying on the web.

## How to Check Your Exact Local Coin Price

The most accurate price is always the one TikTok shows you. To find it:

1. **Open the coin recharge screen.** In the app, go to your profile, then the wallet or Balance section, and tap to recharge coins.
2. **Note the packages in your currency.** TikTok lists each package's coin count and price in your local currency, tax included where applicable.
3. **Compare against the web.** Open tiktok.com, sign in, and view the same packages — the web price is usually a little lower.
4. **Do the per-coin math.** Divide the package price by its coin count to get your local per-coin rate, then compare it to the ~$0.0106 US anchor to see how your region stacks up.

**Worked example (illustrative).** Suppose your app shows 100 coins for €1.19. Divide €1.19 by 100 to get about **€0.0119 per coin**. At an example exchange rate of $1.08 per euro, that is roughly **$0.0129 per coin** — a bit above the US anchor, largely because EU prices include VAT in the sticker. Now check tiktok.com: if the same 100 coins there costs €1.09, that is about **€0.0109 per coin**, confirming the web channel is cheaper. The exact numbers will differ for you, but the method is the same everywhere: convert to a per-coin figure, then compare in-app against web. ## Common Mistakes About Coin Prices by Country

- **Expecting one global price.** There isn't one — prices are localized and move with currency and tax.
- **Trusting an old US figure.** The US anchor is about **$0.0106 per coin**, not older $1.29-per-100 quotes; and the maximum single package is **6,607 coins for $69.99**, not the stale 16,500-coin figure.
- **Confusing coin price with creator payout.** What a viewer pays for coins is separate from what a creator earns. Creators receive only about **50%** of a gift's coin value after TikTok's cut.
- **Region-spoofing to save a few cents.** It risks your account for savings the web channel already provides legitimately.

## FAQ

### How much do 100 TikTok coins cost?

At the US in-app anchor of about **$0.0106 per coin**, 100 coins cost roughly **$1.06**. The exact amount varies by country with currency, tax and app-store fees, and it is a little lower when you buy on the web.

### Which country has the cheapest TikTok coins?

TikTok publishes no official global ranking. Emerging markets with weaker currencies — such as Turkey, Argentina, India and Brazil — **tend to** show the lowest USD-equivalent prices, but you generally cannot buy from another region without that region's payment method and app-store account.

### Are TikTok coins cheaper on the website than in the app?

Yes. Buying on **tiktok.com** avoids the ~30% Apple/Google in-app fee, so the web channel is the cheapest reliable option in every country — about **$0.0105 per coin** versus $0.0106 in-app.

### Does TikTok publish official coin prices for each country?

No. Prices are set per region and change with local currency and app-store policies, and TikTok even A/B-tests price points. The only price you can fully trust is the one displayed in your own app or checkout.

### What is the largest TikTok coin package I can buy?

The largest single package is **6,607 coins for $69.99**. To spend more than that at once you simply buy multiple packages. The minimum package is 65 coins for $0.69.

### Are TikTok coin purchases refundable?

Generally no. Coins are virtual items and are non-refundable once purchased under TikTok's Virtual Items Policy, so only buy what you plan to spend.
