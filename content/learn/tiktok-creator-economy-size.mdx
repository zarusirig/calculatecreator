The TikTok creator economy reached an estimated **$15.5 billion in total creator earnings in 2025**, spanning direct platform payouts, brand-deal revenue, LIVE gifting, and TikTok Shop commissions paid to creators worldwide. That figure is about **28% higher than the $12.1 billion earned in 2024**, driven mainly by the rapid expansion of TikTok Shop and rising advertiser spend on the platform. With **more than 2 million active monetizing creators**, TikTok now runs the second-largest creator economy behind YouTube and is projected to pass **$24 billion by 2028**.

Last updated: July 2026 · Figures are informed estimates based on TikTok's official documentation, published GMV milestones, and industry reports through Q1 2026.

## Key Findings

Because ByteDance does not publish audited creator-payout data, every figure here is an informed estimate assembled from platform disclosures, official program documentation, published commerce milestones, and third-party market research. Treated as directional market sizing rather than exact accounting, the picture is consistent and clear.

Total creator earnings on TikTok have more than doubled since 2022 on our modeled series, moving from an estimated $5.4 billion to $15.5 billion in 2025. The earliest phase (2020–2021) was dominated by the original Creator Fund — a fixed pool TikTok publicly seeded with $200 million in 2020. Today the mix is very different: brand deals are the largest single category, followed by TikTok Shop commissions and direct platform payouts.

Brand-deal spending on TikTok influencers is now the biggest slice of the creator economy, estimated at roughly **$6.5 billion in 2025**, as advertising budgets shift toward short-form video and TikTok's engagement advantage. This is the category with the widest uncertainty margin, since most deals are negotiated privately.

TikTok Shop is the fastest-growing component. Creator commissions from Shop are estimated at **$3.2 billion in 2025**, up from a few hundred million dollars two years earlier. Embedding commerce directly in the content feed created a revenue stream that scales with audience purchasing behavior rather than views alone — and that barely existed three years ago.

Despite the headline growth, [income across creators](/learn/tiktok-creator-income-distribution/) remains sharply skewed. The **median monetizing creator earns roughly $150 per month**, about **67% of monetized creators make under $100 per month**, and only the **top 5% clear $5,000 or more per month**. The top 1% earn more than 500 times the median. A $15.5 billion economy is real, but most of it flows to a thin top tier.

### Total Payouts

TikTok's direct creator payouts — money paid by the platform itself through the Creator Rewards Program (formerly the Creator Fund), LIVE subscription revenue shares, and Series (paid content) — reached an estimated **$2.5 billion in 2025**. That is only about 16% of total creator economy value, but it is the most-watched metric because it comes straight from TikTok's own finances. LIVE gifting, worth an estimated $2.0 billion more, is counted separately because that money originates from viewers rather than the platform.

The shift from the fixed-pool Creator Fund to the performance-based Creator Rewards Program was the single largest structural change in TikTok's direct-payout model. TikTok's [Creator Rewards documentation](https://support.tiktok.com/en/business-and-creator/creator-rewards-program) describes the current system: payouts are tied to qualified views, video performance, and content-quality signals, with no fixed cap. The old Fund shared a limited annual pool across all eligible creators regardless of how many joined; the [Creator Rewards Program](https://www.tiktok.com/creator-academy/en/articles/monetize/creator-rewards) uncapped payouts and rewards individual video performance.

| Metric | Creator Fund (legacy) | Creator Rewards (current) |
|---|---|---|
| Payout model | Fixed pool, shared | Performance-based, uncapped |
| Est. RPM (US) | $0.02 – $0.05 per 1K views | $0.50 – $2.00 per 1K qualified views |
| Uplift vs Fund | — | 10–40x higher |
| Minimum video length | None | 60 seconds (original content) |
| Quality threshold | Low | Originality + content-quality scoring |

The correction here matters: the current program pays **$0.50 to $2.00 per 1,000 qualified views**, roughly 10 to 40 times the legacy Fund's $0.02–$0.05. Older articles that still quote a few cents per thousand views are describing a program that has been superseded in most markets. Independent 2026 analyses corroborate this order of magnitude — [Miraflow's monetization report](https://miraflow.ai/blog/tiktok-rpm-monetization-2026-how-much-tiktok-pays) estimates US Creator Rewards RPM at roughly $0.40 to $1.50 and names audience geography as the single biggest RPM factor. However, creators who post primarily sub-minute clips see little of this uplift, because Creator Rewards only pays on original videos of at least 60 seconds.

For context, YouTube paid creators an estimated $16.5 billion through its Partner Program in 2025, while Instagram's creator payouts (bonuses, subscriptions, and Reels incentives) totaled an estimated $1.5 billion. TikTok's $2.5 billion in direct payouts sits between these two, reflecting a younger direct-payout model catching up fast.

"Monetizing creators" means accounts that earned at least $100 in a calendar year from any TikTok monetization program — Creator Rewards, LIVE gifts, TikTok Shop commissions, or Series — including both full-time and part-time creators. To reach any of these programs, a creator generally needs 10,000 followers and 100,000 video views in the last 30 days (1,000 followers to go LIVE; 5,000 for TikTok Shop affiliate access).

Divide the $15.5 billion total by 2 million monetizing creators and you get an average of roughly **$7,750 per creator per year** — but that average is deeply misleading. Because earnings are so top-heavy, the **median monetizing creator earns closer to $150 per month (about $1,800 per year)**, and two-thirds earn under $100 per month. Applying the distribution to the creator base, the **top 5% — roughly 100,000 creators — earn $5,000 or more per month**, while a handful of [top creators earn millions annually](/learn/top-tiktok-creators-income/). The gap between the average and the median is the single most important thing to understand about this economy.

## What $15.5 Billion Means for an Individual Creator

Market-sizing numbers are abstract until you translate them to a single account, so here is the arithmetic worked out.

- **The headline average is not your target.** $15.5B ÷ 2M monetizing creators = ~$7,750 per year on average. But averages are dragged upward by the top tier.
- **The median is the realistic anchor.** Half of monetizing creators earn less than roughly $150 per month; two-thirds earn under $100 per month. First meaningful income typically arrives 3 to 6 months in.
- **Direct platform pay is the smallest piece.** A creator with 1 million qualified monthly views earns roughly $500 to $2,000 from Creator Rewards — but brand deals, Shop commissions, and LIVE gifts usually make up the larger share of a serious creator's income.
- **Getting to the top 5% is the goal.** The ~100,000 creators earning $5,000+ per month almost always combine several income streams rather than relying on RPM alone.

## Methodology

The market-sizing estimates in this article are compiled from multiple primary and secondary sources. Because TikTok (ByteDance) does not publicly report granular creator-payout data, all figures should be treated as informed estimates with inherent uncertainty margins. Our constants were last verified on 2026-03-01 against TikTok's Creator Portal and industry reports from Q1 2026.

**Direct platform payout estimates** are derived from TikTok's publicly disclosed Creator Fund allocation ($200M in 2020), scaled forward using the announced Creator Rewards transition and the program's published qualified-view payout model, combined with the per-view RPM ranges documented across our monetization dataset ($0.50–$2.00 per 1,000 qualified views for Creator Rewards).

**Brand-deal spending estimates** are modeled from published advertiser-spend forecasts, industry influencer-marketing reports, and creator rate-card ranges by follower tier. This is the least precise category, because most deals are private and many carry non-disclosure terms.

**TikTok Shop commission estimates** are based on TikTok's published commission structures (a 5% standard affiliate rate, ranging 2–20% by category) applied to reported and estimated GMV, anchored to the $33B global GMV milestone for 2025 and its regional breakdowns.

**LIVE gifting estimates** use published coin-to-diamond economics (a diamond is worth about $0.005, roughly a 50% creator share) applied to estimated total streaming hours and viewership.

**Income distribution figures** — the ~$150 median, the 67% under $100/month, and the top-5% threshold — reflect our analysis of 4,100 monetized accounts, detailed on our [creator income distribution](/learn/tiktok-creator-income-distribution/) page.

**Projection methodology.** Forward-looking estimates (2026–2028) are modeled from historical growth rates, announced platform features, consensus advertising-market forecasts, and TikTok Shop's geographic expansion timeline. They assume no major regulatory disruptions.

**Limitations.** ByteDance does not publish audited creator-economy data. Brand-deal revenue is the hardest category to estimate precisely. Use these figures as directional market sizing, not exact financial data. All figures are in USD and reflect calendar-year estimates.

## FAQ

### How big is the TikTok creator economy in 2026?

TikTok's creator economy reached an estimated $15.5 billion in total creator earnings in 2025, up about 28% from $12.1 billion in 2024. That total spans direct platform payouts, brand deals, LIVE gifting, and TikTok Shop commissions. It is projected to pass $24 billion by 2028 as TikTok Shop expands into new markets.

### How many creators make money on TikTok?

More than 2 million creators actively monetized on TikTok in 2025, defined as accounts earning at least $100 in the year from any program. That is roughly 1.8% of the platform's active creators — lower than YouTube's share but rising as newer programs like TikTok Shop widen who can earn.

### Is TikTok's creator economy bigger than YouTube's?

No. YouTube remains the largest creator economy at an estimated $35 billion, with TikTok second at $15.5 billion — just ahead of Instagram's $14 billion. However, TikTok is growing more than twice as fast as YouTube, so the gap is narrowing each year.

### How much does the average TikTok creator earn?

The arithmetic average is about $7,750 per year ($15.5 billion divided by 2 million monetizing creators), but that number is misleading. The median monetizing creator earns closer to $150 per month, roughly two-thirds earn under $100 per month, and only the top 5% clear $5,000 or more per month.

### What is the fastest-growing part of the TikTok creator economy?

TikTok Shop. Global Shop GMV grew about 115% in 2025 to roughly $33 billion, and creator commissions from Shop reached an estimated $3.2 billion. Because it ties earnings to product sales rather than views alone, Shop has become the highest-growth income stream for creators and is projected to keep expanding through 2028.

### Who earns the most in the TikTok creator economy?

Earnings are highly concentrated: the top 5% of monetizing creators (around 100,000 accounts) earn $5,000 or more per month, and the top 1% earn more than 500 times the median. The very highest-paid creators pull in millions annually, driven mostly by brand deals, merchandise, and outside ventures rather than platform RPM — as detailed in our [top TikTok creators income](/learn/top-tiktok-creators-income/) breakdown.
