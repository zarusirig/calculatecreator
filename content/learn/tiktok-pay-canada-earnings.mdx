TikTok paid Canadian creators approximately C$0.02 to C$0.04 per 1,000 views through the legacy Creator Fund, placing Canada in the upper tier of TikTok's global payout structure alongside the [United Kingdom](/learn/tiktok-pay-uk-earnings/) and [Australia](/learn/tiktok-pay-australia-earnings/). Under those Fund-era rates, a Canadian creator generating 1 million monthly views could expect about C$20 to C$40 from the Fund alone, with total earnings reaching C$400 to C$3,500 or more when brand deals, live gifts, and TikTok Shop commissions are factored in. Canada's growing influencer marketing industry and proximity to the US advertising market make it an increasingly attractive country for TikTok monetization.

**Important context on the program names.** The per-view rates and earnings figures on this page reflect TikTok's **legacy Creator Fund**, a fixed-pool program that has been replaced. TikTok's current direct-payment program is the [**Creator Rewards Program**](https://support.tiktok.com/en/business-and-creator/creator-rewards-program), which pays substantially more -- about **$0.50 to $2.00 per 1,000 qualified views** (US baseline; varies by market and eligibility), or roughly **10 to 40 times** the Fund-era numbers shown here. Creator Rewards only pays for **original videos longer than one minute**; the one-minute-original rule is a Creator Rewards qualifier, not a legacy Fund requirement. Treat the Fund-era figures below as historical benchmarks, and expect qualifying Creator Rewards content to earn considerably more.

## Key Findings

The Canadian earnings figures on this page are estimates, modeled from TikTok's published Creator Fund and Creator Rewards ranges, Canada-adjusted advertising rates, and publicly available agency rate cards rather than any private survey. They reflect Canada's unique position in the global TikTok economy.

The legacy Creator Fund paid Canadian creators roughly C$0.02 to C$0.04 per 1,000 views. (The newer Creator Rewards Program, which requires original videos longer than one minute and pays about $0.50 to $2.00 per 1,000 qualified views -- roughly 10 to 40 times the Fund -- pays far more; see the note above.) In USD-equivalent terms, this placed Canadian RPM roughly 30% to 40% below [US rates](/learn/tiktok-pay-us-earnings/) and roughly on par with [UK rates](/learn/tiktok-pay-uk-earnings/) when adjusted for purchasing power. Payouts are made in CAD.

Brand deals are the primary income driver for Canadian creators who actively pursue sponsorships. Nano-influencers (10K to 50K followers) earn C$200 to C$700 per sponsored video, while mid-tier creators (250K to 1M followers) command C$1,500 to C$7,000 per post. The Canadian influencer marketing industry has grown rapidly, reaching an estimated C$800 million in 2025.

Canadian creators benefit from proximity to the US market. Many Canadian creators attract a mixed US-Canadian audience, which can boost RPM because US-based viewers carry higher CPM value. Bilingual creators in Quebec also access the French-Canadian market, which has its own advertiser demand.

CRA tax obligations require self-employed creators to pay both income tax and Canada Pension Plan (CPP) contributions. The basic personal amount of C$15,705 shelters lower-earning creators from income tax, but CPP contributions of 11.9% (combined employee and employer portions for self-employed) apply on net self-employment earnings between C$3,500 and C$73,200.

### RPM CAD

RPM in Canadian dollars is the key metric for understanding TikTok's direct payments to Canadian creators, and it varies based on several factors specific to the Canadian market.

Under the legacy Creator Fund, Canadian TikTok RPM ranged from C$0.02 to C$0.04 per 1,000 views. (The one-minute-original-video and originality thresholds are Creator Rewards qualifiers, not legacy Fund rules; Creator Rewards pays about $0.50 to $2.00 per 1,000 qualified views for that eligible long-form content.) Shorter videos under 60 seconds earned roughly C$0.008 to C$0.015 per 1K views, making them unviable as a primary income source.

| RPM Factor | Impact on Canadian Earnings | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Content niche | High-CPM niches earn 1.5x to 2.5x base | Finance: C$0.035 – C$0.055; Comedy: C$0.015 – C$0.025 |
| Audience geography | US viewers in audience boost RPM | 70% Canadian + 20% US: C$0.03 – C$0.04; 90% Canadian: C$0.02 – C$0.03 |
| Video length | Longer videos earn higher RPM | 1-3 min: baseline; 3-5 min: 1.2x – 1.4x |
| Language | English content reaches broader audience | English: baseline; French-only: 0.8x – 0.9x (smaller ad market) |
| Seasonal timing | Q4 RPM 40% to 65% higher than Q1 | November: C$0.03 – C$0.05; January: C$0.015 – C$0.025 |

A unique advantage for Canadian creators is audience spillover from the United States. Because Canadian and American culture overlap significantly, many Canadian creators attract 15% to 30% US viewers organically. Since US-based viewers carry higher CPM value, this mixed audience can push effective RPM above the pure-Canadian rate. Creators who intentionally produce content appealing to both markets can earn RPM closer to C$0.035 to C$0.045. Independent creator-reported trackers broadly agree on the current program: [IncomeFromViews](https://incomefromviews.com/blog/tiktok-creativity-program-rpm-2026/) estimates Canadian Creator Rewards RPM at roughly $0.60 to $0.90 per 1,000 qualified views. These are estimates from creator-reported data, not official TikTok rates, since TikTok publishes no per-country RPM table.

Bilingual creators in Quebec face a trade-off. French-language content accesses a dedicated but smaller advertising market, which can mean slightly lower RPM. However, bilingual creators who post in both English and French often build larger total audiences, offsetting any per-view rate difference. For niche-level RPM data, see our [RPM rates by niche](/learn/rpm-rates-by-niche/) analysis.

## TikTok Pay in Canada — Extended Data

Beyond RPM and brand deal rates, Canadian creators face specific tax obligations, market dynamics, and cross-border considerations that affect real earnings.

**CRA tax obligations** require all self-employed creators earning more than C$3,500 in net self-employment income to make CPP contributions. Income tax applies after the basic personal amount of C$15,705 (federal) is exceeded.

| Annual TikTok Earnings | Federal Income Tax Rate | CPP Contribution (Self-Employed) | Provincial Tax (Avg) | Approximate Take-Home % |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| C$1,000 – C$15,705 | 0% | 0% (below C$3,500 threshold) | 0% – 5% | 95% – 100% |
| C$15,706 – C$55,867 | 15% | 11.9% (on earnings above C$3,500) | 5% – 10% | 63% – 70% |
| C$55,868 – C$111,733 | 20.5% | 11.9% (capped at C$73,200) | 8% – 15% | 55% – 62% |
| C$111,734 – C$154,906 | 26% | Capped | 10% – 17% | 47% – 55% |
| C$154,907+ | 29% – 33% | Capped | 12% – 20% | 40% – 50% |

Provincial tax rates vary significantly. Alberta has the lowest combined provincial rate, while Quebec has the highest. Creators in Quebec also pay into the Quebec Pension Plan (QPP) instead of CPP, at slightly different rates. GST/HST registration is required once gross revenue exceeds C$30,000 in a 12-month period, though most digital creator services are zero-rated for GST purposes. See the [creator taxes guide](/learn/tiktok-creator-taxes-guide/) for detailed Canadian tax planning.

**Cross-border content opportunities** are a major advantage for Canadian creators. Producing content that resonates with US audiences can attract US-based brand deals, which pay 30% to 50% more than domestic Canadian sponsorships. Some Canadian creators register US LLCs or work with US-based agencies to access the larger American market while remaining tax-resident in Canada.

**TikTok Shop in Canada** is available but less mature than in the US or UK. Canadian creators have access to affiliate linking and earn seller-set commissions -- commonly around 5%, and up to 20% for select categories -- on sales. The product catalog is smaller than the US Shop, which limits earning potential for now. As TikTok expands Shop inventory in Canada, this revenue stream is expected to grow. ## Methodology

The Canadian earnings figures in this article are estimates, modeled from published rate ranges rather than a private creator survey.

**Rate inputs.** Per-view figures apply TikTok's legacy Creator Fund ranges and the current Creator Rewards Program structure ($0.50 to $2.00 per 1,000 qualified views, US baseline) to Canada-adjusted advertising rates, which run below the US baseline. Exchange rate conversions use the average CAD/USD rate for the reporting period where cross-country comparisons are made.

**Reference sources** include publicly available rate cards from Canadian influencer marketing agencies, [TikTok's official Creator Rewards documentation](https://www.tiktok.com/creator-academy/en/articles/monetize/creator-rewards), CRA self-employment tax guidance, and third-party RPM trackers used only for directional corroboration. Brand deal ranges reflect commonly cited agency and creator-reported rates.

**Limitations.** These are estimates, not guarantees, and individual results vary widely with niche, audience geography (US viewers raise effective RPM), engagement, and season. Only qualified views (unique For You feed views watched more than five seconds) earn under Creator Rewards. Bilingual French-English earnings dynamics add further variance. Tax estimates are general guidance -- consult a Canadian accountant or the CRA directly for personalized advice. Constants were last verified March 2026; we update this data quarterly.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does TikTok pay per 1,000 views in Canada?

Under the legacy Creator Fund, TikTok paid Canadian creators roughly C$0.02 to C$0.04 per 1,000 views. The current Creator Rewards Program pays far more -- about $0.50 to $2.00 per 1,000 qualified views for original videos longer than one minute (US baseline; varies by market). Shorter videos earned roughly C$0.008 to C$0.015 per 1K views under the Fund model. Rates vary by niche, audience geography (US viewers boost RPM), engagement rate, and seasonal demand. Use the [pay per 1000 views tool](/learn/tiktok-pay-per-1000-views/) to estimate your specific rate.

### Do Canadian TikTok creators pay taxes on earnings?

Yes. All Canadian TikTok earnings are taxable income. Creators must report self-employment income to the CRA and pay federal and provincial income tax after the basic personal amount of C$15,705. CPP contributions of 11.9% apply on net self-employment earnings between C$3,500 and C$73,200. GST/HST registration is required once gross revenue exceeds C$30,000 in 12 months. See the [creator taxes guide](/learn/tiktok-creator-taxes-guide/) for Canadian-specific advice.

### How do Canadian TikTok earnings compare to the US?

Canadian legacy Creator Fund RPM (C$0.02 to C$0.04) was roughly 30% to 40% lower than US Fund rates ($0.03 to $0.05) in USD-equivalent terms. (Both markets' current Creator Rewards payouts are far higher -- about $0.50 to $2.00 per 1,000 qualified views.) Canadian brand deal rates are also 25% to 40% lower than US equivalents. However, Canada's higher basic personal amount (C$15,705 vs effectively $0 for US SE tax) means lower-earning Canadian creators keep a larger after-tax share. See our [US earnings breakdown](/learn/tiktok-pay-us-earnings/) for detailed comparison.

### Is the Creator Rewards Program available in Canada?

Yes. Canadian creators have full access to the Creator Rewards Program with the same [eligibility requirements](https://support.tiktok.com/en/business-and-creator/creator-rewards-program) as the US: 10,000 followers and 100,000 views in the last 30 days. Payouts are in CAD, and all core features including the analytics dashboard and search value bonus are available. Some new features may launch in Canada 2 to 6 weeks after their US debut.
