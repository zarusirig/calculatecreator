TikTok pays roughly $0.50 to $2.00 per 1,000 qualified views through its current Creator Rewards Program, and about $0.02 to $0.05 per 1,000 views under the older, legacy Creator Fund. In practical terms, 1,000 views is worth anywhere from a few cents to a couple of dollars — the exact figure depends on which program you qualify for, your content niche, and where your viewers are located.

Those two programs are the reason the same 1,000 views can pay wildly different amounts. TikTok has never published official RPM (revenue per mille) rates, so every per-view figure on this page is an estimate built from published creator reports, TikTok's own eligibility documentation, and industry data current to Q1 2026. Below you will find rates by program, niche, and country, plus worked dollar examples you can apply to your own view counts.

## TikTok Payment Rates in 2026

TikTok pays creators through several programs at once, and your per-view rate depends heavily on which one you use. The platform's two direct, view-based programs are Creator Rewards (the current program) and the older Creator Fund (legacy, still active in a handful of markets). Everything else — brand deals, LIVE gifts, and TikTok Shop — pays on top of these and is not tied to a fixed per-view rate.

| Program | Pay per 1,000 views | Video requirement | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| Creator Rewards | $0.50 – $2.00 (qualified views) | 60+ seconds, original | Current program |
| Creator Fund | $0.02 – $0.05 | Any length | Legacy, being phased out |

The difference is stark: on identical view counts, Creator Rewards pays roughly 10 to 40 times more than the legacy Creator Fund. If you are still enrolled in the Creator Fund, switching to Creator Rewards is the single highest-impact change you can make to your per-view earnings.

### Creator Rewards Program Rates

TikTok's [Creator Rewards Program](https://support.tiktok.com/en/business-and-creator/creator-rewards-program), which replaced the earlier Creativity Program Beta, pays an estimated $0.50 to $2.00 per 1,000 qualified views. Rates above $1.00 are common for content that holds attention and lands in a high-value niche; lower-value categories sit nearer the floor.

Two rules define the program. First, videos must be at least 60 seconds long and original to earn. Second, only "qualified views" count — TikTok defines these as unique For You feed views watched for more than five seconds, and it excludes paid or promoted views, artificial or fraudulent traffic, views on disliked videos, and repeat views from the same account. A video needs at least 1,000 qualified For You feed views before it starts earning. TikTok's [Creator Academy](https://www.tiktok.com/creator-academy/en/articles/monetize/creator-rewards) and its [rewards calculation page](https://www.tiktok.com/support/faq_detail?id=7543897461245450808) describe a two-part model: a Standard Reward (qualified views × RPM) plus an Additional Reward for content quality.

Worked example: a 90-second original video that earns 100,000 qualified views at a $1.00 RPM pays about $100 (100,000 ÷ 1,000 × $1.00). The same video at a $2.00 RPM in a premium niche pays about $200. Learn how the underlying rate is derived in our explainer on [what TikTok RPM means](/learn/what-is-tiktok-rpm/).

### Old Creator Fund Rates

The original TikTok Creator Fund pays far less — an estimated $0.02 to $0.05 per 1,000 views, averaging around $0.03. That works out to roughly $20 to $50 per million views, a rate many creators found impossible to build an income on. Our breakdown of [Creator Fund pay per view](/learn/creator-fund-pay-per-view/) covers how those payouts are calculated.

The Fund splits a fixed pool of money among all participating creators, so your individual rate falls as more creators join and total views rise. That structural flaw is exactly why TikTok built Creator Rewards as a replacement. See the full side-by-side in our [Creator Rewards vs Creator Fund comparison](/learn/creator-rewards-vs-creator-fund/).

## What Counts as a Qualified View

Under Creator Rewards, not every play earns money. A qualified view is a unique For You feed view watched for longer than five seconds. TikTok excludes:

- Paid or promoted views (from ads or the Promote tool)
- Fraudulent, artificial, or bot traffic
- Views on videos a user has disliked
- Repeat views from the same account

This is why "views" in your analytics and "qualified views" in your rewards dashboard rarely match. A video with 500,000 total plays might have only 300,000 qualified views once repeats and sub-five-second scrolls are stripped out. When you estimate earnings, always apply the RPM to qualified views, not raw plays.

## How to Qualify for Creator Rewards

Before you earn a cent per view, your account has to clear the program's entry bar. To join Creator Rewards you need:

- **10,000 followers** on a personal account in good standing
- **100,000 video views in the last 30 days**
- An account holder **aged 18 or older** (19 in South Korea)
- Residence in a country where the program is available

Once you are in, each individual video still has to earn at least **1,000 qualified For You feed views** before it begins generating a reward, and it must be at least 60 seconds long and original. A reposted trend audio over someone else's footage, a Duet, or a Stitch generally will not qualify — TikTok weights originality heavily in the Additional Reward portion of the payout.

A practical monthly example: a creator with 400,000 qualified views a month in the food niche (roughly a $0.75 RPM) earns about $300 from Creator Rewards (400 × $0.75). Move that same 400,000 qualified views into the finance niche at a $2.50 RPM and the monthly reward climbs to roughly $1,000 — same effort, very different payout, purely because of niche RPM and audience value.

## How TikTok Per-View Pay Compares to Other Platforms

TikTok's per-view rate sits below long-form YouTube but is competitive with other short-form platforms. The table below shows estimated RPM across the major video platforms.

| Platform | Est. RPM (per 1,000 views) |
|---|---|
| YouTube (long-form AdSense) | $2.00 – $4.00 |
| Twitch | $2.00 – $5.00 |
| TikTok Creator Rewards | $0.50 – $2.00 |
| YouTube Shorts | $0.04 – $0.10 |

In per-view terms, the legacy Creator Fund pays roughly 50 to 100 times less than YouTube's long-form AdSense; Creator Rewards narrows that gap to a small multiple of the YouTube rate rather than a two-order-of-magnitude gulf.

The takeaway: TikTok's direct per-view pay is modest, but its reach is enormous and its monetization stack — Creator Rewards, brand deals, LIVE gifts, and TikTok Shop — lets creators earn far more per view in aggregate than the Creator Rewards RPM alone suggests.

## Factors That Affect Your Per-View Earnings

### Niche and Content Category

Finance, business, and technology content commands the highest RPM because advertisers in those categories pay premium rates. Comedy and entertainment scale faster in raw views but earn less per view. You do not have to abandon your niche — angling existing content toward higher-commercial-intent topics within it lifts your effective rate.

### Video Length and Watch Time

Under Creator Rewards, longer videos that hold attention earn more. The 60-second minimum is a hard gate: anything shorter earns nothing from the program. Beyond that, a three-minute video watched to 70% completion generates far more qualified-view value than a 60-second clip most viewers skip after a few seconds. Optimize for completion, not just plays.

### Audience Geography

As the country table shows, where your viewers live can matter as much as how many you have. A creator with 100,000 US views can out-earn a creator with 300,000 views from lower-CPM markets. Posting in English during US and UK evening hours shifts your audience mix toward higher-paying regions.

## Common Mistakes That Lower Your Per-View Rate

- **Posting videos under 60 seconds.** Short clips do not qualify for Creator Rewards at all, capping you at legacy-Fund rates or nothing.
- **Staying on the legacy Creator Fund.** At 10 to 40 times lower pay, the Fund leaves the vast majority of your potential earnings on the table.
- **Chasing global virality over geography.** A million views from low-CPM markets can pay less than 200,000 views from Tier 1 countries.
- **Counting raw plays instead of qualified views.** Repeat views and sub-five-second scrolls do not earn — always estimate on qualified views.
- **Ignoring watch time.** Completion rate feeds both the algorithm and the Additional Reward, so weak retention hurts your RPM twice.

## How to Increase Your Per-View Rate

To maximize your TikTok earnings per view:

1. **Make every video 60+ seconds and original** so it qualifies for Creator Rewards, which pays 10 to 40 times more than the old Creator Fund.
2. **Lean into higher-CPM angles** within your niche to attract premium advertisers.
3. **Build a Tier 1 audience** by posting in English during US and UK peak hours.
4. **Optimize for completion** — hook hard in the first two seconds and hold attention to the end, since retention feeds both the algorithm and the Additional Reward.
5. **Stack revenue streams** — pair per-view income with brand deals, LIVE gifts, and TikTok Shop so your effective earnings per view multiply. Start by benchmarking your rate with our [RPM calculator](/calculators/rpm/).

Independent 2026 analyses, such as [Miraflow's RPM breakdown](https://miraflow.ai/blog/tiktok-rpm-monetization-2026-how-much-tiktok-pays), reach the same conclusion: audience geography is the single biggest lever on your RPM, followed by niche and retention.

## FAQ

### How much does TikTok pay per 1,000 views in 2026?

An estimated $0.50 to $2.00 per 1,000 qualified views through the Creator Rewards Program, or about $0.02 to $0.05 per 1,000 views under the legacy Creator Fund. TikTok does not publish official RPM rates, so these figures are drawn from creator reports and industry data.

### Does TikTok pay the same in every country?

No. US-based views pay the most, and rates scale down by region — roughly 0.9x in the UK, 0.85x in Canada, 0.8x in Australia, 0.75x across the EU, and as low as 0.3x in Sub-Saharan Africa relative to the US baseline. Advertiser demand in each market drives the difference.

### Do TikTok videos under one minute make money?

Not through Creator Rewards. The program requires videos to be at least 60 seconds long and original. Clips shorter than a minute can still drive follower growth, brand-deal interest, and LIVE traffic, but they do not earn a per-view reward.

### How many followers and views do you need to get paid on TikTok?

Creator Rewards requires 10,000 followers, at least 100,000 video views in the last 30 days, an account holder aged 18 or older (19 in South Korea), and a personal account in good standing. Individual videos then need 1,000 qualified For You feed views before they start earning.

### Is TikTok or YouTube better for earnings per 1,000 views?

Per view, YouTube's long-form AdSense pays more — an estimated $2.00 to $4.00 RPM versus TikTok Creator Rewards' $0.50 to $2.00. YouTube Shorts ($0.04–$0.10) and Instagram Reels ($0.01–$0.03) are closer to TikTok. TikTok's advantage is reach and its stacked monetization tools rather than raw per-view rate.

### Which country has the highest TikTok RPM?

The United States. It is TikTok's highest-paying market and the baseline all other regions are measured against, largely because US advertisers spend the most to reach TikTok audiences.
