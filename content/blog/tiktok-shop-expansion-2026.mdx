## New Seller and Creator Features

The 2026 expansion comes with several significant feature updates:

### Enhanced Affiliate Program

TikTok Shop's affiliate program, which allows creators to earn commissions by promoting products without holding inventory, received a major upgrade in January 2026. Key changes include:

- **Tiered commission boosts.** High-performing affiliates (over $10,000 in monthly sales) now receive commission rate bonuses of 2-5% above the base rate set by sellers.
- **AI product recommendations.** TikTok's algorithm now suggests products to affiliates based on their audience demographics and content niche, making it easier to find relevant products to promote.
- **Sample program expansion.** More sellers are offering free product samples to affiliates, reducing the barrier to creating authentic product reviews.

### Live Shopping Improvements

Live shopping has been TikTok Shop's highest-converting sales channel, and the 2026 updates enhance it further:

- **Multi-host live streams.** Sellers can now add up to 4 co-hosts to a single live shopping session, enabling collaborative selling.
- **Automated product showcasing.** Products can now be pinned and rotated automatically during live streams based on viewer interest signals.
- **Improved analytics.** Real-time sales dashboards during live streams now show conversion rates, average order value, and audience retention by product.

These live features complement [live earnings from gifts and tips](/calculators/earnings-revenue/live-earnings/), giving creators two simultaneous revenue streams during a single broadcast.

### Shop Tab Redesign

TikTok redesigned its in-app Shop tab in January 2026 to feature more personalized product recommendations, trending products, and creator-curated collections. Early data suggests the redesign increased Shop tab browse-to-purchase conversion rates by approximately 18%.

## Commission Structure Changes

The reductions in several categories reflect TikTok's strategy to undercut Amazon and other e-commerce platforms on seller costs. Lower platform commissions mean sellers can offer more competitive prices or higher affiliate commissions to creators, both of which benefit the ecosystem.

Note that these are TikTok's platform fees. Affiliate commissions paid to creators by sellers are separate and set by each individual seller, typically ranging from 5% to 20% depending on the product category and margin structure.
