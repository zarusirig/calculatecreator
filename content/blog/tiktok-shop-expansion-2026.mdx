## What Is New With TikTok Shop in 2026

TikTok Shop's 2026 European rollout is the platform's largest geographic push since its US launch in September 2023. It is happening alongside feature updates and a clearer, more standardized fee structure that affects both new and existing markets.

The context matters. According to [TechNode Global](https://technode.global/2026/02/11/tiktoks-southeast-asia-doubles-gmv-year-on-year-to-45-6b-in-2025/), TikTok Shop's global gross merchandise value (GMV) reached roughly $64.3 billion in 2025, up about 94% year over year, with the US contributing an estimated $15.1 billion and Southeast Asia $45.6 billion. TikTok's internal projections put GMV on track to roughly double again by the end of 2026. For creators who earn through Shop, that trajectory translates directly into [more money-making opportunities on TikTok](/guides/how-to-make-money-on-tiktok/).

## New Markets: Europe Leads the 2026 Expansion

The headline of the 2026 expansion is Western Europe. After years of regulatory and logistics groundwork, TikTok Shop launched in three of the continent's largest economies on the same day.

For European creators, the launch opens the same commerce tools US and UK creators have used for over a year: shoppable video, LIVE shopping, and the affiliate program that lets creators earn commissions without holding inventory. ## New Seller and Creator Features

The 2026 expansion arrived with several feature updates aimed at both sides of the marketplace.

### Enhanced Affiliate Program

TikTok Shop's affiliate program — which lets creators earn commissions by promoting products without holding inventory — received upgrades in early 2026:

- **AI product recommendations.** TikTok's system now surfaces products to affiliates based on audience demographics and content niche, making it easier to find relevant items to promote.
- **Multi-SKU sampling.** More sellers offer free product samples to affiliates, lowering the barrier to creating authentic, hands-on reviews.
- **Clearer rate protection.** Sellers set commission rates, and rate changes now carry a protection window so creators are not caught off guard mid-campaign.

### Live Shopping Improvements

LIVE shopping remains TikTok Shop's highest-converting sales channel, and 2026 updates push it further:

- **Multi-host live streams.** Sellers can add co-hosts to a single LIVE shopping session for collaborative selling.
- **Automated product showcasing.** Products can be pinned and rotated during streams based on viewer interest signals.
- **Improved real-time analytics.** LIVE dashboards now surface conversion rate, average order value, and audience retention by product.

These features complement [live earnings from gifts and tips](/calculators/earnings-revenue/live-earnings/), giving creators two revenue streams in a single broadcast.

### Logistics and Fulfillment Changes

The expansion also came with tighter logistics standards for sellers. [Dashboardly](https://www.dashboardly.io/post/tiktok-shop-affiliate-commissions-2026-payouts-clawbacks-profit-math) reports a US TikTok Shop Logistics mandate taking effect March 31, 2026, standardizing shipping partners and in-transit timelines. For affiliates this is mostly background, but it matters indirectly: faster, more reliable delivery reduces cancellations and returns, and returns are where affiliate commissions get clawed back.

## Commission and Fee Structure in 2026

It is worth separating the two fee concepts that get confused constantly.

**Seller fees.** TikTok Shop charges sellers a platform referral fee — not affiliates. Site guidance frames this as roughly 2% to 8% depending on category (about 2% for essentials, 5% standard, and up to 8% for luxury goods), while third-party analysts such as Dashboardly report an effective referral fee closer to 6% for US sellers, with optional fulfillment fees stacking on top.

**Affiliate commissions.** Affiliates are paid a gross commission set by each seller. The site-standard affiliate rate is 5%, with most programs falling between 2% and 20% depending on category and margin. Sellers in the newly launched European markets are generally offering 5-15% to start.

## FAQ

### Which countries have TikTok Shop in 2026?

As of mid-2026, TikTok Shop operates in the United States, the United Kingdom, six Southeast Asian markets (Indonesia, Malaysia, the Philippines, Singapore, Thailand, and Vietnam), and — following the March 27, 2026 launch — France, Germany, and Italy. Spain is planned for the third quarter of 2026, with more European markets targeted through 2027.

### How much commission do TikTok Shop affiliates earn?

The site-standard affiliate rate is 5%, with most categories paying between 2% and 20%. Newly launched European programs generally start creators in the 5-15% range. Commissions are set per product by each seller, not by TikTok, and they can be clawed back if the customer returns the item within the settlement window.

### What is the difference between Open and Targeted Collaboration?

Open Collaboration lets any eligible affiliate promote a product at a fixed, seller-set rate (often around 10-15%). Targeted Collaboration is an invite-based or negotiated arrangement in which a seller offers a specific creator a higher rate — [ShortFormNation](https://www.shortformnation.com/blog/tiktok-shop-affiliate-marketing-the-complete-2026-guide) reports negotiated rates ranging from 18% to as high as 50% for top performers.

### Is TikTok Shop GMV the same as creator earnings?

No. GMV is total sales volume flowing through the marketplace, not money that reaches any single creator or seller. After commissions, referral fees, and returns, a meaningful share of GMV never lands as profit — always model your own numbers rather than assuming a slice of headline GMV.
