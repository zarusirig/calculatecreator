The short-form video landscape in 2026 is still dominated by two giants: TikTok and YouTube Shorts. Both pay creators directly for short-form content, but they use completely different payment models. And once you replace the outdated Creator Fund figures that older guides still quote with TikTok's current Creator Rewards rates, the "which pays more" question has a different answer than most comparisons suggest.

This breakdown compares what each platform actually pays per 1,000 views in 2026, how eligibility and reach differ, and how the two stack up in realistic monthly earnings scenarios. Every figure here is an estimate based on published RPM ranges and official platform documentation — never a guarantee. Actual pay varies with niche, region, retention, and how much of your audience meets each program's rules.

## What TikTok and YouTube Shorts Pay Per 1,000 Views

RPM (revenue per mille) is how much you earn per 1,000 views, and it is the fastest way to compare platform payouts. The critical correction for 2026 is this: TikTok's current direct short-form program is the Creator Rewards Program (formerly the Creativity Program), not the legacy Creator Fund. Creator Rewards pays an estimated **$0.50-$2.00 per 1,000 qualified views** — roughly 10-40x the old fund's $0.02-$0.05. YouTube Shorts pays around **$0.04-$0.10 per 1,000 views** through its pooled ad-revenue share.

| Program | Rate per 1,000 views | Estimated pay per 1M views |
| --- | --- | --- |
| TikTok Creator Rewards (original, 1 min+) | $0.50 - $2.00 (qualified views) | $500 - $2,000 |
| YouTube Shorts revenue share | $0.04 - $0.10 | $40 - $100 |
| TikTok Creator Fund (legacy, superseded) | $0.02 - $0.05 | $20 - $50 |
| YouTube long-form AdSense (for context) | $2.00 - $4.00 | $2,000 - $4,000 |

Per qualified short-form view, TikTok Creator Rewards pays several times more than YouTube Shorts. Three caveats keep this from being a blowout:

- **TikTok's rate is conditional.** It applies only to original videos at least one minute long, and only to qualified views — unique For You feed views watched longer than 5 seconds, per [TikTok's official Creator Rewards documentation](https://support.tiktok.com/en/business-and-creator/creator-rewards-program). Sub-minute clips, Duets, Stitches, and photo posts fall back to legacy-fund economics, and each video needs 1,000 qualified views before it earns anything.
- **YouTube's real edge is the long-form funnel.** A Short can send a viewer to a 10-minute video earning an estimated $2.00-$4.00 per 1,000 views — roughly 20-100x the Shorts rate. That pipeline does not exist on TikTok.
- **Creator-reported payouts often look tiny.** In threads like [this r/NewTubers discussion](https://www.reddit.com/r/NewTubers/comments/1n3pv4x/are_people_making_the_same_money_on_tiktok_that/), creators report figures like $50-$60 for 7 million TikTok views — legacy-fund-era numbers from videos that never met Rewards criteria. Anecdotes like these describe the floor, not the program's ceiling.

## Reach and Volume: Where TikTok Still Wins

Higher RPM is only half the story — the other half is how many views you actually get. TikTok's For You page distributes short-form content to non-followers more aggressively than YouTube's Shorts shelf, so TikTok maintains a reach advantage across every follower tier, though the gap has narrowed since 2024 as YouTube improved its recommendation system.

| Follower Tier | TikTok Avg. Views/Post | Shorts Avg. Views/Post |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Under 10K | 2,800 - 8,500 | 1,500 - 5,200 |
| 10K - 50K | 12,000 - 35,000 | 8,000 - 28,000 |
| 50K - 200K | 45,000 - 120,000 | 35,000 - 95,000 |
| 200K - 1M | 150,000 - 450,000 | 120,000 - 380,000 |
| 1M+ | 500,000 - 2,000,000 | 400,000 - 1,600,000 |

The two platforms also discover content differently. TikTok evaluates each video independently, giving new creators an unusually fair shot at going viral regardless of follower count. YouTube leans on its knowledge graph and, crucially, indexes Shorts in Google and YouTube search — a passive, evergreen reach channel that TikTok lacks. If your topics are searchable, that SEO tail can outlast any TikTok trend.

## Monetization Beyond Ad Revenue

Both platforms layer several income streams on top of ad revenue. Understanding the full ecosystem matters more than any single RPM number.

| Monetization Feature | TikTok | YouTube Shorts |

## YouTube's Long-Form Funnel Advantage

The most underrated difference between the platforms is not the Shorts RPM — it is what happens after the Short. YouTube Shorts feed viewers into a creator's long-form library, and long-form YouTube videos earn an estimated $2.00-$4.00 per 1,000 views, roughly 20-100x the Shorts rate. A creator earning a few hundred dollars a month from Shorts can earn several times that from the long-form videos those Shorts help discover.

TikTok is extending its own longer formats (up to 10 minutes) and Series content, but longer TikToks still earn at short-form Creator Rewards rates rather than at YouTube's long-form AdSense rates. For creators building a durable, ad-monetized library, that funnel is YouTube's strongest structural advantage. ## Our Verdict: Which Platform Should You Choose?

### Choose TikTok if:

- You want the fastest path to viral reach and audience growth
- You can consistently produce original, 1-minute-plus videos that qualify for Creator Rewards' $0.50-$2.00 RPM
- Your content is entertainment-focused, trend-driven, or personality-based
- You want to leverage TikTok Shop and LIVE gifts for extra income
- You thrive on high-volume posting (1-3 videos per day)

### Choose YouTube Shorts if:

- You want to monetize earlier — 1,000 subscribers is a lower bar than 10,000 followers plus 100K monthly views
- You plan to build a long-form YouTube channel alongside Shorts
- Your content is educational, tutorial-based, or search-friendly
- You want Google and YouTube search discoverability and a mature monetization ecosystem
- You prefer a slower, more sustainable posting cadence

### The Best Strategy: Do Both, but Lead With One

The highest-earning creators in 2026 publish on both platforms. Use TikTok for rapid audience growth and its higher qualified-view RPM, then funnel viewers to YouTube for long-form monetization and search-driven staying power. Export clean files with no visible TikTok watermark (YouTube demotes watermarked Shorts) and re-title each upload for search. ## FAQ

### Which platform pays more per view in 2026 — TikTok or YouTube Shorts?

Per qualified short-form view, TikTok. Creator Rewards pays an estimated $0.50-$2.00 per 1,000 qualified views versus roughly $0.04-$0.10 per 1,000 on Shorts. But TikTok's rate applies only to original videos over one minute, and YouTube's long-form ads ($2.00-$4.00 per 1,000 views) give Shorts creators a higher total earnings ceiling once viewers convert to channel subscribers.

### What are the monetization requirements for each platform?

TikTok Creator Rewards requires 10,000 followers, 100,000 video views in the last 30 days, age 18+ (19 in South Korea), and a Personal Account — Business Accounts are ineligible. YouTube Shorts revenue sharing requires 1,000 subscribers plus 10 million Shorts views in 90 days. YouTube's subscriber bar is lower; its view requirement is steeper for small channels.

### Why do so many creators say TikTok barely pays?

Most of those reports predate Creator Rewards or come from videos that never qualified for it — sub-minute clips, Duets, Stitches, or photo posts that fell back to the legacy Creator Fund's $0.02-$0.05 per 1,000 views. On original, 1-minute-plus content, Creator Rewards pays 10-40x more than the old fund.

### Should I post the same video on both platforms?

Yes, but lead with one platform and adapt each upload. Multi-platform creators consistently out-earn single-platform creators at the same audience size. Keep videos original and at least one minute long so they still qualify for TikTok's Creator Rewards, remove any TikTok watermark before uploading to YouTube, and re-title your Shorts for search.
