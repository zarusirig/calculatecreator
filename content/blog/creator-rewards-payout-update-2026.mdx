TikTok's Creator Rewards Program pays an estimated **$0.50 to $2.00 per 1,000 qualified views** in the US in 2026 — roughly **$500 to $2,000 per million qualified views**, or about **10 to 40 times** the rate of the discontinued legacy Creator Fund. TikTok publishes no official rate card, so treat every figure on this page as a planning estimate built from creators' reported earnings and [TikTok's official Creator Rewards documentation](https://support.tiktok.com/en/business-and-creator/creator-rewards-program/creator-rewards-program), not a guaranteed rate.

That single shift — from the Fund's **$0.02–$0.05** per 1,000 views to Creator Rewards' **$0.50–$2.00** per 1,000 *qualified* views — is the real 2026 story for TikTok's built-in monetization. Below we break the current rates down by niche and country, model what different view counts earn, and cover who qualifies, all using published ranges rather than invented precision.

*Last updated: July 2026. Figures are planning estimates, not guarantees — confirm current rates and thresholds in your own TikTok Studio dashboard.*

## What Creator Rewards Pays in 2026

The Creator Rewards Program is TikTok's current in-feed monetization program and the successor to the original Creator Fund, which is now discontinued. Instead of splitting a fixed pool, Creator Rewards pays **per qualified view**, so earnings scale with performance rather than with a shrinking shared budget.

Your payout for a video is built from two parts, per [TikTok's Creator Rewards support page](https://support.tiktok.com/en/business-and-creator/creator-rewards-program/creator-rewards-program):

- **Standard Reward** — qualified views multiplied by your effective RPM. This is the bulk of most creators' earnings.
- **Additional Reward** — a top-up tied to content quality, engagement, and audience or niche factors. Strong retention and originality push this higher.

Two numbers anchor the current US picture:

- **$0.50–$2.00 per 1,000 qualified views** (planning range; finance and tech can run higher).
- **~$500–$2,000 per 1,000,000 qualified views.**

TikTok adjusts effective rates over time as advertiser demand and ad-targeting value shift — RPM tends to soften after the Q1 seasonal dip and firm up toward Q4. Because there is no published rate card, the only reliable read on your own rate is your TikTok Studio earnings divided by your qualified views. TikTok's [Creator Academy explainer](https://www.tiktok.com/creator-academy/en/articles/monetize/creator-rewards) describes the same two-part reward structure.

### Qualified views, not raw views

A "qualified view" is not the same as a view in your analytics. Per TikTok, it is a **unique For You feed view watched for more than 5 seconds**, excluding paid, promoted, fraudulent, and repeat views, plus anything a viewer marked "not interested." A video also has to clear **1,000 qualified For You feed views** before it earns anything at all. In practice only a portion of raw views qualify, which is the single biggest reason real payouts land below back-of-envelope estimates.

## Creator Fund vs Creator Rewards: What Actually Changed

The headline 2026 change is not a specific rate tweak — it is that the legacy Creator Fund is fully discontinued and Creator Rewards has taken its place across most markets. Here is the side-by-side:

| Feature | Legacy Creator Fund | Creator Rewards Program |
|---|---|---|
| Status in 2026 | Discontinued (historical) | Active |
| Pay model | Fixed shared pool | Performance-based, no fixed pool |
| US RPM | $0.02–$0.05 / 1K views | $0.50–$2.00 / 1K qualified views |
| Per 1M views | ~$20–$50 | ~$500–$2,000 |

## Who Qualifies for Creator Rewards in 2026

Eligibility is set by TikTok and confirmed on its [official support page](https://support.tiktok.com/en/business-and-creator/creator-rewards-program/creator-rewards-program):

- **10,000 followers**
- **100,000 video views in the last 30 days**
- **Age 18+** (19 in South Korea)
- **A Personal Account in good standing** — Business, political, and government accounts are **not** eligible
- **An eligible region**
- **Original, public videos at least 1 minute long** — Duets, Stitches, reposts, watermarked clips, and slideshow or text-only content do not qualify as original

Apply in-app under **Profile → Menu → TikTok Studio → Creator Rewards Program**; TikTok typically replies within about three days, and only videos uploaded after you join can collect rewards. ## How to Maximize Your Creator Rewards Earnings

- **Lean into high-RPM niches.** Finance, tech, and education earn multiples of what comedy or entertainment do per qualified view, so specializing in a high-value category pays.
- **Make every video at least a minute — and hold attention.** The 60-second minimum is a hard floor to earn; watch time within the video drives the Additional Reward, so aim for 1–3 minutes with a strong hook and payoff.
- **Optimize for qualified views, not vanity views.** Only unique For You feed views over 5 seconds count, so a strong opening protects your payout — reach outside the For You feed does not earn.
- **Prioritize high-value audiences.** US, UK, Canadian, and Australian viewers earn more per view; knowing your geographic mix helps you set realistic expectations.
- **Post consistently and keep it original.** Irregular posting shrinks distribution and qualified views, and non-original formats are excluded outright.

## FAQ

### Is the TikTok Creator Fund still available in 2026?

No. The legacy Creator Fund is discontinued and has been superseded by the Creator Rewards Program in most markets. Its $0.02–$0.05 RPM is now only a historical baseline — you cannot sign up for the Fund today.

### How much does TikTok pay per 1,000 views in 2026?

Under Creator Rewards, an estimated $0.50–$2.00 per 1,000 *qualified* views in the US, with finance and tech niches running higher. There is no official rate card, so this is a planning range from creator-reported earnings, and it applies to qualified For You feed views, not raw views.

### How much does TikTok pay for 1 million views?

Roughly $500–$2,000 for 1,000,000 qualified US views at the $0.50–$2.00 range. High-RPM niches can exceed that; low-RPM niches and non-US audiences earn less. Remember that only qualified views count toward the total.

### How many followers and views do you need to join the Creator Rewards Program?

10,000 followers and 100,000 video views in the last 30 days, plus an age of 18+ (19 in South Korea), a Personal Account in good standing, and an eligible region. Business and political or government accounts are not eligible.

### Do TikTok videos have to be longer than 1 minute to earn money?

Yes. Creator Rewards only counts qualified views on original, public videos that are at least 60 seconds long. Shorter clips can still go viral, but they do not generate Creator Rewards earnings.

### Why did my Creator Rewards RPM change?

Effective RPM moves with advertiser demand, your niche and audience mix, watch time, and the qualified-view share of your traffic. Rates commonly soften after Q1 and firm up toward Q4, and TikTok does not guarantee any fixed rate — track your own TikTok Studio earnings divided by qualified views to see your real number.
