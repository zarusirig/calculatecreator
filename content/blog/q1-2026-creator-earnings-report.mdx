## Key Findings

The TikTok earnings picture for Q1 2026 is anchored by one number: the Creator Rewards Program pays $0.50 to $2.00 per 1,000 qualified views (US baseline), and rates have kept climbing as the program has matured. This analysis is built from TikTok's official program documentation, the published RPM ranges across our calculators, and industry ad-spend reports for the quarter (data last verified March 2026) — not a proprietary survey. Every dollar figure below is an estimate, never a guaranteed payout.

Three trends define Q1 2026 creator earnings. First, the Creator Rewards Program — which replaced the Creativity Program Beta, itself the successor to the legacy Creator Fund — now pays roughly 10 to 40 times what the Creator Fund pays per 1,000 qualified views. Second, finance and technology niches continue to command the highest RPMs, in the $1.50-$3.00 and $1.00-$2.50 ranges respectively. Third, because RPM is set by where a video's viewers are located rather than where the creator lives, content watched by US audiences earns several times more per view than content watched in lower-CPM markets.

The most significant pattern holds steady: brand deals remain the single largest income source for full-time creators, ranking ahead of TikTok Shop, affiliate marketing, and the platform's own reward programs. Direct monetization through TikTok's programs stays a secondary income stream for most, but the gap narrows as the Creator Rewards Program matures.

### RPM Trends: January Through March 2026

RPM (revenue per mille, or per 1,000 views) is the single most important metric for understanding TikTok creator pay. The figures below are estimates modeled from TikTok's published Creator Rewards ranges and legacy Creator Fund rates — treat them as directional, not guaranteed. Every Creator Rewards RPM here sits inside the program's $0.50-$2.00 per-1,000-qualified-view band.

|---|---|---|---|---|
| January 2026 | $0.95 | $0.035 | $1.80 | $0.92 |

| March 2026 | $1.15 | $0.032 | $2.25 | $1.18 |
| Q1 2026 Average | $1.05 | $0.03 | $2.05 | $1.05 |

The Creator Rewards Program pays far more because it rewards longer content (at least 60 seconds) on qualified views, which carry higher-value ad placements. The [legacy Creator Fund](https://support.tiktok.com/en/business-and-creator/tiktok-creator-fund-us) keeps paying an average of about $0.03 per 1,000 views (a $0.02-$0.05 range) — a clear signal to migrate to Creator Rewards where it is offered, though the Fund is still active in some regions.

TikTok Pulse, which places creator content alongside premium brand advertising, is an ad-revenue-share program open only to top creators; its figures above are estimates for that premium tier. ## Methodology

This report is an analysis, not a proprietary survey. Every figure is an estimate built from published sources — we do not collect earnings screenshots or claim a private data set.

**Sources**: TikTok's official program documentation (the [Creator Rewards Program](https://support.tiktok.com/en/business-and-creator/creator-rewards-program/creator-rewards-program) and [Creator Fund](https://support.tiktok.com/en/business-and-creator/tiktok-creator-fund-us) support pages), the RPM ranges published across our calculators, and industry ad-spend reports for Q1 2026. Data last verified March 2026.

**RPM basis**: All Creator Rewards figures reflect qualified views — unique For You feed views on original videos of at least 60 seconds, excluding clips watched under five seconds, repeat views from the same account, and paid or promoted views. A video must reach 1,000 qualified views before it starts earning.

**Limitations**: These are directional estimates, not guaranteed payouts. Actual earnings vary widely by niche, audience location, posting frequency, and engagement, and no creator should treat any figure here as a promise. All values are in US dollars.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does TikTok pay per 1,000 views in Q1 2026?

It depends on the program. The Creator Rewards Program — which most eligible creators should use — pays $0.50 to $2.00 per 1,000 qualified views (US baseline), with US audiences at the top of that range. The legacy Creator Fund pays far less, around $0.02 to $0.05 per 1,000 views. RPM also varies by niche and by where your viewers are located.

### Why did the Creator Fund RPM decrease year-over-year?

The Creator Fund operates from a fixed daily pool that gets divided among all eligible creators. As more creators qualify, each individual share decreases. The Creator Rewards Program uses a different revenue-share model tied to actual ad revenue, which is why its RPM is increasing. TikTok has been encouraging migration away from the Creator Fund since mid-2025.

### Which TikTok niche pays the most in 2026?

Finance and investing pays the highest Creator Rewards RPM, roughly $1.50 to $3.00 per 1,000 qualified views, followed by technology ($1.00-$2.50) and education ($0.80-$2.00). Comedy and entertainment sit at the low end ($0.30-$1.00). However, the highest total monthly earnings often go to beauty and comedy creators, whose larger view counts outweigh their lower per-view rates.

### How do TikTok earnings compare to YouTube Shorts in Q1 2026?

YouTube Shorts pays roughly $0.04 to $0.10 per 1,000 views — the same low range as TikTok's Creator Fund, and below the Creator Rewards Program's $0.50 to $2.00 per 1,000 qualified views. YouTube's long-form AdSense content pays far more, about $2 to $4 per 1,000 views, which keeps long-form the more lucrative format for creators who produce both.
