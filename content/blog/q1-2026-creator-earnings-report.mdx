## Key Findings

The TikTok earnings report for Q1 2026 shows that average creator RPM across all monetization programs reached $1.05 per 1,000 views, a 22% increase compared to Q1 2025. This comprehensive analysis draws from self-reported earnings data across 4,200 creators in 18 countries, covering January through mid-February 2026 with projections through March.

Three trends define Q1 2026 creator earnings. First, the Creativity Program now pays 40% more than the legacy Creator Fund per 1,000 qualified views. Second, finance and technology niches continue to command the highest RPMs, reaching $1.80 and $1.65 respectively. Third, creators in the United States earn 2.3x more per view than the global average.

The most significant shift: brand deal revenue now accounts for 62% of total income for full-time creators, up from 55% in Q1 2025. Direct monetization through TikTok's own programs remains a secondary income stream for most, but the gap is narrowing as the Creativity Program matures.

## Methodology

This Q1 2026 earnings report aggregates data from multiple sources to provide the most accurate picture of TikTok creator income available.

**Sample size**: 4,200 creators across 18 countries who submitted verified earnings screenshots through our quarterly survey, supplemented by publicly available data from creator communities and platform announcements.

**Time period**: January 1 through February 15, 2026, with March projections based on seasonal patterns and confirmed advertiser spend data.

**RPM calculations**: All RPM figures represent net creator payouts (after TikTok's revenue share) per 1,000 qualified views. For the Creativity Program, only views on videos exceeding 60 seconds with more than 1,000 total views are counted.

**Limitations**: Self-reported data carries inherent biases. Higher-earning creators are more likely to participate in surveys. We apply statistical weighting to correct for this, but actual median earnings may be 10-15% lower than reported figures.

All currency figures are in US dollars. International earnings are converted at the exchange rate as of February 1, 2026.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does TikTok pay per 1,000 views in Q1 2026?

TikTok pays between $0.033 and $2.05 per 1,000 views depending on the monetization program. The legacy Creator Fund pays the lowest at $0.033 per 1,000 views, while TikTok Pulse pays the highest at $2.05. The Creativity Program, which most eligible creators should use, pays an average of $1.05 per 1,000 views in Q1 2026.

### Why did the Creator Fund RPM decrease year-over-year?

The Creator Fund operates from a fixed daily pool that gets divided among all eligible creators. As more creators qualify, each individual share decreases. The Creativity Program uses a different revenue-share model tied to actual ad revenue, which is why its RPM is increasing. TikTok has been encouraging migration away from the Creator Fund since mid-2025.

### Which TikTok niche pays the most in 2026?

Finance and investing content pays the highest RPM at $1.80 per 1,000 views on the Creativity Program, followed by technology at $1.65 and business at $1.45. However, the highest total monthly earnings often go to beauty and comedy creators who generate more than 3 million monthly views despite their lower per-view rates.

### How do TikTok earnings compare to YouTube Shorts in Q1 2026?

YouTube Shorts pays an average of $0.05-$0.07 per 1,000 views through its revenue sharing program, significantly lower than TikTok's Creativity Program average of $1.05 per 1,000 views. However, YouTube's long-form content pays $3-$8 per 1,000 views, making it more lucrative overall for creators who produce both formats.
