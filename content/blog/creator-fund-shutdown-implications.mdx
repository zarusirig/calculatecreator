## Why TikTok Ended the Creator Fund

TikTok's Creator Fund shutdown marks the platform's biggest monetization shift since the program launched in 2020 with a $200 million pool (later expanded toward $1 billion). The Fund paid **$0.02–$0.05 per 1,000 views**, splitting a fixed pool among a growing number of eligible accounts. As enrollment surged into the millions, individual payouts shrank each quarter — a fixed pool divided among more people means everyone earns less, regardless of quality.

By late 2023, many creators reported effective rates below $0.01 per 1,000 views, and TikTok began winding the program down. Its own [Creator Fund support page](https://support.tiktok.com/en/business-and-creator/creator-fund-us/creator-fund) now lists the Fund as no longer available. In its place, the [Creator Rewards Program](/guides/creator-rewards-program/) pays on qualifying content using originality, engagement, and retention signals rather than a fixed pool — a model much closer to how YouTube compensates creators.

## How the Creator Fund Originally Worked

The Fund ran on a fundamentally different model than its replacement. TikTok set aside a fixed dollar amount and divided it among eligible creators; your share depended on your percentage of total qualifying views across every Fund member. That created a zero-sum dynamic: when new creators joined, the pool did not grow proportionally, so each person's slice shrank. A creator earning $0.04 per 1,000 views in early 2021 might see $0.015 by mid-2023 with identical performance, simply because the denominator grew.

The eligibility bar was low — 10,000 followers, 100,000 views in 30 days, and age 18+ — which brought millions into the Fund and accelerated the dilution.

## The Shutdown Timeline

The transition rolled out in stages across regions and account tiers.

| Date | Event |
|---|---|
| February 2023 | Creator Rewards (then "Creativity Program Beta") launched in the US |
| May 2023 | Beta expanded to the UK, Germany, and France |
| August 2023 | Open enrollment began for eligible creators |
| December 2023 | TikTok announced Creator Fund deprecation |
| March 2024 | New Creator Fund enrollments closed |
| Q3 2024 | Final Creator Fund payouts distributed |
| 2025–2026 | Creator Rewards became the sole per-view program |

Migration was not automatic. Each creator had to manually accept the new terms, verify eligibility, and agree to content guidelines that emphasize originality over virality.

## How Earnings Changed After the Transition

Creator Rewards pays far more than the Fund for content that meets its quality signals. Creator-reported RPM runs **$0.50–$2.00 per 1,000 qualified views** — roughly 10–40x the old Fund — versus the Fund's $0.02–$0.05.

| Metric | Creator Fund | Creator Rewards |
|---|---|---|
| RPM range | $0.02–$0.05 | $0.50–$2.00+ |
| Pay model | Fixed pool split | Performance-based |
| Minimum video length | None | 1 minute |
| Originality requirement | Low | High |
| Account type | Personal or Business | Personal only |

The catch: only original videos longer than one minute qualify. Short clips under 60 seconds — which dominated the Fund era — earn nothing under the new program, which pushed creators toward longer, more substantive content.

Educational, tutorial, and review creators gained the most: their content naturally runs past one minute and scores well on quality signals, and niches like finance, cooking, and tech have reported the largest per-view gains. Dance, lip-sync, and short-trend creators were hit hardest — their sub-30-second clips are locked out entirely, making brand deals and LIVE gifting their primary paths.

## Migration Steps: What to Do Now

Creator Rewards has its own eligibility bar, and being a former Fund member grants no automatic access. Per [TikTok's official program page](https://support.tiktok.com/en/business-and-creator/creator-rewards-program/creator-rewards-program), you need:

- **10,000+ followers**
- **100,000+ video views** in the last 30 days
- **Age 18+** (19 in South Korea)
- **A Personal account** in good standing — Business and government/political accounts are not eligible
- **Residence in an eligible country** (US, UK, Germany, France, and others)
- **Original videos at least one minute long**

To enroll: open **Profile → Menu → TikTok Studio → Creator Rewards Program**, review and accept the terms, then post 1-minute-plus original videos to start earning. Rewards are not retroactive — only videos posted after you join can earn, and each must clear 1,000 qualified views first.

The financial impact varied widely by creator size and content type. The illustrative ranges below assume the creator posts qualifying (1-minute-plus, original) content; they are estimates built from published RPM ranges, not guarantees, and creators who kept posting only short clips saw platform income fall to zero.

## Strategies to Maximize Creator Rewards Earnings

- **Answer specific questions.** Content that resolves a clear query ("how to remove stains from white sneakers") tends to hold attention and earn more than generic entertainment clips.
- **Extend length strategically.** One minute is the floor, not the ceiling; 2–5 minute videos with strong retention earn the most, but add depth, not filler.
- **Prioritize quality over volume.** Creator Rewards weights quality signals heavily, so one well-produced daily video outperforms five rushed clips.
- **Diversify beyond platform pay.** Even at improved RPMs, rewards alone rarely fund a full-time income — combine them with brand deals, affiliate marketing, and LIVE gifting.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I still join the Creator Fund in 2026?

No. TikTok closed new Creator Fund enrollments in March 2024 and distributed final payouts by Q3 2024. The Creator Rewards Program is the only per-view content payment program now.

### What happens to my unpaid Creator Fund balance?

TikTok disbursed remaining Fund balances during the shutdown. If you had a balance and never received it, contact TikTok Creator Support through the app with your payment records.

### Do I need to reapply if I was already in the Creator Fund?

Yes. Creator Rewards has separate eligibility and a distinct enrollment process. You must independently meet the 10,000-follower and 100,000-view thresholds and apply through TikTok Studio.

### Why are my Creator Rewards earnings lower than expected?

Videos under one minute earn nothing, and low originality, weak retention, or a shift toward lower-RPM audiences all reduce your rate. Remember that only qualified For You feed views (watched over five seconds) count, and a video earns only after 1,000 qualified views.
