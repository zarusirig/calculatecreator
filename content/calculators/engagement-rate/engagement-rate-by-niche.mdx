TikTok engagement rates vary dramatically by niche in 2026. Pets (7.2%), comedy (6.8%), and gaming (5.5%) dominate with the highest average engagement, while tech (3.2%) and beauty (3.5%) sit at the lower end. Across all niches, the platform-wide average TikTok engagement rate is 4.85%, calculated from likes, comments, and shares divided by views. These benchmarks, drawn from analysis of over 50,000 TikTok accounts across 10 major content categories, give creators the baseline they need to evaluate whether their content is underperforming, on track, or outpacing their competition within a specific vertical.

Second, niche engagement rates do not correlate with follower counts the way most creators expect. This suggests TikTok's algorithm increasingly rewards content that generates rapid, reflexive interactions over content that requires deliberate consumption.

### Beauty

Beauty content on TikTok averages a 3.5% engagement rate in 2026, placing it ninth out of ten major niches. This might surprise creators given beauty's massive audience on the platform, but the lower rate has a structural explanation: beauty is one of TikTok's most saturated categories.

With millions of beauty creators posting daily, the average video competes against an enormous content pool. Viewers have more options, which disperses engagement across a wider range of creators. The likes-per-view ratio of 2.4% reflects that viewers often watch beauty tutorials without interacting — they consume the information passively.

Beauty creators who beat the 3.5% average tend to share one trait: they produce content with a clear transformation or surprise element. Before-and-after reveals, unexpected product reactions, and technique demonstrations that produce dramatic results consistently outperform standard product reviews. Comments per view (0.5%) stay relatively low because beauty content is often instructional rather than conversational. Creators who ask direct questions — "Which look should I do next?" or "Has anyone else tried this?" — see comments jump to 0.8-1.0%.

For beauty creators looking to improve, the key lever is shares. At 0.6% shares per view, there is significant room to grow by creating content people want to forward to friends — relatable skincare struggles, unexpected drugstore finds, or side-by-side product comparisons.

### Finance

Finance content averages 5.2% engagement, making it the fourth-highest niche despite being primarily informational. The standout metric is comments per view at 1.2%, the highest of any niche. Finance is inherently opinion-driven — every investing tip, budgeting method, or economic prediction invites disagreement or personal anecdotes.

This comment-heavy engagement pattern is valuable for the algorithm. TikTok treats comments as a strong signal of content relevance and interest, so finance creators benefit from a feedback loop: high comments trigger broader distribution, which brings in more viewers who also comment. Understanding [what qualifies as a good engagement rate on TikTok](/calculators/engagement-rate/good-tiktok-engagement-rate/) matters here, because finance creators should benchmark against their niche average rather than the platform-wide number.

Shares per view (1.0%) are also strong. Viewers frequently send finance content to friends and family, especially content about savings hacks, tax tips, or investment warnings. Likes per view (3.0%) trail behind entertainment niches because finance content requires more cognitive processing — viewers need to think about the information before reacting.

Finance creators who exceed the 5.2% average typically use storytelling formats. Personal stories about debt payoff, investment wins and losses, or financial mistakes resonate more than pure data presentations. The emotional component drives the like and share numbers up while maintaining the strong comment engagement.

## TikTok Engagement Rate by Niche — Extended Data

Beyond the top-level averages, the data reveals important variation within each niche based on content format, posting time, and account age.

**Content format impact by niche.** Duets and stitches perform differently across niches. In comedy, duets boost engagement by an average of 1.4 percentage points over solo content. In beauty, stitches that react to product claims outperform standard tutorials by 0.9 points. Gaming clips using the green screen effect for commentary see a 1.1-point lift over raw gameplay footage. Creators in every niche should test collaborative and reactive formats against their standard content.

**Posting time variation.** Finance content performs best between 7-9 AM and 6-8 PM, aligning with commute times when viewers check financial news. Fitness content peaks at 5-7 AM and 4-6 PM, matching workout schedules. Comedy and pets content show flatter distribution throughout the day, suggesting these niches are less time-sensitive. Education content spikes on weekday evenings between 7-10 PM, when viewers are in a learning mindset after work or school.

**Seasonal trends.** Fitness engagement spikes 35% in January due to New Year's resolutions. Finance engagement peaks in March-April during tax season, jumping 28% above baseline. Beauty engagement rises 22% in October-November around holiday party season. Gaming engagement surges 40% during major game releases and E3 coverage periods.

**Account age patterns.** Newer accounts (under 6 months) across all niches show engagement rates approximately 15-25% higher than established accounts. This reflects TikTok's documented new-account boost. However, this advantage fades by month 8-12. Creators who are concerned about declining numbers as their account matures should review our analysis of [why TikTok engagement rates drop](/calculators/engagement-rate/engagement-rate-dropping/) and the common causes behind that trend.

**Cross-niche opportunities.** The highest-engagement content often blends niches. Fitness + comedy hybrids average 5.8% engagement, outperforming both niches individually. Finance + education content averages 5.6%. Pet + comedy content reaches 7.8%. Creators willing to experiment at the intersection of two niches frequently outperform those who stay strictly within one lane.

## Methodology

This analysis covers data collected between January 2025 and January 2026 from a sample of 50,000 TikTok accounts across 10 content niches. The methodology was designed to produce benchmarks that reflect real-world creator performance rather than skewed outlier-driven averages.

**Sample selection.** Accounts were categorized into niches based on their primary content focus, determined by analyzing bio keywords, hashtag usage patterns, and content classification across their most recent 50 posts. Accounts that posted across multiple niches with no clear primary category were excluded. Each niche includes a minimum of 3,000 accounts.

**Follower distribution.** The sample was stratified across follower tiers to prevent bias toward any single account size. Each niche includes accounts from five follower brackets: nano (1K-10K), micro (10K-50K), mid-tier (50K-500K), macro (500K-1M), and mega (1M+). Account distribution within each bracket was weighted to reflect the actual proportion of active creators at each tier.

**Engagement rate calculation.** Engagement rate for each account was calculated as: (likes + comments + shares) / views x 100. This was computed across each account's 30 most recent posts, then averaged. Posts older than 90 days at time of data collection were excluded to prevent recency bias from affecting the results.

**Outlier treatment.** Accounts with engagement rates above the 99th percentile or below the 1st percentile within each niche were excluded. This removes viral anomalies and inactive accounts that would distort niche averages. Accounts with fewer than 10 posts in the analysis window were also excluded.

**Data refresh cadence.** These benchmarks represent a rolling 12-month analysis. Niche averages are updated quarterly. The figures published here reflect the Q1 2026 update cycle.

**Limitations.** TikTok does not provide a public API for engagement data at this granularity. Data was collected through a combination of public profile analysis and third-party analytics platforms with creator-authorized access. Private accounts, region-locked content, and accounts with disabled comment sections were excluded. These benchmarks represent global averages and may differ significantly by geographic market.
