TikTok's legacy Creator Fund paid an estimated **$0.02-$0.05 per 1,000 views** — about $0.00002 to $0.00005 per individual view, or $20 to $50 per million. The current Creator Rewards Program, which replaced it, pays a planning estimate of **$0.50-$2.00 per 1,000 qualified views** (roughly $500 to $2,000 per million), so a view is worth 10 to 40 times more today than it was under the old Fund.

The Creator Fund is now discontinued, so its per-view rates are historical reference points only. This page breaks down what each program pays per view, how audience location and niche move that number, and how to model your own rate — using [TikTok's official Creator Rewards documentation](https://support.tiktok.com/en/business-and-creator/creator-rewards-program/creator-rewards-program) and published RPM ranges. TikTok does not publish a rate card, so every dollar figure here is an estimate, not a guarantee.

## Current Payment Rates

TikTok has paid creators for in-feed views through two programs, and the per-view rates between them differ by a factor of 10 to 40. Knowing which set of numbers applies to you is the first step toward a realistic income goal.

**Creator Fund rates (legacy, discontinued):** The original TikTok Creator Fund paid between $0.02 and $0.05 per 1,000 views — $0.00002 to $0.00005 per individual view. At that rate a video with 1 million views earned roughly $20 to $50. The Fund ran on a fixed-pool model: TikTok allocated a set amount of money each day and split it among all participants by their share of total views. As more creators joined, individual payouts shrank — a structural flaw that drove years of frustration and is a core reason [Creator Fund earnings kept dropping](/guides/tiktok-creator-fund/). Per TikTok's own [Creator Fund support page](https://support.tiktok.com/en/business-and-creator/creator-fund-us/creator-fund), the program is no longer available, so these rates are a baseline for comparison only.

**Creator Rewards rates (current):** TikTok's Creator Rewards Program (the program it tested as the Creativity Program Beta) pays an estimated $0.50 to $2.00 per 1,000 qualified views. Independent trackers report typical accounts landing lower — [FlowShorts reports $0.50-$1.00+ per 1,000 qualified views](https://flowshorts.app/blog/tiktok-creator-fund) — so treat the top of the range as a ceiling for strong niches, not a default. That same 1-million-view video now earns roughly $500 to $2,000 *if every view qualifies* — a dramatic jump from the Fund.

The critical word is "qualified." Creator Rewards does not count every view. A qualified view is a unique view from the For You feed watched for more than 5 seconds; paid, promoted, fraudulent, and "not interested" views are excluded, and repeat views from the same account count once. On top of that, videos must be at least one minute long, and each video must cross 1,000 qualified views before it earns anything at all. So a one-minute video where most viewers watch to the end earns far more per raw view than one where they drop off at the 15-second mark.

### Per-view, per-1K, and per-1M at a glance

| Program | Per view | Per 1,000 views | Per 1,000,000 views |
|---|---|---|---|
| Creator Fund (legacy, discontinued) | $0.00002 - $0.00005 | $0.02 - $0.05 | $20 - $50 |
| Creator Rewards (current) | $0.0005 - $0.002 | $0.50 - $2.00 | $500 - $2,000 |

*Creator Rewards figures assume qualified views. Because only a share of raw views qualify, model your real payout on your qualified-view count, not your total view count.*

## Factors That Affect Per-View Pay

Your per-view rate is not fixed. It moves with several variables you can influence through content strategy.

### Audience Location

Where your viewers live is the single largest factor in your per-view rate. Views from the United States, United Kingdom, Canada, Australia, and Western Europe command the highest payouts because advertisers pay premium CPMs in those markets. A creator whose audience is 80% US-based earns substantially more per view than one with the same view count concentrated in South and Southeast Asia, Latin America, or Africa.

The gap is real. Using the US as a 1.0x baseline, published regional multipliers put other markets well below it:

| Region | Multiplier vs US | Est. Fund RPM (per 1K views) |
|---|---|---|
| United States | 1.0x | $0.02 - $0.05 |
| United Kingdom | 0.9x | $0.018 - $0.045 |
| Canada | 0.85x | $0.017 - $0.043 |
| Australia | 0.8x | $0.016 - $0.04 |
| EU (average) | 0.75x | $0.015 - $0.038 |
| Asia | 0.6x | $0.012 - $0.03 |
| MENA | 0.55x | $0.011 - $0.028 |
| LATAM | 0.5x | $0.01 - $0.025 |
| Africa | 0.3x | $0.006 - $0.015 |

A US view can be worth roughly three times an African view and about twice a Latin American one — and the same multipliers scale Creator Rewards rates too. If you earn less than expected despite strong view counts, audience geography is the likeliest reason; our [average earnings per views data](/data/earnings/average-tiktok-earnings-per-views/) shows how the spread plays out in practice.

### Content Niche

Advertisers pay different rates by category, and Creator Rewards passes that through to you. Finance, technology, business, and education content attracts higher-paying advertisers, which lifts per-view rates; comedy, entertainment, and general lifestyle earn less per view but often accumulate views faster. Here are planning estimates for Creator Rewards RPM by niche:

|---|---|

Under Creator Rewards, TikTok also weighs the "search value" of your content — whether it answers questions people actively look for. High-search-value videos tend to earn better because TikTok can serve them over weeks and months, generating sustained qualified views instead of a single spike. See [what TikTok RPM actually measures](/calculators/rpm/what-is-tiktok-rpm/) for how these rates are built up.

### Video Length and Watch Time

Video length drives both eligibility and earning potential under Creator Rewards. One minute is the floor, but creators who produce content in the 2-5 minute range often see the highest RPMs, because longer videos create more room for sustained watch time — and the payout formula rewards videos that hold attention.

Watch time matters more than raw length. A three-minute video that loses viewers after 30 seconds earns less per view than a 90-second video with a 75% completion rate. Remember the mechanic: a view only becomes a qualified view once someone watches past 5 seconds, so the first few seconds decide whether a view counts at all.

### Engagement Quality

Comments, shares, saves, and likes feed into how TikTok evaluates content for monetization. Our modelling applies an engagement multiplier to base RPM using the standard formula **(likes + comments + shares + saves) ÷ views × 100**:

| Engagement rate | Multiplier on base RPM |
|---|---|
| Excellent (10%+) | 1.5x |
| Good (5-10%) | 1.2x |
| Average (2-5%) | 1.0x |
| Below average (under 2%) | 0.8x |

The platform-wide average engagement rate is about 4.07%, which sits in the "average" band. Shares and saves carry particular weight because they signal content valuable enough to revisit or pass along.

## How to Estimate Your Own Per-View Rate

Because TikTok publishes no rate card, the reliable way to project earnings is to build your own effective RPM from the factors above rather than trusting a single headline number. Here is the four-step method our calculators use, framed so you can run it by hand.

**Step 1: Convert raw views to qualified views.** Only unique For You feed views watched past 5 seconds count under Creator Rewards. Across most accounts, roughly 40-70% of raw views qualify once repeat views, quick scroll-bys, and paid or promoted views are stripped out. If you do not know your share, start at 50%.

**Step 2: Pick your niche RPM.** Use the niche range above — for example finance at $1.50-$3.00 or comedy at $0.30-$0.80 per 1,000 qualified views. Take the midpoint unless your own analytics let you narrow it.

**Step 3: Apply your audience-geography multiplier.** Weight the niche RPM by where your viewers live using the regional multipliers (US 1.0x down to Africa 0.3x). A 70% US / 30% EU audience lands around 0.93x.

**Step 4: Apply an engagement multiplier.** Excellent engagement (10%+) multiplies base RPM by 1.5x; good (5-10%) by 1.2x; average (2-5%) by 1.0x; below 2% by 0.8x.

**Worked example.** A finance video pulls 400,000 raw views from a 70% US / 30% EU audience with 6% engagement. Estimate qualified views at 60% → 240,000. Take a mid finance RPM of $2.00, apply the 0.93x geography weight → about $1.85, then the 1.2x "good engagement" multiplier → about $2.22 per 1,000 qualified views. Earnings ≈ 240,000 ÷ 1,000 × $2.22 = **$533**, before any Additional Reward for content quality. TikTok's [Creator Academy explainer](https://www.tiktok.com/creator-academy/en/articles/monetize/creator-rewards) describes that Standard-plus-Additional Reward structure in its own terms. ## Creator Fund vs Creator Rewards Rates

The difference between the two programs is not incremental — it is transformational for creator income.

**Creator Fund (legacy, discontinued):** $0.02-$0.05 per 1,000 views. Fixed daily pool shared among all participants. No minimum video length. Payments shrank as more creators joined. Discontinued and replaced by Creator Rewards.

**Creator Rewards (current):** an estimated $0.50-$2.00 per 1,000 qualified views. No fixed pool — pay scales with each video's performance. Requires original videos of at least one minute. Weighs qualified views, watch time, search value, and originality.

At Fund rates you needed roughly 20 to 50 million views a month to earn $1,000. At Creator Rewards rates you need roughly 500,000 to 2 million qualified views for the same $1,000. That gap is the difference between TikTok being a hobby and a viable income source. For a fuller side-by-side, see our [Creator Rewards vs Creator Fund comparison](/calculators/tiktok-creator-fund/creator-rewards-vs-creator-fund/).

For context beyond TikTok, YouTube's long-form ad revenue pays an estimated $2.00-$4.00 per 1,000 views — our house benchmark is that YouTube pays roughly 50-100x more per view than the legacy Fund did. Creator Rewards' estimated $0.50-$2.00 narrows that gap from a hundredfold to a few times. But YouTube's rate applies to a different format — long-form watch-page views with mid-roll ads — so it is not an apples-to-apples per-view swap, and TikTok's feed typically builds qualifying view volume faster for a new account.

### Common mistakes reading your per-view rate

- **Dividing earnings by raw views.** Under Creator Rewards, divide by *qualified* views to get your true RPM — raw-view math understates it.
- **Comparing to the old Fund's $0.02-$0.05.** Those rates are discontinued; benchmark against $0.50-$2.00, not the Fund.
- **Assuming one viral video sets your rate.** RPM varies video to video with niche, geography, and watch time. Average across several videos before drawing conclusions.
- **Ignoring the 1,000-qualified-view threshold.** A video earns nothing until it clears 1,000 qualified For You feed views, so a low-reach upload can post a $0 RPM that is not representative of your account.

## How to Maximize Per-View Earnings

Maximizing your per-view rate takes deliberate decisions about content, audience, and program participation. These strategies apply to the Creator Rewards Program, where the meaningful earnings are.

**Join Creator Rewards as soon as you qualify.** The Creator Fund is closed, so the practical move is meeting the Creator Rewards bar: 10,000 followers, 100,000 views in the last 30 days, a Personal account in good standing, and being 18 or older (19 in South Korea). Check where you stand with our [eligibility breakdown](/calculators/tiktok-creator-fund/creator-fund-eligibility-2026/).

**Target Tier 1 audiences.** Post during US and UK peak hours, use English captions, and reference cultural touchpoints that resonate with North American and European audiences. Every point you shift your audience toward high-CPM countries lifts your average RPM.

**Create content over one minute.** This is non-negotiable for Creator Rewards eligibility. Beyond the minimum, aim for 2-3 minutes when the topic supports it — longer content that holds watch time earns the highest per-view rates.

**Optimize for watch time, not just views.** A hook that holds viewers past the 5-second mark (the point a view qualifies), a clear narrative arc, and a reason to finish beat a clickbait open that generates clicks but not completions. A video with 100,000 views at 80% average watch time can out-earn one with 500,000 views at 15%.

**Build search value into your content.** Videos that answer specific questions — how-tos, tutorials, explainers — tend to earn higher RPMs because TikTok can surface them for months, generating sustained qualified views.

**Post consistently.** Aim for 4-5 videos a week. Regular publishing trains distribution and sustains the watch-time and engagement metrics that drive per-view pay. ## FAQ

### How much does TikTok pay per 1,000 views in 2026?

The current Creator Rewards Program pays an estimated $0.50-$2.00 per 1,000 qualified views. The legacy Creator Fund paid $0.02-$0.05 per 1,000 views, but it is discontinued, so those rates are historical only. Neither is a guaranteed rate — TikTok publishes no rate card, and actual pay depends on niche, audience geography, and watch time.

### How much does TikTok pay for 1 million views?

Under Creator Rewards, an estimated $500-$2,000 per million *qualified* views. Under the old Fund it was about $20-$50 per million. Because only a share of raw views qualify (unique For You feed views watched past 5 seconds), a million raw views typically pays below the top of the Creator Rewards range.

### Why is my TikTok per-view rate so low?

Usually audience geography or view quality. Views from lower-CPM regions such as Asia, LATAM, and Africa pay a fraction of US views, and under Creator Rewards only qualified views earn — so a high raw-view count with low watch time or many non-qualifying views produces a low effective per-view rate.

### Does TikTok pay more per view for longer videos?

Indirectly. Creator Rewards only pays on videos of at least one minute, and longer videos create more room for the sustained watch time the payout formula rewards. But a padded long video with poor retention earns less than a tight one-minute video with high completion — watch time matters more than length itself.

### Is the Creator Fund still paying per view in 2026?

No. The Creator Fund is discontinued and closed to new creators. TikTok moved in-feed view monetization to the Creator Rewards Program, which pays substantially more per qualified view but requires original videos over one minute.

### How do I calculate my own TikTok per-view rate?

Start with your qualified views (estimate 40-70% of raw views), multiply by a niche RPM from the ranges above, then adjust for audience geography (US 1.0x down to Africa 0.3x) and engagement (0.8x to 1.5x). For example, 240,000 qualified finance views at an effective $2.22 RPM works out to about $533 before any Additional Reward. Because TikTok publishes no rate card, treat the result as a planning estimate and refine it against your own analytics over several videos.
