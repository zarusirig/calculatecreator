The legacy TikTok Creator Fund paid creators monthly, with a $10 minimum payout sent by PayPal or bank transfer — but that program is discontinued, so no new Creator Fund payments are being scheduled. Its replacement, the Creator Rewards Program, accrues earnings daily, holds them through a short validation window, and lets you withdraw via PayPal or bank transfer through Creator Tools on a roughly monthly cycle. This page explains both schedules — the historical Fund cycle and the current Creator Rewards flow — plus the most common reasons a TikTok payment runs late and how to fix them.

One honesty note up front: TikTok does not publish an official payout calendar for Creator Rewards. The timing patterns below combine TikTok's own documentation with what creators consistently report. Always confirm your balance, threshold, and payout status inside the app.

## Payment Schedule at a Glance

| Program | Status in 2026 | Payment cycle | Minimum payout | Methods |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Creator Fund (legacy) | Discontinued | Monthly (historical) | $10 | PayPal, bank transfer |
| Creator Rewards | Active | Daily accrual, roughly monthly withdrawal | Confirm in-app (creators commonly report $10) | PayPal, bank transfer via Creator Tools |
| LIVE gifts (diamonds) | Active, separate system | Withdraw on demand | $1 new creators / $10 regular | Varies by region; 5–15 business days typical |

These are three different pipelines. A LIVE gift withdrawal arriving quickly tells you nothing about when your Creator Rewards balance will clear, and vice versa.

## How the Legacy Creator Fund Paid (Historical)

The original Creator Fund followed a simple monthly rhythm: earnings from a calendar month were totaled after the month closed, and creators could withdraw once their balance reached **$10**, via PayPal or direct bank deposit. Per [TikTok's Creator Fund support page](https://support.tiktok.com/en/business-and-creator/creator-fund-us/creator-fund), the program is no longer available — every number in this section is historical baseline, not something you can sign up for today.

The Fund's low rates made the $10 floor a real obstacle. At the legacy RPM of **$0.02–$0.05 per 1,000 views** (about $20–$50 per million views), the math looked like this:

- **300,000 monthly views**: 300 × $0.02–$0.05 = **$6.00–$15.00**. At the low end, you missed the $10 minimum and the balance rolled into the next month.
- **Views needed to reliably clear $10**: roughly **200,000–500,000 views every month**, depending on where your RPM landed in the range.

Balances did not expire — they carried forward until they crossed the threshold. But many smaller creators effectively got paid once every two or three months. ## How Creator Rewards Payments Work in 2026

The Creator Rewards Program replaced the Fund and changed both the pay rate and the payment mechanics. Three stages matter.

### Stage 1: Daily accrual

Earnings accrue daily as your videos collect qualified views. Under [TikTok's official Creator Rewards documentation](https://support.tiktok.com/en/business-and-creator/creator-rewards-program/creator-rewards-program), a qualified view is a unique video view from the For You feed watched for more than 5 seconds — excluding paid, promoted, fraudulent, and repeat views, plus views where someone tapped "not interested." A video must reach **1,000 qualified For You feed views** before it starts generating rewards at all, so a new upload can show thousands of raw views and still display $0.

### Stage 2: Validation window

Accrued earnings do not become withdrawable instantly. They pass through a validation period in which TikTok verifies that views were genuine and the content complies with program rules. This is the stage most creators misread as a "missing payment" — the money is visible as estimated earnings but has not yet cleared into your withdrawable balance.

### Stage 3: Withdrawal

Once validated, you withdraw through **Profile → TikTok Studio → Creator Rewards → Balance**, using PayPal or a linked bank account, on a roughly monthly cycle. TikTok does not commit to a fixed payout date, and thresholds and methods vary by region — creators commonly report a $10 minimum and mid-month arrivals, but treat that as a reported pattern, not a guarantee. Bank transfers usually take a few business days longer than PayPal to land.

At Creator Rewards rates of **$0.50–$2.00 per 1,000 qualified views** (a US planning estimate, not a promise), the minimum threshold is far easier to reach than it was under the Fund: **5,000–20,000 qualified views** is enough to generate about $10. Compare that with the 200,000–500,000 views the legacy Fund required for the same payout.

## Worked Example: From Views to Money in Your Account

Say your account generates **400,000 qualified views** in a month:

- **Standard reward estimate**: 400 × $0.50–$2.00 = **$200–$800** accrued for the month.
- **In a high-RPM niche** like finance (site planning range $1.50–$3.00 per 1,000 qualified views), the same views model out to **$600–$1,200**.
- Those earnings accrue daily, then pass validation before appearing as withdrawable balance.
- You request withdrawal to PayPal or bank; the transfer itself typically takes a few business days.

Two things commonly shrink the number people expect: only *qualified* views count (raw analytics views always run higher), and only videos posted **after** you joined the program earn — Creator Rewards is not retroactive.

## Common Payment Delays and How to Fix Them

| Issue | Likely cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| No payment available | Balance below the minimum threshold | Keep posting; balance carries forward |
| Earnings stuck as "estimated" | Still in the validation window | Wait for validation; check for policy notices |
| First payout delayed | Identity or tax verification incomplete | Finish ID verification in payment settings |
| Video shows views but $0 | Under 1,000 qualified views, posted before joining, or ineligible format | Check originality rules and post-join date |
| Withdrawal failed | Wrong PayPal email or bank details | Re-enter and verify payment details |
| No schedule at all | Ineligible account type — Creator Rewards requires a Personal Account (Business, political, and government accounts cannot earn) | Switch to a Personal Account and re-apply |
| Balance frozen | Community guideline or program violation | Review notices; appeal if you believe it is an error |

The single most common first-payment delay is **incomplete identity verification**. TikTok requires it before releasing money, and it can take several business days — complete it the day you join, not the week you expect a payout. If a payment is more than a week past its usual pattern, contact Creator Support through the in-app help center with your account details, the period in question, and the expected amount. ## What Actually Moves Your Payout Date

1. **Verify identity and payment details immediately** after acceptance — this removes the biggest first-payout bottleneck.
2. **Use PayPal if speed matters** — creators consistently report it clearing faster than bank transfers.
3. **Post 1-minute-plus original content** that satisfies program rules, so earnings do not get clawed back in validation.
4. **Watch your qualified-view count, not raw views** — it is the only number that feeds the payout math.
5. **Raise your RPM** so each cycle clears the threshold comfortably — see [how to maximize your RPM](/calculators/tiktok-creator-fund/maximize-creator-fund-rpm/) and our [guide to getting paid faster](/guides/monetization/get-paid-faster-tiktok/).

## FAQ

### When does TikTok pay Creator Rewards earnings?

Earnings accrue daily, pass a validation window, and become withdrawable on a roughly monthly cycle through Creator Tools. TikTok does not publish exact payout dates; creators commonly report mid-month arrivals, but you should confirm timing in your own Balance tab.

### What is the minimum payout on TikTok?

The legacy Creator Fund used a $10 minimum. For Creator Rewards, creators commonly report a $10 threshold, though TikTok's official documentation does not specify one globally — check your region's threshold in-app. LIVE gift withdrawals are separate: $1 (200 diamonds) for new creators, $10 (2,000 diamonds) for regular accounts.

### Is the Creator Fund still paying creators in 2026?

No. The Creator Fund is discontinued and has been superseded by the Creator Rewards Program in most markets. Its $0.02–$0.05 RPM figures are useful only as a historical baseline.

### How many views do I need to reach a $10 payout?

Under Creator Rewards, roughly 5,000–20,000 qualified views at the $0.50–$2.00 per-1,000 planning range. Under the legacy Fund, the same $10 took 200,000–500,000 views.

### Do LIVE gift withdrawals follow the same schedule?

No. Diamond withdrawals from LIVE gifts run on their own system — typically 5–15 business days to arrive, occasionally up to 21. Do not use LIVE payout speed to judge whether a Creator Rewards payment is late.

### Why is my payment late?

The usual culprits, in order: incomplete identity verification, earnings still in validation, a balance below the minimum, or incorrect PayPal/bank details. Work through the delay table above before contacting support.
