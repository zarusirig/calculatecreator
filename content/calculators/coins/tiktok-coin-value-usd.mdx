## TikTok Coin Value Breakdown

One TikTok coin is worth about **$0.0106 in US dollars** — just over one cent — based on the standard in-app price where $0.69 buys 65 coins. That works out to roughly **94 coins per dollar** (94.34 to be exact), the baseline rate that holds across most US transactions in 2026.

_Prices last verified: July 2026. TikTok does not publish an official coin-to-dollar rate, so every figure here reflects the commonly observed purchase price rather than a rate TikTok guarantees._

This number matters because coins are the entry point to TikTok's entire virtual economy. Every gift sent during a livestream — every Rose, Hand Heart, or TikTok Universe thrown at a creator — starts as a coin bought with real money. If you are a viewer wondering what your spending actually translates to, or a creator trying to understand how much 1 TikTok coin is worth in take-home earnings, this guide traces every step.

At scale the math adds up. A viewer who spends $69.99 on the largest single package receives 6,607 coins. A creator who receives those coins as gifts pockets roughly half of their dollar value after TikTok's cut. Understanding these conversion rates is essential whether you are spending money or earning it. ## How TikTok Coins Work

TikTok coins are the platform's virtual currency. They exist only inside the TikTok ecosystem and serve one purpose: letting viewers buy and send virtual gifts to creators during livestreams and on video content. Coins cannot be transferred between users, cannot be refunded or converted back to cash by the buyer, and cannot be spent outside TikTok. They are governed by [TikTok's Virtual Items Policy](https://www.tiktok.com/legal/virtual-items), which states that purchases are generally non-refundable.

The system runs as a three-step pipeline: buy coins, send gifts, convert to diamonds. Each step involves a conversion, and each conversion is where TikTok takes its share.

### Buying Coins

You buy coins with real money. Inside the app, payments run through the Apple App Store on iOS or the Google Play Store on Android, so each store's fee (around 30%) is baked into the price — that is why the in-app rate sits at about $0.0106 per coin. You can also buy coins on the TikTok website, where those store fees do not apply and the rate is slightly cheaper at roughly $0.0105 per coin. If you buy coins often, purchasing on desktop or the web is the single easiest way to stretch each dollar. The official [LIVE, Gifts and wallet hub](https://support.tiktok.com/en/live-gifts-wallet) walks through the purchase flow.

The minimum purchase is 65 coins for $0.69, and the largest single package is 6,607 coins for $69.99. Once purchased, coins sit in your wallet with no expiration date and can be spent whenever you choose. There is no reliable way to earn free coins through normal activity; occasional promotions distribute small bonuses, but they are not predictable.

### Sending Gifts

Once you hold coins, you spend them by sending virtual gifts to creators. Gifts range from a 1-coin Rose (about $0.01) to the 34,999-coin TikTok Universe (about $370.99). Each gift has a fixed coin price, and when you send one during a livestream it appears as an animated effect on screen. Larger gifts trigger more prominent animations and draw more attention to the sender in the chat — the social spectacle is much of what drives spending on TikTok LIVE.

To send or receive gifts you must be 18 or older (19 in South Korea) with an account in good standing, per [TikTok's LIVE Gifts rules](https://support.tiktok.com/en/live-gifts-wallet/tiktok-live/live-gifts-on-tiktok). Creators see a running tally of gifts during a stream, and top gifters often get shoutouts.

### Converting to Diamonds

When a creator receives a gift, TikTok does not credit the coin value directly. The gift instead converts to **diamonds** — a separate unit only creators hold, and the intermediary between receiving gifts and withdrawing cash.

This conversion is where TikTok takes its primary cut. For every 100 coins a viewer spends (worth about $1.06), the creator earns roughly **$0.53** in diamond value — a roughly 50% revenue share, with TikTok keeping the rest. TikTok officially frames diamonds as being "awarded based on popularity" and publishes no fixed conversion rate, so treat 50% as the widely observed benchmark rather than a guaranteed figure. Diamonds accrue automatically; no action is needed from the creator.

## From Coins to Creator Earnings

Understanding how much 1 TikTok coin is worth requires tracing the full path from viewer purchase to creator payout. The headline number — $0.0106 per coin — only tells the buyer's side. Creators do not receive $0.0106 for every coin spent on them; the actual creator payout is roughly half that.

### The Gift-to-Diamond Pipeline

Here is the chain, traced from a single dollar:

1. A viewer spends $1.00 in the TikTok app.
2. After store fees are absorbed into pricing, that dollar buys about 94 coins.
3. The viewer sends those 94 coins as gifts to a creator.
4. TikTok converts the gifts to diamonds at roughly a 50% rate.
5. The creator earns about $0.50 in diamond value from that $1.00 in gifts.

So for every dollar a viewer spends, the creator nets about $0.50 before any withdrawal or currency fees. The other half is split between TikTok's revenue share and the store processing fees already baked into coin pricing. If a stream pulls in 100,000 coins of gifts (about $1,060 in coin value), the creator earns roughly $530.

### Diamond to Cash Conversion

Each diamond is worth about **$0.005** when cashed out — so 200 diamonds equal $1 and 2,000 diamonds equal $10. Creators withdraw diamonds through TikTok's payout system to a linked PayPal account or, in some regions, a bank transfer.

The minimum withdrawal is tiered: new creators can cash out from **$1 (200 diamonds)**, while established creators face a **$10 (2,000 diamonds)** minimum. Processing typically takes **5–15 business days**, and up to 21 in some cases. TikTok does not charge a percentage fee on the withdrawal itself — the ~50% cut is already taken during the coin-to-diamond step — though PayPal may apply its own transaction or currency-conversion fees. ## Why Coin Values Vary by Country

The coin-to-dollar rate above reflects US in-app pricing. TikTok publishes no official per-country coin price table, and the actual cost per coin abroad shifts with several factors, so treat any non-US figure as an estimate rather than a fixed rate:

- **App-store regional tiers.** Apple and Google set price tiers per country based on local purchasing power and exchange rates. These tiers update periodically and do not track real-time currency markets.
- **Local taxes and VAT.** Some countries add digital-goods taxes or VAT on top of the base price, raising the effective cost per coin without changing the coin count you receive.
- **Currency-conversion timing.** When you buy in a non-USD currency, the conversion happens at the store's exchange rate at the moment of purchase, so the effective USD value drifts with currency markets even though each package's coin count stays fixed.

For creators, this buyer-side variation does not change what they receive: a Rose sent from the US and a Rose sent from Japan convert to the same diamond value. Country differences only affect what the viewer pays, not the creator's payout.

## Tips for Maximizing Coin Value

**Buy on the web, not in the app.** The web rate (~$0.0105 per coin) skips the ~30% store fee baked into in-app pricing (~$0.0106 per coin). The gap is small per purchase but compounds for heavy spenders.

**Watch for promotions.** TikTok occasionally runs events with bonus coins or amplified gift effects. Timing larger purchases around these stretches your spending further.

**Gift for impact, not just size.** Because the ~50% cut applies to every gift equally, one Drama Queen and 5,000 individual Roses deliver the same creator payout — the difference is on-screen visibility, not value.

**Creators: withdraw regularly.** Once you clear your minimum ($1 new / $10 established), move diamonds to your own account rather than leaving them on the platform.

**Creators: track earnings per stream hour.** Knowing your average diamonds per hour and your top-gifting time slots lets you schedule around demand. Our guide to [TikTok LIVE earnings](/calculators/earnings-revenue/live-earnings/) breaks down the benchmarks by niche.

### Common mistakes to avoid

- **Assuming coins equal take-home cash.** A 100-coin gift costs the viewer $1.06 but nets the creator about $0.53. Halve the coin value to estimate creator earnings.
- **Quoting the stale 16,500-coin maximum.** The current top single package is 6,607 coins for $69.99.
- **Treating $0.0106 as official.** TikTok publishes no fixed rate; it is the observed in-app price and can move with store policy or currency.
