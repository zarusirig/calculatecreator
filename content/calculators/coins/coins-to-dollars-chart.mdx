## Coin Purchase Packages

TikTok coins convert to US dollars at about **$0.0106 per coin** in the app — so 100 coins are worth about $1.06, 1,000 coins about $10.60, and 10,000 coins about $106. TikTok sells coins in fixed packages priced in US dollars, and the rate barely changes with package size. Here are the standard US packages as of July 2026:

| 1,321 coins | $13.99 | $0.0106 |
| 3,303 coins | $34.99 | $0.0106 |
| 6,607 coins | $69.99 | $0.0106 |

_Prices last verified: July 2026. TikTok publishes no official coin-to-dollar rate; these figures reflect the commonly observed in-app price._

The per-coin cost is remarkably consistent — about $0.0106 no matter which bundle you buy. Larger packages shave off fractions of a cent at most, a far cry from the steep bulk discounts on other virtual-currency platforms. The minimum purchase is 65 coins ($0.69) and the largest single package is **6,607 coins ($69.99)** — if you have seen "16,500 coins" quoted as the maximum, that number is stale.

One important note on where you buy: in-app purchases on iOS and Android carry the store's ~30% fee, baked into that $0.0106 rate. Buying coins on the TikTok website skips that fee, landing at roughly **$0.0105 per coin**. For frequent buyers, purchasing on desktop or the web is the simplest way to get more coins per dollar. ## What Creators Actually Receive

This is the number that matters most for creators: TikTok keeps roughly **50%** of a gift's dollar value. The other ~50% reaches the creator as **diamonds**, which can then be withdrawn as cash. TikTok officially frames diamonds as "awarded based on popularity" and does not publish a fixed rate, so treat 50% as the widely observed benchmark rather than a published guarantee.

Here is the split in practice:

- A viewer spends $0.69 on 65 coins and sends 65 Roses (1 coin each). The creator receives about **$0.33** in diamonds (65 × $0.005). The viewer paid $0.69, TikTok kept roughly $0.34, and the creator earned about $0.33 — almost exactly half.
- A **Drama Queen** costs the viewer $53.00; the creator keeps about **$26.50**.
- A **TikTok Universe** costs the viewer $370.99; the creator keeps about **$185.50**.

The exact share is not always a clean 50% — coin-to-diamond-to-cash conversion rounds at multiple steps, and creators report effective take-home rates in the high-40s to low-50s percent depending on gift type. But 50% is the reliable benchmark for estimating gift earnings.

### How the Coin-to-Cash Pipeline Works

The full journey from a viewer's wallet to a creator's bank account runs through four stages, and money changes hands at each one:

1. **Buy coins.** A viewer pays real money for coins at about $0.0106 each in-app (or ~$0.0105 on the web). The app store already took its ~30% cut inside that price.
2. **Send a gift.** The viewer spends coins on a gift during a livestream or on a video. The coins leave their wallet at the gift's fixed coin price.
3. **Convert to diamonds.** TikTok credits the creator with diamonds worth roughly half the gift's dollar value — the ~50% revenue share.
4. **Withdraw.** The creator cashes diamonds out to PayPal or a bank once they clear the minimum, at about $0.005 per diamond.

Put together for a full stream, the math is straightforward. Say a creator receives **50,000 coins** in gifts over a night — a mix of Roses, Hand Hearts, and a couple of Gold Mines. At $0.0106 per coin, viewers spent about **$530** in total. At the ~50% share, the creator earns roughly **$265** in withdrawable diamonds (about 53,000 diamonds). The remaining ~$265 covers TikTok's revenue share and the store fees already priced into the coins. Halving the coin value is the fastest way to estimate any stream's take-home total.

### Withdrawal Minimums and Timing

Creators withdraw diamond earnings once they clear a tiered minimum: **$1 (200 diamonds) for new creators** and **$10 (2,000 diamonds) for established creators**. Payouts go to PayPal or, in some regions, a bank transfer, and processing typically takes **5–15 business days** — up to 21 in some cases. TikTok charges no additional percentage fee on withdrawals, though PayPal may apply its own transaction fees. Collecting diamonds also requires an account in good standing; per [TikTok's LIVE Gifts rules](https://support.tiktok.com/en/live-gifts-wallet/tiktok-live/live-gifts-on-tiktok), senders and recipients must be 18+ (19 in South Korea), and government or politician accounts cannot collect diamonds.

## Getting the Best Value

Whether you are a viewer supporting creators or a creator estimating revenue, these principles help you get the most from the coin economy.

### For Viewers

**Buy on the web when possible.** Purchasing through the TikTok website rather than the iOS app skips Apple's ~30% fee, so you get more coins per dollar (~$0.0105 versus ~$0.0106). See [how to buy TikTok coins](/calculators/coins/how-to-buy-tiktok-coins/) for the steps.

**Watch for bonus-coin promotions.** TikTok periodically offers bonus coins on larger packages, advertised with a banner on the purchase screen. Stocking up during a promotion gives you more gifting power per dollar. Coins are non-refundable under [TikTok's Virtual Items Policy](https://www.tiktok.com/legal/virtual-items), so only buy what you plan to send.

**Bigger gifts are not more efficient for the creator.** Because the ~50% cut applies equally across gift sizes, one Drama Queen and 5,000 individual Roses net the creator the same amount. The difference is visibility — larger gifts trigger bigger animations and are more likely to earn a shoutout.

### For Creators

**Track your diamond-to-dollar rate.** 50% is the benchmark, but comparing your actual withdrawal totals against total gift value tells you whether rounding or gift mix is nudging your real take-home rate.

**Encourage gifting without being pushy.** Creators who acknowledge gifts by name and react genuinely earn far more than those who passively stream. Make viewers feel their gift created a moment, not just a transaction.

**Compare income streams accurately.** Knowing a $53 gift nets you about $26.50 helps you weigh LIVE gifting against brand deals or Creator Rewards. To estimate a full stream, use our [live earnings calculator](/calculators/earnings-revenue/live-earnings/).

### Common mistakes to avoid

- **Counting coins as cash.** Coins are the viewer's cost, not the creator's income. Halve the dollar value to estimate creator payout.
- **Using the old 16,500-coin maximum.** The current top single package is 6,607 coins for $69.99.
- **Expecting a flat $5 withdrawal floor.** The minimum is $1 for new creators and $10 for established ones — not a flat $5.

## Are TikTok Coins Worth Buying?

Whether coins are "worth it" depends entirely on which side of the transaction you are on, and the honest answer is different for viewers and creators.

**For viewers, coins are a tip, not an investment.** Buying coins is a way to support a creator and stand out in a livestream — nothing more. Coins carry no cash value back to you: they cannot be sold, transferred to another user, or refunded once spent, and even unspent coins are generally non-refundable under [TikTok's Virtual Items Policy](https://www.tiktok.com/legal/virtual-items). So the "value" of a coin is entirely social — the animation it triggers and the acknowledgment it earns from the creator. If you would enjoy sending a $5 tip at a live show, 471 coins (about $5) delivers roughly that experience. If you are hoping to get money back, coins are the wrong tool.

**For creators, the coin economy is real income, but it is not the whole picture.** Because you keep only about half of a gift's face value, a stream that shows 50,000 coins of gifts is really a ~$265 night, not a $530 one. That still adds up: a creator who nets $265 across a handful of streams a week is earning a meaningful side income. But comparing it fairly to other channels matters — a single mid-tier brand deal or a strong month of Creator Rewards can rival weeks of gifting. Treat LIVE gifts as one revenue stream among several rather than the sole plan.

**Avoid third-party "cheap coin" sellers.** Sites promising coins far below the ~$0.0106 in-app rate almost always violate TikTok's terms and risk your account. The only safe places to buy are the TikTok app and the official TikTok website — and the website is already the cheapest legitimate option.

## FAQ

### How much is 1,000 TikTok coins in dollars?

About $10.60 for the viewer buying and sending them (the value of the 1,000-coin Gold Mine gift). The creator who receives 1,000 coins in gifts keeps roughly $5.30 after TikTok's ~50% cut.

### How much is 10,000 TikTok coins worth?

Around $106 at the in-app rate of ~$0.0106 per coin. To the creator, 10,000 coins in gifts are worth about $53 after the ~50% share.

### How much does TikTok take from gifts?

Roughly 50% of each gift's dollar value. The creator receives the other half as diamonds, worth about $0.005 each. TikTok does not publish a fixed rate, so 50% is a benchmark, not a guarantee.

### What is the most expensive TikTok gift?

The TikTok Universe at 34,999 coins (about $370.99), followed by the Lion at 29,999 coins (about $317.99). To the creator they are worth about $185.50 and $159.00 respectively.

### How do I convert TikTok diamonds to real money?

Withdraw through TikTok to PayPal or bank transfer. Each diamond is worth about $0.005 (2,000 diamonds = $10). New creators can cash out from $1 (200 diamonds), established creators from $10, with processing in 5–15 business days.

### Are TikTok coins cheaper on the website than in the app?

Yes, slightly. The web rate is about $0.0105 per coin versus $0.0106 in-app, because the website avoids the ~30% Apple or Google store fee.
