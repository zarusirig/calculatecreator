To qualify for TikTok monetization in 2026 you generally need to be 18 or older, live in an eligible country, and hit the follower and view thresholds for the specific program you want: the Creator Rewards Program requires 10,000 followers and 100,000 video views in the last 30 days, while LIVE Gifts and the TikTok Shop affiliate program each require only 1,000 followers. There is no single "monetization" switch. TikTok runs several separate programs, and you unlock each one as your account meets its criteria.

This guide lays out the current requirements for every major program, explains how each one pays, notes where they are available, and walks through how to turn monetization on inside the app. It also clears up the most common point of confusion: the current Creator Rewards Program is not the old Creator Fund, which TikTok has retired.

## What Are the TikTok Monetization Requirements?

TikTok monetization requirements are the eligibility rules that decide which creators can earn money on the platform, and they differ by program rather than applying to your whole account at once. Every program shares a few baseline conditions:

- Age: You must be at least 18 to earn from most monetization features (you can go LIVE at 16, but you need to be 18 to send or receive virtual gifts).
- Location: Your account must be registered in a country where the program is available.
- Account standing: You need an account in good standing that follows the Community Guidelines and Terms of Service.
- Original content: Rewards-based programs require original, non-duplicated public videos.

On top of those baselines, each program adds its own follower count and, in the case of the Creator Rewards Program, a recent-views threshold. The table below is the fastest way to see where you stand.

## TikTok Monetization Requirements by Program

The Creator Rewards Program has the highest bar; LIVE Gifts and TikTok Shop are reachable at 1,000 followers. Use this checklist to confirm which programs your account already qualifies for.

| Program | Followers | Other core requirements | What it pays for |
|---|---|---|---|
| Creator Rewards Program | 10,000+ | 100,000+ views in the last 30 days, age 18+, eligible country, account in good standing, original public videos over 1 minute | Reward payments based on qualified video views |
| LIVE Gifts | 1,000+ | Age 18+ to send and receive gifts (16+ to go LIVE), eligible country | Virtual gifts converted to diamonds, then cash |

A follower count alone is never enough on its own. For the Creator Rewards Program in particular, you must also clear the 100,000-view requirement over a rolling 30-day window, and individual videos only start earning after they pass roughly 1,000 qualified views on the For You feed. ## Creator Rewards Program vs. the Retired Creator Fund

The Creator Rewards Program is the current, active program; the original Creator Fund has been discontinued in supported regions and no longer accepts creators. This is the single biggest source of confusion in 2026, because a lot of older advice still references "the Creator Fund."

The two work very differently. The retired Creator Fund paid from a fixed pool and was widely criticized for very low, unpredictable per-view rates. The Creator Rewards Program replaced it with a model built around longer videos (over one minute) and "qualified" For You feed views, and TikTok positions it as paying more for content that drives real watch time and search value. If you are researching payouts, read the dedicated [Creator Rewards Program guide](/guides/creator-rewards-program/) for how qualified views work, and the [Creator Fund background guide](/guides/tiktok-creator-fund/) for what changed and why.

## How Each TikTok Monetization Program Works

TikTok monetization works by layering separate income streams, each triggered when your account meets that program's threshold, rather than through one blanket approval.

### Creator Rewards Program

Once you hit 10,000 followers and 100,000 views in 30 days, you can apply through Creator Tools. Payments are tied to qualified views on original videos longer than one minute, so short clips and reposts do not count. Earnings vary widely by niche, audience country, and watch time.

### LIVE Gifts

At 1,000 followers you can go LIVE and, once age-verified as 18+, receive virtual gifts. Viewers buy coins, send them as gifts, and TikTok converts those gifts into diamonds that you cash out at roughly half of the coins' face value after the platform's cut. Gift income is real-time and community-driven rather than view-based.

### TikTok Shop affiliate

Also reachable at 1,000 followers, the affiliate program lets you tag products in your content and earn a commission on sales you drive. You need to pass identity verification and link a payout method. This is often the fastest stream to add because it does not depend on a large recent-view count.

## Which Countries Are Eligible for TikTok Monetization?

TikTok monetization is not available worldwide, and availability differs by program. The Creator Rewards Program has rolled out to a limited list of markets that has included the United States, United Kingdom, France, Germany, Japan, South Korea, and Brazil, while LIVE Gifts and TikTok Shop are available in their own, sometimes broader, sets of countries.

Because TikTok expands and adjusts this list over time, treat any country list as a planning reference and confirm your own eligibility inside the app. If a program is available in your region and you meet the thresholds, the option appears in Creator Tools; if it does not appear, the program is either unavailable where you are or you have not yet met a requirement.

## How to Turn On Monetization on TikTok

You turn on monetization by opening the specific program in Creator Tools and applying once you are eligible, not through a single account-wide setting. The general path is the same for each program:

1. Open the TikTok app and go to your Profile.
2. Tap the menu (three lines) in the top corner and open Creator Tools or TikTok Studio.
3. Select the program you want, such as Creator Rewards Program, LIVE, or TikTok Shop.
4. If you are eligible, review the terms and tap to apply or join.
5. Complete any required verification, including a government-issued ID and tax details.
6. Wait for review; approval is usually quick when you clearly meet the requirements.

If a program does not show an apply button, you have not met a threshold yet or it is not offered in your country.

## TikTok Monetization Requirements FAQ

### What are the TikTok monetization requirements?

You must be at least 18, live in an eligible country, and keep an account in good standing, then meet each program's threshold: 10,000 followers plus 100,000 views in 30 days for the Creator Rewards Program, or 1,000 followers for LIVE Gifts and the TikTok Shop affiliate program.

### How does TikTok monetization work?

TikTok monetization works through several separate programs rather than one payout. You unlock each one as your account qualifies, and they pay in different ways: the Creator Rewards Program pays for qualified video views, LIVE Gifts convert viewer gifts into cash, and TikTok Shop pays commission on sales you drive.

### How do you turn on monetization on TikTok?

Open your Profile, tap the menu, and go to Creator Tools or TikTok Studio, then select the program you qualify for and follow the prompts to apply, verify your identity, and add payout details. If the option is missing, you have not met a requirement or the program is unavailable in your country.

### Is the Creator Fund still available?

No. The original Creator Fund has been retired in supported regions and replaced by the Creator Rewards Program, which requires 10,000 followers and 100,000 views in the last 30 days and pays based on qualified views of videos longer than one minute.
