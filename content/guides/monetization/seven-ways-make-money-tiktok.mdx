## 1. Brand Deals and Sponsored Content

Brand deals are the single most lucrative way to make money on TikTok. Sponsors pay creators directly to feature products or services in their content, and rates scale dramatically with audience size and engagement quality.

### What Brands Pay in 2026

A useful per-follower baseline is roughly **$0.05 to $0.25 per follower** for a single in-feed video, depending on your niche — about $50 to $250 per 1,000 followers. Creators with high [engagement rates](/calculators/engagement-rate/) negotiate well above that benchmark. The table below shows typical per-post rates by follower tier:

| Follower Tier | Followers | Rate per In-Feed Post |
|---|---|---|
| Nano | 1K - 10K | $50 - $200 |
| Micro | 10K - 50K | $200 - $1,000 |
| Mid | 50K - 200K | $1,000 - $5,000 |
| Macro | 200K - 1M | $5,000 - $15,000 |
| Mega | 1M+ | $15,000 - $50,000 |

Deliverable type changes the price too. A Spark Ad (where the brand paid-boosts your organic post) typically carries a **1.3x** multiplier, a dedicated product showcase around **1.2x**, and a quick LIVE mention roughly **0.7x** of your standard in-feed rate. Beauty and fashion command the highest niche premiums.

### How to Land Your First Brand Deal

Start by building a media kit with your follower count, average views, engagement rate, and audience demographics. Join platforms like TikTok Creator Marketplace to get discovered, or pitch brands directly by emailing their marketing team a short portfolio of your best-performing content. Estimate a fair rate with our [brand deal rate calculator](/calculators/brand-deal-rate/) before any negotiation, and read our full walkthrough on [how to get brand deals](/guides/how-to-get-brand-deals/).

### Maximizing Sponsorship Revenue

Creators who negotiate usage rights, exclusivity clauses, and multi-post packages earn 30-50% more per campaign than those who accept flat-rate, one-off deals. Long-term ambassador partnerships are especially valuable because they provide predictable monthly income while giving you creative freedom to integrate the brand authentically.

## 2. TikTok Shop and E-Commerce

TikTok Shop has become one of the highest-earning monetization methods on the platform. Creators sell products directly through in-feed videos, LIVE streams, and a dedicated shop tab, keeping a significant share of every sale.

### Selling Your Own Products

If you have a physical or digital product, TikTok Shop lets you tag items directly in your videos. TikTok charges a platform fee of approximately **2.9% per transaction** on your own-product sales (category referral fees may apply on top). Creators in niches like beauty, fashion, fitness, and home goods routinely generate $1,000 to $15,000 per month at the 100K-follower level by featuring products in organic content. Project your numbers with our [TikTok Shop calculator](/calculators/commerce-ads/shop-profit/), and see the full [TikTok Shop guide](/guides/tiktok-shop/) for setup steps.

### Selling as an Affiliate Through TikTok Shop

Even without your own products, you can earn commissions by promoting items from other TikTok Shop sellers. The standard affiliate commission is **5%**, ranging from **2% to 20%** by category. Shop affiliate access requires **5,000 followers**, and payments are made **biweekly**. Top affiliate sellers earn over $10,000 per month by consistently posting product reviews and haul videos.

## 3. Affiliate Marketing Beyond TikTok Shop

Traditional affiliate marketing remains one of the most accessible ways to make money on TikTok because it requires no minimum follower count for external programs. You promote products from outside brands and earn a commission every time someone purchases through your unique link.

Commission rates range from 5% to 50% depending on the product type. Digital products and software subscriptions offer the highest commissions, often 20-50%, while physical goods from programs like Amazon Associates pay 1-10%. (By comparison, TikTok Shop affiliate commissions sit in the 2-20% range.) Creators who focus on a specific niche and build trust convert at significantly higher rates than those who promote random products. To see how these commissions translate into earnings at your audience size, check the [TikTok earnings calculator](/calculators/tiktok-money/).

The key advantage of affiliate marketing is that old videos keep generating commissions for months or even years after posting. A single viral product review can produce passive income long after it leaves the For You Page, making it one of the best [passive income strategies for TikTok creators](/guides/monetization/passive-income-tiktok-creators/).

## 4. LIVE Gifts and Diamonds

TikTok keeps approximately **50%** of the gift value, so creators net roughly $0.50 for every $1.00 in gifts sent. You need **1,000 followers to go LIVE**, must be **16+** to stream, and **18+** to receive gifts.

## 5. Creator Rewards Program

The TikTok Creator Rewards Program (formerly the Creativity Program Beta, which replaced the original Creator Fund) pays creators based on qualified video views. To earn, your videos must be at least one minute long and meet TikTok's quality thresholds.

### Current Pay Rates

The Creator Rewards Program pays between **$0.50 and $2.00 per 1,000 qualified views** — a 10-40x improvement over the original Creator Fund, which paid $0.02 to $0.05. Not every view is "qualified," though. A qualified view is a unique For You feed view lasting at least five seconds; TikTok excludes repeat views, paid or promoted views, and views on videos that fail its originality standards. A video must reach **1,000 qualified For You feed views** before it starts earning. TikTok also adds a smaller "Additional Reward" on top of the base rate for content it judges well-crafted or specialized.

A creator averaging 500,000 qualified views per month can expect roughly $250 to $750. Those hitting 5 million qualified views typically earn $2,500 to $7,500. Estimate your own with the [Creator Fund calculator](/calculators/tiktok-creator-fund/) and the [pay per 1,000 views](/calculators/tiktok-money/tiktok-pay-per-1000-views/) breakdown.

### Eligibility Requirements

You need at least **10,000 followers**, **100,000 video views in the last 30 days**, an account in good standing, and you must be **18 or older** (19+ in South Korea) in an eligible market. Videos must be original, at least 60 seconds long, and posted from a personal account. Duets, Stitches, and clips with more than a minute of copyrighted music are not eligible. ## 6. TikTok Series (Paid Content)

[TikTok Series](https://support.tiktok.com/en/business-and-creator/tiktok-series) lets creators publish collections of premium videos behind a paywall. Viewers pay a one-time fee of **$0.99 to $189.99** to unlock a Series, which can hold 3 to 80 videos with episodes up to 20 minutes. Eligibility requires 10,000 followers, a personal account, and good standing.

TikTok's platform fee on Series sales is roughly **30% on iOS** and **10-15% on Android and web**, so pricing the same Series higher on web can meaningfully improve your take. This method works best for educational, tutorial, or in-depth content that audiences will pay for — fitness trainers, business coaches, cooking instructors, and skill-based creators see the highest conversion. A creator with 50,000 engaged followers who prices a Series at $4.99 and converts just 1% of their audience earns roughly $2,500 from a single launch (before the platform fee).

One important caveat: videos linked to a Series are **excluded from the Creator Rewards Program**, so you cannot earn RPM payouts and Series revenue on the same video. Learn more in our [TikTok Series monetization guide](/guides/monetization/tiktok-series-monetization/).

## 7. Subscriptions

TikTok Subscriptions let fans pay a recurring monthly fee (typically $0.99 to $4.99) for exclusive content, badges, and perks. This model provides the most predictable recurring revenue of any TikTok monetization method.

Subscription revenue depends heavily on conversion rate. Most creators convert 0.5% to 2% of their follower base into paying subscribers. A creator with 100,000 followers converting 1% at $4.99 per month earns roughly $4,990 monthly before TikTok's platform cut. The subscription model rewards creators who invest in community building and deliver consistent exclusive value.

## Income Stacking: Why Top Creators Use Multiple Methods

The highest-earning TikTok creators do not rely on a single monetization method. They stack multiple income streams to maximize revenue and reduce risk. A typical income mix for a full-time creator with 200,000 followers might look like this:

- **Brand deals**: 40-50% of total income ($3,000 - $7,000/month)
- **TikTok Shop or affiliate**: 20-30% ($1,500 - $4,000/month)
- **Creator Rewards**: 5-10% ($400 - $1,000/month)
- **LIVE gifts**: 10-15% ($700 - $2,000/month)
- **Series or subscriptions**: 5-10% ($400 - $1,000/month)

This diversified approach means a slow month for brand deals does not wipe out your income entirely. ## Common Mistakes When Monetizing TikTok

- **Chasing views instead of value.** Millions of views in a low-CPM market or a low-RPM niche can pay less than a single mid-tier brand deal. Platform payouts are the floor, not the ceiling.
- **Underpricing brand deals.** Nano and micro creators frequently accept flat product-only "gifting" instead of cash. Anchor to the $0.05-$0.25 per-follower baseline and charge for usage rights.
- **Ignoring the qualified-views rule.** Creator Rewards only pays on qualified For You feed views over five seconds — your dashboard view count overstates payable views by 20-40%.
- **Trying to double-dip on Series.** Series videos are excluded from Creator Rewards, so putting your best evergreen content behind a paywall can cost you RPM income.
- **Waiting for one stream to scale.** The creators who earn the most start stacking a second and third income source early rather than perfecting a single one.

## Maximizing Earnings Across All Methods

A few principles apply universally to increasing TikTok revenue, regardless of method.

First, engagement rate matters more than follower count for every income stream. Brands pay premiums for high engagement, Creator Rewards rewards watch time, and LIVE gifts depend on active viewers. Use our [engagement rate calculator](/calculators/engagement-rate/) to benchmark where you stand — the platform average is about 4.07%, and brands look for 4%+ (6%+ for premium deals).

Second, consistency compounds. Creators who post 4-7 times per week and go live 2-3 times per week earn substantially more than those who post sporadically, even at identical follower counts. Track your [follower growth trajectory](/calculators/follower-growth/) to stay on target.

Third, understand the [TikTok algorithm](/guides/growth/tiktok-algorithm-explained/). It determines how many people see your content, which directly impacts every monetization method. Optimize for watch time, shares, and comments to earn exponentially more views — and revenue.

Finally, set a concrete earnings target. Whether your goal is your [first $1,000 on TikTok](/guides/how-to-make-first-1000-dollars/) or a six-figure annual income, a clear financial roadmap keeps you focused on the methods that move the needle fastest.

## FAQ

### How much does TikTok pay per 1,000 views in 2026?

Through the Creator Rewards Program, TikTok pays $0.50 to $2.00 per 1,000 qualified views for US audiences — a 10-40x jump over the legacy Creator Fund's $0.02-$0.05. High-CPM niches like finance can exceed $2.00, while comedy and entertainment sit lower. Only qualified For You feed views over five seconds count.

### How many followers do you need to start making money on TikTok?

It depends on the method. Affiliate marketing and UGC brand work have no follower minimum. TikTok Shop affiliate access opens at 5,000 followers. Going LIVE requires 1,000 followers, and the Creator Rewards Program and Series both require 10,000 followers plus 100,000 views in the last 30 days.

### What is the difference between the Creator Fund and the Creator Rewards Program?

The Creator Fund is the legacy program, paying $0.02-$0.05 per 1,000 views; it has been superseded by the Creator Rewards Program in most markets but remains active in some regions. The Creator Rewards Program pays $0.50-$2.00 per 1,000 qualified views and requires videos at least 60 seconds long.

### How much does TikTok take from LIVE gifts and Series sales?

TikTok keeps approximately 50% of LIVE gift value, so creators net about $0.50 per $1.00 sent. On Series, the platform fee is roughly 30% on iOS and 10-15% on Android and web. For LIVE, 2 coins equal 1 diamond and each diamond is worth $0.005 to the creator.

### Can you make money on TikTok without 10,000 followers?

Yes. Affiliate marketing, UGC content for brands, and (at 5,000 followers) TikTok Shop affiliate sales all work below 10,000 followers. You can also go LIVE and receive gifts at just 1,000 followers. The Creator Rewards Program and Series are the two methods that require 10,000.

### Which way of making money on TikTok pays the most?

Brand deals pay the most at every follower tier, from $50-$200 per post for nano-creators to $15,000-$50,000 per post for mega-creators. TikTok Shop ranks second, followed by affiliate marketing and LIVE gifts. Platform payouts through the Creator Rewards Program are the most predictable but the lowest per view.
