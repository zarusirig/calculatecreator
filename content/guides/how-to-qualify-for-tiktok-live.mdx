## LIVE Access Requirements: Quick Check
          To unlock TikTok LIVE streaming, you must meet ALL these requirements:

- **1,000+ followers** — the main gate for LIVE access.
- **Age 16 or older to host a LIVE**, and **18 or older to send or receive virtual gifts** (19 in South Korea).
- **Account in good standing** — no active community-guidelines strikes or bans.
- **A supported region** — LIVE is available in most countries, though gift monetization varies (see the country list below).

Per TikTok's official [LIVE Gifts policy](https://support.tiktok.com/en/live-gifts-wallet/tiktok-live/live-gifts-on-tiktok), gift recipients must also be in an eligible location and in good standing; government, politician, and political-party accounts cannot collect Diamonds.

## How to Start Your First LIVE Stream
          Once you meet the requirements, here's how to go LIVE:




                1



#### Open TikTok and Tap the "+" Button
                At the bottom center of your screen, tap the create button (same button you use to record videos).





                2



#### Swipe to "LIVE" Mode
                At the bottom of the recording screen, swipe left past "Video", "Photo", "Text" until you see "LIVE". If you don't see it, you haven't met the requirements yet (check age, followers, violations).





                3



#### Set Your LIVE Title
                Write a compelling title (50 characters max). Good titles: "Q&A: Ask me anything!", "Trying viral food hacks", "Come hang out + chat". Avoid vague titles like "Live" or "Hi".





                4



#### Choose Your Cover Image
                This is the thumbnail people see when browsing LIVE streams. Use a clear, bright image of your face or an attention-grabbing visual. Test your lighting first!





                5



#### Tap "Go LIVE"
                When ready, hit the pink "Go LIVE" button. You're now streaming! Your followers will receive notifications (if enabled) and your stream appears in the LIVE tab + FYP.





            **First LIVE Tips:** Stream for at least 30 minutes (short streams hurt your algorithm ranking). Acknowledge viewers by name when they join. Set a goal (e.g., "Let's hit 50 viewers!") to encourage engagement. End with a follow CTA.







## Troubleshooting: Why Can't I Go LIVE?

If the LIVE button doesn't appear even though you think you qualify, check these common causes:

- **You're under 1,000 followers.** LIVE access unlocks only at the 1,000-follower threshold.
- **Your birthday on file makes you under 16.** You must be 16+ to host; correct your date of birth in Settings if it's wrong.
- **An active violation or strike.** A recent community-guidelines penalty temporarily blocks LIVE — wait for it to expire.
- **The app isn't updated.** Update TikTok to the latest version; older builds sometimes hide the LIVE option.
- **Regional restrictions.** LIVE or gifting may be limited where you are; check the country list above.

If everything checks out and LIVE still won't load, log out and back in, or contact TikTok support through the app.

## FAQ

### How many followers do you need to go LIVE on TikTok?

You need at least 1,000 followers to unlock TikTok LIVE. There is no official workaround — the threshold applies to every account. To also receive virtual gifts, you must be 18 or older and in an eligible region.

### How old do you have to be to go LIVE and receive gifts?

You must be at least 16 to host a LIVE stream and at least 18 to send or receive virtual gifts (19 in South Korea), per [TikTok's Virtual Items Policy](https://www.tiktok.com/legal/virtual-items). Make sure your account birthday is accurate, since TikTok uses it to gate these features.

### Why can't I see the LIVE button even though I have 1,000 followers?

The most common reasons are an inaccurate birthday (under 16), an active community-guidelines strike, an outdated app version, or regional restrictions. Update the app, verify your date of birth, and confirm your account is in good standing.

### How much can you earn from TikTok LIVE gifts?

Earnings scale with concurrent viewers: roughly $2–$10 per stream at 20–50 viewers, up to $500+ per stream at 500–1,000 viewers. Creators keep about 50% of gift value as Diamonds, which are worth around $0.005 each.
