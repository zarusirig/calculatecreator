Pricing your own work is the hardest part of turning a TikTok following into income. Charge too little and you drag down rates for every creator; quote a number you can't defend and brands walk away. This guide pairs the [Brand Deal Rate Calculator](/calculators/brand-deal-rate/) with a clear framework so you can set rates, package deliverables, and negotiate from data instead of guesswork.

_Last updated: July 2026 · Rates reflect TT Calculator's Q1 2026 dataset._

## Common Deal Types

Most TikTok brand partnerships fall into a handful of formats, and each carries a different price:

- **Single sponsored post** — one in-feed video featuring the brand, the building block for every other package.
- **Multi-video bundle** — three to six videos sold together at a modest per-video discount in exchange for guaranteed volume.
- **Spark Ads / whitelisting** — you grant the brand rights to run your video as a paid ad from your handle, priced above a standard post because the content keeps working as media.
- **Product showcase or unboxing** — a demo-heavy video that takes more production effort.
- **Brand ambassadorship** — an ongoing, multi-month relationship with exclusivity, priced at a large multiple of a single post.

## What Affects Your Rate

Four factors move your price more than anything else:

- **Follower tier.** Rates scale with reach. TikTok's tiers run Nano (1K–10K), Micro (10K–50K), Mid (50K–200K), Macro (200K–1M), and Mega (1M+).
- **Engagement rate.** Brands pay for attention, not vanity metrics. The platform average is about 4.07%, and most brands look for 4%+ (premium brands 6%+). Above-average engagement justifies a premium over the baseline rate.
- **Niche.** Finance, tech, and B2B audiences command far higher rates than broad entertainment because advertiser budgets are larger.
- **Deliverables and rights.** Extra videos, Spark Ads whitelisting, exclusivity, and multi-platform usage each add to the fee.

Start from a per-follower baseline of **$0.05–$0.25**, then adjust up for engagement, niche, and rights. For a full breakdown by size, see [brand deal rates by follower count](/data/brand-deal-rates-by-followers/).

## How to Negotiate with Brands

### The negotiation framework

1. **Let the brand make the first offer if possible.** Ask "What's your budget for this campaign?" — brands often name a number higher than you would have quoted.
2. **If you must quote first, start 20–30% above target.** "My rate for this type of campaign is $5,000" leaves room to negotiate down.
3. **Justify with data.** "Based on my 8% engagement rate and 50K followers, the Brand Deal Rate Calculator puts my rate around $4,000 for my tier."
4. **Use silence.** After stating your rate, stop talking. The first person to speak concedes leverage.
5. **Trade deliverables, not just price.** "At $3,000 I can do 2 videos instead of 3, or drop the Spark Ads rights."
6. **Know your walk-away number.** If your minimum is $2,500, don't accept $2,000 just because a brand pushes.

### When to walk away

- The offer is under 50% of your calculator estimate (unless you love the brand).
- They want unlimited usage rights without a 3–4x premium.
- Exclusivity is on the table without a rate increase.
- It's product-only and you're past 25K followers.
- Red flags: no contract, vague payment terms, or "exposure" talk.

## Contract Essentials

Never start work without a signed contract, and build in FTC disclosure from the start — [Darkroom's 2026 TikTok policy guide](https://www.darkroomagency.com/observatory/what-brands-need-to-know-about-tiktok-new-rules-2026) walks through the branded-content and AI-disclosure rules brands now expect. Every agreement should spell out:

- **Payment amount and schedule** — exact figure, currency, and terms (50% upfront / 50% on delivery, or Net 30/60).
- **Deliverables** — exactly what you're creating (for example, one 60-second TikTok).
- **Timeline** — draft, final, and posting dates.
- **Usage rights** — duration (30/60/90 days vs perpetual), platforms, and purposes.
- **Revisions** — how many rounds and what counts as a revision.
- **FTC compliance** — who handles disclosure and the approved language.
- **Kill fee** — what you're paid if the brand cancels mid-project (50–100%).
- **Content ownership** — you own the content; the brand holds licensed rights only.

Treat these as red flags: "payment upon satisfactory performance," unlimited revisions, perpetual worldwide exclusive rights without a large premium, Net 90+ terms, and penalty clauses for underperformance you cannot control.

## Scaling Your Rates Over Time

Raise prices as your leverage grows:

- **Follower milestones.** Increase 20–30% every 25K–50K followers; hitting 100K often doubles your 50K rate.
- **Performance triggers.** A 2%+ jump in engagement, case studies proving ROI, or demand that exceeds your capacity all justify a raise.
- **Grandfather existing clients.** Honor old rates for a 3–6 month transition, then move everyone to current pricing.
- **Premium positioning.** Specializing in one niche lets you charge 2–3x a generalist at the same follower count.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much should I charge for a TikTok sponsored post?

Industry standard is $0.05–$0.25 per follower as a base rate, adjusted for engagement and niche. Per in-feed post that works out to roughly Nano (1K–10K) $50–$200, Micro (10K–50K) $200–$1,000, Mid (50K–200K) $1,000–$5,000, Macro (200K–1M) $5,000–$15,000, and Mega (1M+) $15,000–$50,000. Premium niches and Spark Ads rights push the top of each range higher.

### How do I calculate my influencer rate?

Base Rate = Followers × $0.05–$0.25, then apply an engagement multiplier (the calculator uses 0.8x to 1.5x depending on how your rate compares to the ~4% platform average). Example: 50K followers × $0.05 = $2,500 base; strong 8% engagement applies the top 1.5x multiplier, lifting it toward $3,750–$4,000. Then factor in niche premium (finance/tech 2–3x) and deliverables (Spark Ads whitelisting about +30%).

### Should I charge per post or per campaign?

Both. Offer single-post pricing and package deals. Packages increase average deal size — a 3-post bundle at 2.5x (vs 3x) encourages larger commitments. Long-term partnerships (3–6 months) should run 8–12x a single post.

### What if a brand says my rate is too high?

Justify with data: "Based on my 8% engagement and the Brand Deal Rate Calculator, market rate is $X–$Y for my tier." If they won't budge, negotiate deliverables (fewer posts, or drop Spark Ads rights) rather than cutting your price. Walk away if the offer is under 50% of your estimate — underpricing hurts the whole creator economy.

### How much extra should I charge for usage rights?

Spark Ads whitelisting adds about +30% (roughly 1.3x your in-feed rate), and the 2026 Brand Access Portal lets you set a 7-, 14-, 30-, 60-, or 90-day authorization. Longer or perpetual ad usage commands far more — often +150–300% or a 3–4x multiple. Exclusivity clauses run +100–150%, and multi-platform rights add roughly +50% per extra platform. Always price usage rights separately from the creation fee.

### When should I raise my rates?

Raise every 25K–50K followers (20–30%), when engagement improves significantly (+2% is worth a 30–50% raise), when you're booking every inquiry within 48 hours, or when you have case studies proving ROI. Test new rates on new clients first and grandfather existing clients for 3–6 months.

### Can I charge more in premium niches like finance or tech?

Yes. Finance, tech, and B2B creators charge 2–3x entertainment creators at the same follower count thanks to higher-value audiences, larger brand budgets, and premium positioning. Always factor the niche premium into your calculation.

### Should I work for free to build my portfolio?

Only if you're under about 1,000 followers and need case studies. Once you hit 5K+, always charge cash (a $50–$100 minimum). Product-only deals are acceptable for small creators from brands you genuinely want to promote. Never work for "exposure."
