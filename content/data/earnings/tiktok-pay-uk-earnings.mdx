TikTok pays UK-based creators approximately £0.02 to £0.04 per 1,000 views through the Creator Rewards Program, making the United Kingdom the second-highest-paying English-speaking market after the [United States](/data/earnings/tiktok-pay-us-earnings). A UK creator generating 1 million monthly views can expect £20 to £40 from the Rewards Program directly, but total earnings rise to £400 to £4,000 or more when brand deals, live gifts, and TikTok Shop commissions are included. The UK has one of the strongest influencer marketing industries in Europe, and British creators benefit from high advertiser demand and a mature brand deal ecosystem.

## Key Findings

UK TikTok earnings data from more than 900 British creators reveals several critical benchmarks for anyone monetizing on the platform from England, Scotland, Wales, or Northern Ireland.

The Creator Rewards Program pays UK creators £0.02 to £0.04 per 1,000 views for original videos longer than one minute. This puts UK RPM roughly 20% to 30% below [US rates](/data/earnings/tiktok-pay-us-earnings) in currency-adjusted terms, but ahead of most other European markets. UK creators receive payouts in GBP, with the same payment thresholds and monthly processing schedule as US accounts.

Brand deals represent the largest income stream for UK creators who actively pursue sponsorships. Nano-influencers (10K to 50K followers) earn £200 to £800 per sponsored video, while mid-tier creators (250K to 1M followers) command £2,000 to £8,000 per post. The UK influencer marketing industry was valued at approximately £1.5 billion in 2025, and TikTok's share of that spend continues to grow.

Tax obligations through HMRC require careful planning. UK creators earning more than £1,000 per year from TikTok must register as self-employed and file a Self Assessment tax return. National Insurance contributions (Class 2 and Class 4) apply on top of income tax, and the personal allowance of £12,570 shields lower-earning creators from income tax on their first chunk of earnings.

TikTok Shop is well-established in the UK market. British creators earn 10% to 20% commissions on affiliate sales, and the UK was one of TikTok Shop's earliest Western launch markets. Shop-active creators with 100K followers typically generate £40 to £300 per month in affiliate commissions.

### RPM GBP

RPM in GBP is the central metric for understanding what TikTok pays UK creators per 1,000 video views, and it varies based on several measurable factors.

UK TikTok RPM ranges from £0.02 to £0.04 per 1,000 views for Creator Rewards-eligible content (videos longer than one minute, classified as original). Shorter videos under 60 seconds earn approximately £0.008 to £0.015 per 1K views, which is insufficient for meaningful direct income at most view counts.

The following factors drive RPM variation within the UK market.

| Engagement rate | Higher engagement signals quality content | Top 10% engagement: 1.2x – 1.4x RPM lift |

Finance, property, and technology creators in the UK command the highest RPM because UK advertisers in these verticals pay premium CPM rates. Lifestyle and comedy content earns lower per-view rates but tends to generate higher view volumes. See our [RPM rates by niche](/data/rpm-rates-by-niche) analysis for detailed breakdowns.

Seasonal variation follows the same Q4-peak pattern as the US. UK RPM peaks during October through December, driven by Black Friday adoption in the UK, Christmas shopping, and end-of-year advertising budget flushes. January is the lowest-earning month, with RPM dropping 35% to 45% from December levels. For full seasonal data, see [seasonal TikTok earnings trends](/data/earnings/seasonal-tiktok-earnings-trends).

UK RPM converted to USD typically falls in the $0.025 to $0.05 range depending on the GBP/USD exchange rate, placing it close to but slightly below US rates. This makes the UK the highest-paying European market for TikTok creators.

## TikTok Pay in the UK — Extended Data

Beyond RPM and brand deal rates, UK-specific tax rules, regional market dynamics, and cross-country comparisons affect the real earnings picture for British creators.

**HMRC tax obligations** apply to all UK TikTok earnings above the trading allowance of £1,000 per year. Creators earning more than this threshold must register as self-employed with HMRC and file a Self Assessment tax return annually (deadline: January 31 for the previous tax year).

| Annual TikTok Earnings | Income Tax Rate | National Insurance (Class 4) | Approximate Take-Home % |
|---|---|---|---|
| £1,000 – £12,570 | 0% (personal allowance) | 0% (below threshold) | 95% – 100% |
| £12,571 – £50,270 | 20% (basic rate) | 6% | 74% |
| £50,271 – £125,140 | 40% (higher rate) | 2% | 58% |
| £125,141+ | 45% (additional rate) | 2% | 53% |

Class 2 National Insurance contributions of £3.45 per week apply when profits exceed £12,570. Class 4 NICs are charged at 6% on profits between £12,570 and £50,270, and 2% above that. These are in addition to income tax.

The personal allowance of £12,570 is a significant advantage for part-time UK creators. A creator earning £10,000 per year from TikTok (with no other income) pays no income tax and no Class 4 NICs, keeping nearly all their earnings. This is substantially more favorable than the [US tax structure](/data/earnings/tiktok-pay-us-earnings), where self-employment tax of 15.3% applies from the first dollar of profit.

For detailed tax planning advice, see the [creator taxes guide](/guides/business/tiktok-creator-taxes-guide).

## Methodology

The UK earnings data in this article is compiled from multiple sources to ensure accuracy for British creators.

**Primary data** comes from self-reported earnings from 900+ UK-based TikTok creators who participated in our Q4 2025 and Q1 2026 income surveys. Participants provided analytics screenshots to verify follower counts and view data. Earnings were reported by revenue stream and converted where necessary using the average GBP/USD exchange rate for the reporting period.

**Secondary data** includes rate cards from six UK influencer marketing agencies, TikTok's official Creator Rewards Program documentation, HMRC self-employment tax guidance, and aggregate campaign data from two UK-focused influencer platforms. Brand deal ranges reflect both agency-reported and creator-reported rates.

**Limitations.** Self-reported data carries survivorship and recall biases. UK sample size (900+) is smaller than our US sample (1,800+), which increases margin of error. Brand deal earnings are difficult to verify due to NDA provisions. Tax estimates are general guidance and not tax advice -- UK creators should consult an accountant or HMRC directly. All pound figures are in GBP unless stated otherwise. We update this data quarterly.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does TikTok pay per 1,000 views in the UK?

TikTok pays UK creators £0.02 to £0.04 per 1,000 views through the Creator Rewards Program for original videos longer than one minute. Shorter videos earn approximately £0.008 to £0.015 per 1K views. These rates vary by niche, audience location, engagement rate, and season. Q4 (October to December) delivers the highest RPM, while January is the lowest-earning month. Use the [pay per 1000 views calculator](/calculators/tiktok-money/tiktok-pay-per-1000-views) to estimate your specific rate.

### Do UK TikTok creators pay tax on earnings?

Yes. UK creators earning more than £1,000 per year from TikTok must register as self-employed with HMRC. Income tax applies at 20% (basic rate), 40% (higher rate), or 45% (additional rate) after the £12,570 personal allowance. National Insurance contributions (Class 2 and Class 4) are also due on profits above the threshold. The personal allowance means creators earning less than £12,570 pay no income tax, which is more favorable than the US system. See the [creator taxes guide](/guides/business/tiktok-creator-taxes-guide) for details.

### How do UK TikTok earnings compare to the US?

UK Creator Rewards RPM (£0.02 to £0.04) is roughly 20% to 30% lower than US rates ($0.03 to $0.05) when converted to the same currency. UK brand deal rates are also typically 20% to 40% lower than US equivalents. However, the UK's £12,570 personal allowance means lower-earning UK creators keep a larger after-tax share than their US counterparts, who face self-employment tax from the first dollar. See the [US earnings data](/data/earnings/tiktok-pay-us-earnings) for a direct comparison.

### Is TikTok Shop available for UK creators?

Yes. The UK was one of TikTok Shop's first Western markets, and UK creators have full access to affiliate product linking, live shopping, and Shop commissions. Average commissions range from 10% to 20% per sale, and Shop-active creators with 100K followers typically earn £40 to £300 per month in affiliate income. TikTok Shop is a growing revenue stream that works best for creators in beauty, fashion, home goods, and technology niches.
