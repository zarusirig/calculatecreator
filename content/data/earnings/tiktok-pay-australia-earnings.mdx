TikTok pays Australian creators approximately A$0.02 to A$0.04 per 1,000 views through the Creator Rewards Program, placing Australia among the top-paying markets in the Asia-Pacific region. An Australian creator generating 1 million monthly views can expect A$20 to A$40 from the Rewards Program alone, but total earnings climb to A$450 to A$4,000 or more when brand deals, live gifts, and TikTok Shop commissions are included. Australia's active influencer marketing scene and high advertising CPM rates make it a strong market for TikTok monetization, ranking alongside the [United Kingdom](/data/earnings/tiktok-pay-uk-earnings/) and [Canada](/data/earnings/tiktok-pay-canada-earnings/) in the tier just below the [United States](/data/earnings/tiktok-pay-us-earnings/).

## Key Findings

Australian TikTok earnings data from more than 400 creators reveals the key benchmarks that define what Australian creators can realistically earn from the platform.

The Creator Rewards Program pays Australian creators A$0.02 to A$0.04 per 1,000 views for original videos longer than one minute. In USD-equivalent terms, this places Australian RPM roughly 25% to 40% below US rates, but competitive with UK and Canadian rates when adjusted for purchasing power. Payouts are made in AUD.

Brand deals are the dominant income source for Australian creators who pursue sponsorships. Nano-influencers (10K to 50K followers) earn A$250 to A$900 per sponsored video, while mid-tier creators (250K to 1M followers) command A$2,000 to A$8,000 per post. Australia's influencer marketing industry was valued at approximately A$700 million in 2025.

ATO tax requirements include several obligations specific to Australian creators. The tax-free threshold of A$18,200 is the highest among English-speaking TikTok markets, shielding part-time creators from income tax. However, creators earning more than A$75,000 per year must register for GST, and all creators need an Australian Business Number (ABN) to operate as a sole trader.

TikTok Shop is available in Australia and growing. Australian creators earn 10% to 20% commissions on affiliate sales, though the product catalog is not as extensive as in the US or UK. Live gifting is also active, with Australian audiences showing strong engagement during evening and weekend streams.

### RPM AUD

RPM in Australian dollars is the core metric for understanding TikTok's direct payments to Australian creators, and several Australia-specific factors influence where a creator falls within the rate range.

Australian TikTok RPM ranges from A$0.02 to A$0.04 per 1,000 views for Creator Rewards-eligible content (videos over one minute, original content). Shorter videos under 60 seconds earn roughly A$0.008 to A$0.015 per 1K views.

| Search value | Educational content gets RPM boost | Tutorial: A$0.03 – A$0.05; Trend clip: A$0.015 – A$0.02 |
| Seasonal timing | Q4 RPM 35% to 60% higher than Q1 | November: A$0.03 – A$0.05; January: A$0.015 – A$0.025 |
| Time zone advantage | Peak posting during AU evening hits US morning | Cross-timezone content: potential RPM uplift |

Australian creators have a time zone advantage that is often overlooked. Posting during Australian evening hours (6 PM to 10 PM AEST) coincides with US morning hours, allowing content to catch both audiences in a single posting window. Creators who exploit this timing can build a meaningful US viewer base, which boosts effective RPM due to higher US CPMs.

Finance, property, and technology creators in Australia command the highest RPM, mirroring patterns seen in the [US](/data/earnings/tiktok-pay-us-earnings/) and [UK](/data/earnings/tiktok-pay-uk-earnings/) markets. For detailed niche-level data, see our [RPM rates by niche](/data/rpm-rates-by-niche/) analysis.

Seasonal RPM variation in Australia follows the global Q4 peak pattern, but with an Australian twist. While the October-to-December holiday season drives the same advertiser spending surge seen worldwide, Australia's summer holiday period (December through February) means consumer engagement patterns differ slightly from Northern Hemisphere markets. January RPM still drops as global advertiser budgets reset, but Australian consumer spending remains more active in January than in the US or UK. ## TikTok Pay in Australia — Extended Data

Beyond RPM and brand deal rates, Australian-specific tax rules, ABN requirements, and market characteristics shape the earnings reality for Australian creators.

**ATO tax obligations** include income tax, the Medicare levy, and potentially GST. Australia's tax-free threshold of A$18,200 is the most generous among major English-speaking TikTok markets, which is a significant advantage for part-time creators.

| Annual TikTok Earnings | Income Tax Rate | Medicare Levy | GST Requirement | Approximate Take-Home % |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| A$0 – A$18,200 | 0% (tax-free threshold) | 0% (below threshold) | No | 100% |
| A$18,201 – A$45,000 | 16% | 2% | No | 82% |
| A$45,001 – A$75,000 | 30% | 2% | Register at A$75K | 68% |
| A$75,001 – A$135,000 | 30% | 2% | Yes (10% GST on services) | 60% – 68% |
| A$135,001 – A$190,000 | 37% | 2% | Yes | 55% – 61% |
| A$190,001+ | 45% | 2% | Yes | 48% – 53% |

**ABN requirement.** All Australian creators operating as sole traders must obtain an Australian Business Number (ABN). This is free to apply for through the Australian Business Register and is required before you can invoice brands for sponsored content. Without an ABN, businesses paying you must withhold 47% of the payment amount.

**GST registration** becomes mandatory once your annual gross revenue exceeds A$75,000. GST is charged at 10% on services provided to Australian clients (brand deals with Australian brands). Services exported to overseas clients (brand deals with US or UK brands) are generally GST-free. Most Creator Rewards Program payments are not subject to GST. Creators approaching the A$75,000 threshold should consult a tax professional to understand their specific obligations.

## Methodology

The Australian earnings data in this article is compiled from multiple sources focused on the Australian creator market.

**Primary data** comes from self-reported earnings from 400+ Australian TikTok creators who participated in our Q4 2025 and Q1 2026 income surveys. Participants provided analytics screenshots to verify follower counts and view data. Earnings were reported in AUD by revenue stream.

**Secondary data** includes rate cards from three Australian influencer marketing agencies, TikTok's Creator Rewards Program documentation, ATO self-employment tax guidance, and aggregate campaign data from two influencer platforms operating in Australia. Exchange rate conversions use the average AUD/USD rate for the reporting period.

**Limitations.** The Australian sample (400+) is the smallest among our four English-speaking market analyses (compared to US: 1,800+, UK: 900+, Canada: 500+), which increases margin of error. Self-reported data carries survivorship and recall biases. Tax estimates are general guidance and not tax advice -- consult an Australian accountant or the ATO directly. All dollar figures are in AUD unless stated otherwise. We update this data quarterly.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does TikTok pay per 1,000 views in Australia?

TikTok pays Australian creators A$0.02 to A$0.04 per 1,000 views through the Creator Rewards Program for original videos longer than one minute. Shorter videos earn roughly A$0.008 to A$0.015 per 1K views. Rates vary by niche, audience geography, engagement rate, and seasonal demand. Q4 delivers the highest RPM, while January sees the lowest rates. Use the [pay per 1000 views tool](/calculators/tiktok-money/tiktok-pay-per-1000-views/) to estimate your specific rate.

### Do Australian TikTok creators need an ABN?

Yes. All Australian creators earning income from TikTok should obtain an Australian Business Number (ABN) to operate as a sole trader. An ABN is free to apply for and is required to invoice brands for sponsored content. Without an ABN, businesses must withhold 47% of payments. GST registration becomes mandatory once annual gross revenue exceeds A$75,000.

### How do Australian TikTok earnings compare to the US and UK?

Australian Creator Rewards RPM (A$0.02 to A$0.04) is roughly 25% to 40% below [US rates](/data/earnings/tiktok-pay-us-earnings/) in USD-equivalent terms and comparable to [UK rates](/data/earnings/tiktok-pay-uk-earnings/) in local currency terms. Australian brand deal rates are strong for the Asia-Pacific region. Australia's A$18,200 tax-free threshold is the highest among major English-speaking markets, meaning part-time Australian creators keep more of their earnings before tax kicks in.

### Is TikTok Shop available in Australia?

Yes. Australian creators have access to TikTok Shop with affiliate product linking and earn 10% to 20% commissions per sale. The product catalog is smaller than in the [US](/data/earnings/tiktok-pay-us-earnings/) or [UK](/data/earnings/tiktok-pay-uk-earnings/) but is expanding. Beauty, fashion, and home goods categories currently generate the strongest affiliate earnings for Australian creators.
