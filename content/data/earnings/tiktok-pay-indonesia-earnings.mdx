TikTok pay in Indonesia ranges from Rp5 to Rp20 per 1,000 views through the Creator Rewards Program, approximately $0.003 to $0.013 USD at current exchange rates. Indonesia is TikTok's fourth-largest market globally by user count, with more than 125 million active users, making it one of the most important creator markets in Southeast Asia. Brand deals pay Rp500,000 to Rp30,000,000 per video, and TikTok Shop -- reinstated in 2024 as "TikTok Shop powered by Tokopedia" after a temporary ban -- has become the dominant revenue stream for Indonesian creators.

## Key Findings

Our analysis of Indonesian TikTok creator earnings reveals a market defined by massive scale, low per-view payouts, and an exceptionally strong social commerce ecosystem.

The Creator Rewards Program pays Indonesian creators between Rp5 and Rp20 per 1,000 qualifying views. A video reaching 1 million views generates approximately Rp5,000 to Rp20,000 (roughly $0.30 to $1.30 USD) from the program alone. These rates are among the lowest globally, reflecting Indonesia's lower advertising CPM rates compared to Western markets. However, the sheer volume of Indonesian TikTok users means that viral reach is easier to achieve.

[TikTok Shop](/calculators/commerce-ads/shop-profit/tiktok-shop-commission-rates/) is the defining feature of Indonesia's creator economy. After the Indonesian government temporarily banned TikTok Shop in October 2023 over concerns about its impact on small businesses, the platform relaunched in January 2024 through a partnership with Tokopedia, Indonesia's largest e-commerce platform. The "TikTok Shop powered by Tokopedia" model has grown rapidly, and Indonesian creators now earn more from Shop commissions than from all other monetization programs combined.

Brand deals provide the second-largest income stream. Nano-influencers (10K to 50K followers) earn Rp500,000 to Rp3,000,000 per sponsored video, while mid-tier creators (250K to 1M followers) command Rp8,000,000 to Rp30,000,000. Indonesian brands in the FMCG, beauty, and e-commerce sectors are the most active TikTok advertisers.

Indonesia's young demographics -- median age 30, with more than 200 million internet users -- create an enormous addressable audience for creators. The combination of high platform engagement, growing advertiser spending, and TikTok Shop's resurgence makes Indonesia one of the most opportunity-rich TikTok markets despite its low RPM rates.

## TikTok Pay in Indonesia -- Extended Data

Several factors beyond platform payouts and TikTok Shop commissions shape the total earnings picture for Indonesian creators.

**Tax obligations** in Indonesia are managed by the Direktorat Jenderal Pajak (DJP). Indonesian creators with annual gross income below Rp500 million can use the PP 55 simplified tax regime, which charges a flat 0.5% on gross revenue. This applies for the first seven years of business activity, after which creators must transition to standard income tax rates (5% to 35% on taxable income). All creator income -- including brand deals, TikTok Shop commissions, and Creator Rewards payouts -- is taxable. Many small creators fall below the reporting threshold, but the DJP has increased enforcement on digital income in recent years. ## Methodology

The Indonesia-specific earnings data in this article is compiled from a survey of 420 Indonesian TikTok creators conducted in Q4 2025 and Q1 2026. Participants provided analytics screenshots and verified their TikTok Shop and Creator Rewards earnings. We supplemented this with brand deal rate data from four Indonesian influencer marketing agencies and TikTok Shop GMV estimates from GoTo Group quarterly reports.

All rupiah amounts use the average exchange rate of Rp15,600 to $1 USD for the reporting period. RPM calculations exclude the top and bottom 2% of earners. TikTok Shop commission data reflects the "TikTok Shop powered by Tokopedia" structure, including the Tokopedia marketplace fee layer, which results in slightly lower effective commission rates than pre-ban TikTok Shop.

Self-reported earnings carry inherent biases. TikTok Shop revenue is particularly variable depending on product category, seasonal demand (Ramadan, Harbolnas, and year-end shopping events drive significant spikes), and content format. These figures are informed estimates updated quarterly as new survey data and platform changes emerge.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does TikTok pay per 1,000 views in Indonesia?

TikTok pays Indonesian creators approximately Rp5 to Rp20 per 1,000 views through the Creator Rewards Program, equivalent to roughly $0.003 to $0.013 USD. Finance and technology content earns at the higher end, while comedy and general entertainment earn at the lower end. These rates are among the lowest globally, making TikTok Shop and brand deals far more important than Creator Rewards for Indonesian creator income. See our [average earnings per views](/data/earnings/average-tiktok-earnings-per-views/) data for global context.

### How does TikTok Shop work in Indonesia after the ban?

TikTok Shop was temporarily banned in Indonesia in October 2023 and relaunched in January 2024 as "TikTok Shop powered by Tokopedia." The current model routes all transactions through Tokopedia's e-commerce infrastructure while maintaining the in-app shopping experience. Creators earn affiliate commissions of 5% to 15% on product sales. The platform has grown rapidly since relaunch, with more than 2 million affiliate creators. Commission rates are slightly lower than pre-ban levels due to the additional Tokopedia fee layer. For market trends, check our [TikTok Shop GMV growth](/data/earnings/tiktok-shop-gmv-growth/) data.

### How much do Indonesian TikTok creators earn from brand deals?

Indonesian brand deal earnings range from Rp500,000 to Rp30,000,000 per video (approximately $30 to $1,900 USD) depending on follower count, niche, and engagement rate. Nano-influencers earn Rp500,000 to Rp3,000,000 per post, while mid-tier creators command Rp8,000,000 to Rp30,000,000. FMCG, beauty, and e-commerce brands are the most active sponsors. Use the [brand deal rate calculator](/calculators/brand-deal-rate/) to benchmark your pricing.

### Do Indonesian TikTok creators need to pay taxes?

Yes. The DJP requires all income to be reported, including TikTok creator earnings. Creators with annual gross income below Rp500 million can use the PP 55 simplified tax regime at 0.5% of gross revenue for their first seven years of business activity. After that, standard income tax rates of 5% to 35% apply. The DJP has increased enforcement on digital income, and creators should maintain records of all revenue streams.
