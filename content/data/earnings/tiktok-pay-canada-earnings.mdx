TikTok pays Canadian creators approximately C$0.02 to C$0.04 per 1,000 views through the Creator Rewards Program, placing Canada in the upper tier of TikTok's global payout structure alongside the [United Kingdom](/data/earnings/tiktok-pay-uk-earnings) and [Australia](/data/earnings/tiktok-pay-australia-earnings). A Canadian creator generating 1 million monthly views can expect C$20 to C$40 from the Rewards Program alone, with total earnings reaching C$400 to C$3,500 or more when brand deals, live gifts, and TikTok Shop commissions are factored in. Canada's growing influencer marketing industry and proximity to the US advertising market make it an increasingly attractive country for TikTok monetization.

## Key Findings

Canadian TikTok earnings data from more than 500 creators across the country reveals key benchmarks that reflect Canada's unique position in the global TikTok economy.

The Creator Rewards Program pays Canadian creators C$0.02 to C$0.04 per 1,000 views for original videos longer than one minute. In USD-equivalent terms, this places Canadian RPM roughly 30% to 40% below [US rates](/data/earnings/tiktok-pay-us-earnings) and roughly on par with [UK rates](/data/earnings/tiktok-pay-uk-earnings) when adjusted for purchasing power. Payouts are made in CAD.

Brand deals are the primary income driver for Canadian creators who actively pursue sponsorships. Nano-influencers (10K to 50K followers) earn C$200 to C$700 per sponsored video, while mid-tier creators (250K to 1M followers) command C$1,500 to C$7,000 per post. The Canadian influencer marketing industry has grown rapidly, reaching an estimated C$800 million in 2025.

Canadian creators benefit from proximity to the US market. Many Canadian creators attract a mixed US-Canadian audience, which can boost RPM because US-based viewers carry higher CPM value. Bilingual creators in Quebec also access the French-Canadian market, which has its own advertiser demand.

CRA tax obligations require self-employed creators to pay both income tax and Canada Pension Plan (CPP) contributions. The basic personal amount of C$15,705 shelters lower-earning creators from income tax, but CPP contributions of 11.9% (combined employee and employer portions for self-employed) apply on net self-employment earnings between C$3,500 and C$73,200.

### RPM CAD

RPM in Canadian dollars is the key metric for understanding TikTok's direct payments to Canadian creators, and it varies based on several factors specific to the Canadian market.

Canadian TikTok RPM ranges from C$0.02 to C$0.04 per 1,000 views for Rewards-eligible content. Shorter videos under 60 seconds earn roughly C$0.008 to C$0.015 per 1K views, making them unviable as a primary income source.

| RPM Factor | Impact on Canadian Earnings | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Content niche | High-CPM niches earn 1.5x to 2.5x base | Finance: C$0.035 – C$0.055; Comedy: C$0.015 – C$0.025 |
| Audience geography | US viewers in audience boost RPM | 70% Canadian + 20% US: C$0.03 – C$0.04; 90% Canadian: C$0.02 – C$0.03 |
| Video length | Longer videos earn higher RPM | 1-3 min: baseline; 3-5 min: 1.2x – 1.4x |
| Language | English content reaches broader audience | English: baseline; French-only: 0.8x – 0.9x (smaller ad market) |
| Seasonal timing | Q4 RPM 40% to 65% higher than Q1 | November: C$0.03 – C$0.05; January: C$0.015 – C$0.025 |

A unique advantage for Canadian creators is audience spillover from the United States. Because Canadian and American culture overlap significantly, many Canadian creators attract 15% to 30% US viewers organically. Since US-based viewers carry higher CPM value, this mixed audience can push effective RPM above the pure-Canadian rate. Creators who intentionally produce content appealing to both markets can earn RPM closer to C$0.035 to C$0.045.

Bilingual creators in Quebec face a trade-off. French-language content accesses a dedicated but smaller advertising market, which can mean slightly lower RPM. However, bilingual creators who post in both English and French often build larger total audiences, offsetting any per-view rate difference. For niche-level RPM data, see our [RPM rates by niche](/data/rpm-rates-by-niche) analysis.

## TikTok Pay in Canada — Extended Data

Beyond RPM and brand deal rates, Canadian creators face specific tax obligations, market dynamics, and cross-border considerations that affect real earnings.

**CRA tax obligations** require all self-employed creators earning more than C$3,500 in net self-employment income to make CPP contributions. Income tax applies after the basic personal amount of C$15,705 (federal) is exceeded.

Provincial tax rates vary significantly. Alberta has the lowest combined provincial rate, while Quebec has the highest. Creators in Quebec also pay into the Quebec Pension Plan (QPP) instead of CPP, at slightly different rates. GST/HST registration is required once gross revenue exceeds C$30,000 in a 12-month period, though most digital creator services are zero-rated for GST purposes. ## Methodology

The Canadian earnings data in this article is drawn from multiple sources focused on the Canadian creator market.

**Primary data** comes from self-reported earnings from 500+ Canadian TikTok creators who participated in our Q4 2025 and Q1 2026 income surveys. Participants provided analytics screenshots to verify follower counts and view data. Earnings were reported in CAD by revenue stream.

**Secondary data** includes rate cards from four Canadian influencer marketing agencies, TikTok's Creator Rewards Program documentation, CRA self-employment tax guidance, and aggregate campaign data from two influencer platforms operating in Canada. Exchange rate conversions use the average CAD/USD rate for the reporting period where cross-country comparisons are made.

**Limitations.** The Canadian sample (500+) is smaller than our US (1,800+) or UK (900+) samples, increasing margin of error. Bilingual French-English creators may be underrepresented. Self-reported data carries inherent biases. Tax estimates are general guidance -- consult a Canadian accountant or the CRA directly for personalized advice. We update this data quarterly.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does TikTok pay per 1,000 views in Canada?

TikTok pays Canadian creators C$0.02 to C$0.04 per 1,000 views through the Creator Rewards Program for original videos longer than one minute. Shorter videos earn roughly C$0.008 to C$0.015 per 1K views. Rates vary by niche, audience geography (US viewers boost RPM), engagement rate, and seasonal demand. Use the [pay per 1000 views tool](/calculators/tiktok-money/tiktok-pay-per-1000-views) to estimate your specific rate.

### Do Canadian TikTok creators pay taxes on earnings?

Yes. All Canadian TikTok earnings are taxable income. Creators must report self-employment income to the CRA and pay federal and provincial income tax after the basic personal amount of C$15,705. CPP contributions of 11.9% apply on net self-employment earnings between C$3,500 and C$73,200. GST/HST registration is required once gross revenue exceeds C$30,000 in 12 months. See the [creator taxes guide](/guides/business/tiktok-creator-taxes-guide) for Canadian-specific advice.

### How do Canadian TikTok earnings compare to the US?

Canadian Creator Rewards RPM (C$0.02 to C$0.04) is roughly 30% to 40% lower than US rates ($0.03 to $0.05) in USD-equivalent terms. Canadian brand deal rates are also 25% to 40% lower than US equivalents. However, Canada's higher basic personal amount (C$15,705 vs effectively $0 for US SE tax) means lower-earning Canadian creators keep a larger after-tax share. See our [US earnings breakdown](/data/earnings/tiktok-pay-us-earnings) for detailed comparison.

### Is the Creator Rewards Program available in Canada?

Yes. Canadian creators have full access to the Creator Rewards Program with the same eligibility requirements as the US: 10,000 followers and 100,000 views in the last 30 days. Payouts are in CAD, and all core features including the analytics dashboard and search value bonus are available. Some new features may launch in Canada 2 to 6 weeks after their US debut.
