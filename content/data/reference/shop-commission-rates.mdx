"TikTok Shop commission" means two different things depending on who is paying: the **platform referral fee** TikTok charges sellers — reported at roughly **6% on most US categories in 2026** (5% on jewelry) — and the **creator affiliate commission**, typically **2–20% by category**, that a seller sets to reward creators for driving sales. Confusing the two is the most common budgeting mistake on the platform, because the headline 6% is only a fraction of the true cost once creator commissions, fulfillment, and ads stack on top.

## The Two Kinds of TikTok Shop Commission

Before you can price a product or a partnership, you need to separate the fee TikTok keeps from the commission you pay creators.

### Seller referral fee

The referral fee is TikTok's cut of each sale, deducted automatically before you're paid. For US sellers in 2026, independent analyses put the standard rate at roughly **6% flat across most categories**, with jewelry as the main exception at **5%** ([Dashboardly](https://www.dashboardly.io/post/tiktok-shop-affiliate-commissions-2026-payouts-clawbacks-profit-math), [Hamster Garage](https://www.hamstergarage.com/article/tiktok-shop-affiliate-statistics-benchmarks-roi)). That flat structure replaced the earlier tiered category pricing that many older guides still cite — and it sits well above the stale 2% launch-promo figure that circulated in 2023–2024, so ignore any source still quoting it. The referral fee is charged on the order value, and TikTok settles the remainder to you on its [payment schedule](/data/reference/shop-payment-schedule/).

### Creator affiliate commission

This is the rate **you set** as a seller to reward creators who promote your products through the affiliate program, and it typically runs **2–20% depending on category**. In practice TikTok splits affiliate partnerships into two tracks: **Open Collaboration**, where any eligible creator can list your product at a fixed public rate (commonly **10–15%**), and **Targeted Collaboration**, where you negotiate one-to-one with a specific creator — deals that third-party guides report ranging from **18% up to 50%** for top-tier partnerships ([ShortFormNation](https://www.shortformnation.com/blog/tiktok-shop-affiliate-marketing-the-complete-2026-guide)). Beauty, skincare, and wellness products sit at the top of the public range because that is what it takes to attract creators with real audiences. US creators generally need about **5,000 followers** to unlock Open Collaboration access. From the creator's side, this is the number that determines earnings — the [affiliate commission calculator](/calculators/affiliate-commission/) turns a commission rate and expected sales into a projected payout.

### New-seller promotional rate

TikTok periodically runs reduced-referral-fee promotions for brand-new sellers as a head start for testing products. Terms and eligibility windows change often, so confirm the current new-seller incentive in Seller Center rather than budgeting around a fixed promo rate — and model your steady-state unit economics on the standard ~6% referral fee so margins don't collapse once any introductory rate ends.

## The Real All-In Cost of Selling on TikTok Shop

The 6% headline understates what a sale actually costs a seller. Stack the layers on a typical US order and the platform-plus-channel take can reach **30%+ of the selling price**:

- **Referral fee:** ~6% of order value
- **Creator affiliate commission:** ~10–20% when you use affiliates
- **Fulfillment / shipping:** sellers on TikTok's logistics can see an optional Fulfilled-by-TikTok shipping fee of roughly **+6%** stack on top ([Dashboardly](https://www.dashboardly.io/post/tiktok-shop-affiliate-commissions-2026-payouts-clawbacks-profit-math))
- **Ads:** variable, often 10%+ of revenue for paid campaigns
- **Returns and refunds:** commissions and fees are reversed on returned orders within the settlement window; a return that posts after payout is clawed back from a future balance

Industry commentary notes that roughly a third of gross sales never reaches the seller once fees, commissions, and returns are netted out — so treat gross merchandise value as a top-line figure, not take-home revenue.

For a creator, this stack matters in the opposite direction: your affiliate commission is paid on the sale value and is **not** reduced by the seller's referral fee or fulfillment costs. What you're quoted as your commission rate is what you earn, subject to returns.

## How to Set and Optimize Your Rates

**If you're a seller:**

- Price your margin around the full ~30% all-in take, not the 6% referral fee alone.
- Offer category-competitive affiliate commissions — under-paying creators is the fastest way to get ignored in beauty and wellness.
- Use Open Collaboration to seed reach, then negotiate Targeted deals with the creators who actually convert.

**If you're a creator:**

- Prioritize higher-commission categories (beauty, wellness) when audience fit allows.
- Factor returns into projections; commission on returned orders is clawed back during the settlement window.
- Combine Shop affiliate income with other streams — the [affiliate-without-a-shop guide](/guides/monetization/tiktok-affiliate-without-shop/) explains how to earn commissions even before you run your own storefront.

## FAQ

### What is the TikTok Shop commission rate in 2026?

For creators, affiliate commissions typically run **2–20% depending on category**, with one 2026 benchmark putting the US average near **13%** ([Hamster Garage](https://www.hamstergarage.com/article/tiktok-shop-affiliate-statistics-benchmarks-roi)). For sellers, TikTok's platform *referral* fee is a separate charge of roughly **6%** on most US categories (5% on jewelry).

### What fees does TikTok Shop charge sellers per sale?

The core charge is the ~**6%** referral fee, deducted from order value before payout. Sellers who use TikTok's logistics can also see an optional Fulfilled-by-TikTok shipping fee of about **+6%**, and affiliate commissions — whatever rate you set — come out on top of that ([Dashboardly](https://www.dashboardly.io/post/tiktok-shop-affiliate-commissions-2026-payouts-clawbacks-profit-math)).

### What is the difference between Open and Targeted Collaboration?

**Open Collaboration** lets any eligible creator — generally those with about **5,000+ followers** — promote your product at a fixed public rate, commonly **10–15%**. **Targeted Collaboration** is a negotiated one-to-one deal that can range from **18% to 50%** for sought-after creators ([ShortFormNation](https://www.shortformnation.com/blog/tiktok-shop-affiliate-marketing-the-complete-2026-guide)).

### Do TikTok Shop affiliates lose commission when a customer returns a product?

Yes. Commissions are tied to completed orders, so a return inside the settlement window cancels the pending commission, and a return that posts after you've been paid is clawed back from a future balance ([Dashboardly](https://www.dashboardly.io/post/tiktok-shop-affiliate-commissions-2026-payouts-clawbacks-profit-math)). Always model earnings net of your category's typical return rate.
