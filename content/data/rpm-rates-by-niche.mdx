RPM (Revenue Per Mille) is what a creator earns per 1,000 views, and on TikTok it depends heavily on your niche. One number matters before any table, though: which program you are in. The legacy Creator Fund paid roughly $0.02-$0.05 per 1,000 views, while the current Creator Rewards Program pays about $0.50-$2.00 per 1,000 qualified views — 10-40x more. This page benchmarks both so you can read the rates that actually apply to you. Every figure is an estimate based on published ranges and TikTok's official documentation, not a guaranteed payout.

## Creator Rewards RPM by Niche (2026)

The Creator Rewards Program is the mainstream monetization path in 2026, and it pays on *qualified views* — unique For You feed views watched longer than five seconds, on videos of at least 60 seconds that clear a 1,000-qualified-view threshold. Rates below are for US audiences; other regions scale down (see the regional section).

| Niche | Creator Rewards RPM (per 1K qualified views) | Advertiser demand |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Finance / Business | $1.50 – $3.00 | Highest |
| Technology | $1.00 – $2.50 | Very high |
| Education | $0.80 – $2.00 | High |
| Beauty / Fashion | $0.60 – $1.50 | Above average |
| Fitness | $0.60 – $1.50 | Above average |
| Food | $0.50 – $1.20 | Average |
| Lifestyle | $0.50 – $1.20 | Average |
| Entertainment | $0.40 – $1.00 | Below average |
| Gaming | $0.40 – $1.00 | Below average |
| Comedy | $0.30 – $0.80 | Lower |

Finance and tech sit at the top because financial services, software, and B2B advertisers pay a premium to reach high-intent, high-value audiences. Broad entertainment attracts large view counts but weaker advertiser demand per view, which keeps RPM lower. TikTok's own [Creator Rewards Program documentation](https://support.tiktok.com/en/business-and-creator/creator-rewards-program) confirms payouts combine a Standard Reward (qualified views x RPM) with an Additional Reward for content craft and engagement, so two creators in the same niche can still land at different points in these ranges.

## Legacy Creator Fund RPM: Real-World Examples

The legacy Creator Fund still operates in some regions (including the US, UK, Germany, France, Spain, and Italy), and its rates are far lower. These examples show how the same 5 million monthly views translate into very different Creator Fund earnings by niche:

| Creator (5M views/month) | Legacy Creator Fund RPM | Monthly Creator Fund earnings |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Finance | $0.05 (high) | $250 |
| Beauty | $0.03 (medium) | $150 |
| Comedy | $0.02 (low) | $100 |

**Key insight:** the finance creator earns 2.5x more than the comedy creator with the exact same views — which is why niche selection matters for Creator Fund income. But note the bigger picture: the current Creator Rewards Program pays 10-40x more per view than these legacy Fund rates, so if you qualify, moving onto Creator Rewards changes your earnings far more than switching niches within the old Fund. Independent 2026 breakdowns such as [BitBrowser's TikTok pay analysis](https://www.bitbrowser.net/blog/how-much-does-tiktok-pay-2026) confirm both the legacy Fund's continued regional presence and the Creator Rewards ranges.

## How Geography Affects RPM: Regional Multipliers

Viewer location dramatically impacts RPM. A view from the United States can pay 5x or more than a view from the lowest-income markets, because advertisers pay premium rates to reach high-GDP audiences with more purchasing power. The multipliers below apply to both programs; the dollar column shows legacy Creator Fund ranges for reference.

| Region | RPM Multiplier | Legacy Creator Fund RPM | Why |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| United States | 1.0x (baseline) | $0.02 – $0.05 | Highest advertiser budgets, premium CPMs |
| United Kingdom | 0.9x | $0.018 – $0.045 | Strong economy, slightly lower ad spend |
| Canada | 0.85x | $0.017 – $0.043 | Strong economy, smaller ad market |
| Australia | 0.8x | $0.016 – $0.040 | Solid demand, smaller advertiser base |
| Germany / France / Spain (EU) | 0.75x | $0.015 – $0.038 | Western Europe, moderate advertiser demand |
| Asia (broad) | 0.6x | $0.012 – $0.030 | Large audiences, lower per-capita ad rates |
| LATAM | 0.5x | $0.010 – $0.025 | Growing markets, lower advertiser budgets |
| Africa | 0.3x | $0.006 – $0.015 | Lowest advertiser demand per view |

**Optimization strategy:** to maximize RPM, optimize posting times for Western audiences. Posting at 6-10 PM EST (when US, Canada, and UK viewers are active) rather than 3 AM EST can meaningfully lift your effective RPM, even if you are based in Asia, because a larger share of your qualified views comes from higher-paying markets.

## RPM Trends: How Rates Have Changed (2022-2026)

TikTok RPM has been reshaped less by year-to-year drift than by the program change from Creator Fund to Creator Rewards:

- **2022 (Creator Fund peak):** $0.04-$0.06 average. The Fund was new and fewer creators were enrolled; finance niches reached $0.08-$0.10.
- **2023-2024 (Creator Fund decline):** $0.02-$0.04. Mass enrollment diluted the fixed pool, and average RPM dropped 30-50% across most niches.
- **2024-2025 (Creator Rewards rollout):** TikTok replaced the Fund in most markets with the Creator Rewards Program, lifting eligible creators to $0.50-$2.00 per 1,000 qualified views.
- **2026 (Creator Rewards mainstream):** $0.50-$2.00 is the baseline for eligible creators, while the legacy Fund remains active only in select regions. High-value niches (finance, business, tech) sit at the top of the range.

**Seasonal pattern:** RPM follows advertiser budgets, with a Q4 holiday peak and a Q1 trough. For US audiences, Q4 can run roughly 2-4x the Q1 low, so the same viral video is worth more in November than in March. ## Should You Switch to a Higher-RPM Niche?

The math is not always simple. Switching niches makes sense when you are genuinely interested in the higher-RPM topic, you have (or can build) real expertise, your current niche RPM is very low, and you are early enough in your journey to rebuild an audience. It rarely makes sense when you would only be chasing money, you already have an established audience in your current niche, or your low-RPM content earns well on sheer volume — 1,000,000 views at $0.40 still beats 100,000 views at $2.00.

A hybrid approach usually wins: keep your existing content to hold views, then add 30-40% higher-RPM material. Comedy plus finance ("roasting bad money advice"), fashion plus business ("how influencers actually get paid"), or gaming plus tech ("best budget gaming laptops") all raise your blended RPM without abandoning the audience you have.

## FAQ

### What is a good RPM on TikTok in 2026?

Under the Creator Rewards Program, most eligible US creators land between $0.50 and $2.00 per 1,000 qualified views, with finance and tech at the top. If you are still on the legacy Creator Fund, "good" is closer to $0.04-$0.05. Compare yourself to your own niche and program, not a single universal number.

### Which niches have the highest TikTok RPM?

Finance and business lead ($1.50-$3.00), followed by technology ($1.00-$2.50) and education ($0.80-$2.00). Comedy and general entertainment sit lowest ($0.30-$1.00) because they attract weaker advertiser demand per view.

### Why is my RPM so much lower than these numbers?

The most common reasons are being on the legacy Creator Fund instead of Creator Rewards, an audience concentrated in lower-paying regions, videos under 60 seconds (which do not earn Creator Rewards at all), or the Q1 seasonal low. Fixing eligibility and audience geography usually moves the needle most.

### Does TikTok publish official RPM rates?

No. TikTok never publishes RPM figures. Every rate here is an estimate built from published ranges, TikTok's official documentation, and creator-reported data — use it for planning, not as a promise.
